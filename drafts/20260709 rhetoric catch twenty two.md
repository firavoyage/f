catch22 meaning

A **"Catch-22"** is <mark>a<span> </span><strong>frustrating logical paradox</strong><span> </span>or a<span> </span><strong>"no-win" dilemma</strong><span> </span>where you are trapped by contradictory rules or conditions</mark>. Essentially, it describes an impossible situation where the solution to a problem is denied by a circumstance inherent in the problem itself. You cannot achieve "Goal A" because you need "Condition B" first, but you can only get "Condition B" if you have already achieved "Goal A". [link](https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/catch-22)

### Common Real-World Examples

- **The Job Market Loop:** You need work experience to get a job, but you can only get work experience by getting a job first. [link](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/Xp7wvIm1AUE)
- **Opening a Bank Account:** You need a valid home address to open a bank account, but you cannot secure an apartment lease without a bank account to prove your funds.
- **Credit Score Paradox:** You need a credit card or loan to build up a good credit history, but banks will not approve you for a credit card or loan because you do not have a credit history.

### Origin of the Term

The phrase was coined by American author Joseph Heller in his famous 1961 satirical war novel, _Catch-22_. [link](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catch-22%5F%28logic%29)

In the novel, World War II pilots are trapped by a fictional military regulation: [link](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/catch-22)

1. A pilot can be excused from flying dangerous, near-suicidal combat missions only if they are declared insane.
2. However, any pilot who formally requests to be grounded out of fear for their safety demonstrates a rational mind concerned with self-preservation.
3. Therefore, the very act of asking to be grounded proves they are sane, meaning they must continue to fly. [link](https://www.reddit.com/r/explainlikeimfive/comments/uwdow1/eli5%5Fwhat%5Fis%5Fa%5Fcatch%5F22%5Fand%5Fwhat%5Fare%5Fsome%5Freal/)

### Similar Expressions

- **A lose-lose situation:** A scenario where every choice leads to a bad outcome.
- **Damned if you do, damned if you don't:** A dilemma where you face criticism or trouble regardless of the path you choose.
- **A vicious circle:** A chain of events in which the response to one difficulty creates a new problem that makes the original problem worse. [link](https://www.dictionary.com/browse/catch-22)
