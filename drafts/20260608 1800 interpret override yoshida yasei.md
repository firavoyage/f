## オーバーライド song meaning

The viral Vocaloid song **"Override" (オーバーライド)** by [Yoshida Yasei](https://www.billboard.com/music/music-news/yoshida-yasei-interview-override-1235865852/) featuring Kasane Teto is <mark style="margin: 0px; text-decoration-style: solid; text-decoration-color: rgb(230, 232, 240); border-bottom: 0px none rgb(230, 232, 240);">a deeply personal track about <strong>defying fate, overcoming life's frustrations, and pushing past human limitations</strong></mark>. \[[1](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z-rPmxZexPI), [2](https://www.billboard.com/music/music-news/yoshida-yasei-interview-override-1235865852/)]

While the upbeat music and colorful music video inspired countless internet memes, the core meaning of the song stems from the creator's real-life transition from a software engineer to a full-time musician. \[[1](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z-rPmxZexPI)]

### Core Themes and Meanings

- **Programming Metaphor**: In IT, "override" means rewriting pre-existing rules, settings, or functions to make them do something entirely different. The composer used this coding background to express a desire to "overwrite" his predetermined lifestyle and rewrite his own destiny.
- **Escaping a Dead-End Life**: The song captures the raw frustration, desperation, and angst of feeling trapped in a mundane routine. It paints life like a rigid computer system that needs to be broken out of.
- **Pushing Beyond Limits**: It carries a double meaning of pushing oneself to the absolute limit to force a change in circumstances, regardless of the emotional or physical toll.
- **Biting Lyrics vs. Playful Tone**: The creator purposely paired dark, cynical lyrics about life with chaotic, meme-heavy visuals and upbeat melodies. This creates a coping mechanism where serious existential dread is masked as a giant, energetic joke. \[[1](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z-rPmxZexPI), [2](https://www.billboard.com/music/music-news/yoshida-yasei-interview-override-1235865852/), [3](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=shyRW65dvn0)]

### Verse

- **バッドランドに生まれた (Born in a bad land)**: The speaker starts life with a heavy disadvantage, born into a rough environment or poor circumstances.
- **だけでバッドライフがデフォとか (Just because of that, a bad life is the default)**: They reject the idea that their birthplace dictates a miserable destiny by default.
- **くだらないけど、それが理なんだって (It's stupid, but they say that's just the way the world works)**: They find this societal rule absurd, even though society accepts it as an unchangeable truth.
- **もう参っちゃうね (Man, it really gets to me)**: An expression of deep exhaustion and frustration with this rigged system.
- **抗うために (In order to fight back)**: They refuse to give up and decide to rebel against their fate.
- **エスケープ・フロム・デンエン (Escape from Den-en / the countryside)**: A literal and metaphorical escape from their restrictive, rural, or mundane hometown to chase something bigger.
- **蛇のように這い、善戦 (Crawling like a snake, putting up a good fight)**: They swallow their pride, staying low to the ground, working tirelessly, and fighting a hard battle to survive.
- **だけど最後、逆転の一手だけ (But when it comes down to that final, game-changing move)**: They get so close to completely turning their life around.
- **何故か詰められないの！ (For some reason, I just can't pull it off!)**: The ultimate frustration—failing right at the finish line, unable to seal the victory.

### Pre-Chorus

- **暗い無頼社会 VS bright未来世界 (Dark, rogue society VS bright future world)**: Weighing the bleak reality of a soul-crushing corporate job against the glowing promise of a creative, musical future.
- **ならちょっと後者に行ってくる (Well, I'm gonna head over to the latter for a bit)**: A casual, almost reckless decision to abandon stability and dive headfirst into their dreams.
- **大丈夫か？ うるせえよ ("Are you gonna be okay?" "Shut up.")**: A dialogue with anxious onlookers (or their own inner doubts). They aggressively shut out the skepticism to move forward.

### Chorus

- **限界まで足掻いた人生は (A life struggled for to the absolute limit)**: Pushing their mind and body to the brink of collapse just to make something of themselves.
- **想像よりも狂っているらしい (Appears to be more insane than I ever imagined)**: Realizing that the path of an artist or independent creator is chaotic and mentally breaking.
- **半端な生命の関数を (This half-baked function of a life)**: Using programming terms to describe themselves as an incomplete, glitchy human program.
- **少々ここらでオーバーライド (I'll override it a bit right here)**: The desire to rewrite their own code, rewrite their destiny, and force a system change.
- **...したい所だけど現実は (...is what I'd like to do, but reality)**: The harsh reality crash. Wanting to change your life isn't enough to make it happen.
- **そうそう上手くはいかないようで (Doesn't seem to go that smoothly)**: The system fights back; breaking free from societal expectations is incredibly difficult.
- **吐いた言葉だけが信条だって (That the words I spat out are my only core beliefs)**: People take their lyrics or cynical rants completely literally at face value.
- **思われてまた 離れ離れ (Is what they think, and once again, we drift apart)**: Misunderstandings cause people to leave, leaving the creator isolated and disconnected.

### Bridge

- **まぁ、この世の中ガチャの引き次第で (Well, everything in this world can be explained)**: A cynical take that life is just a gacha game based entirely on the luck of your birth.
- **何もかも説明つくわけだし？ (By how well you pull on the gacha, right?)**: Rationalizing that privilege and luck dictate success, not hard work.
- **巻き返しに必要な力で (With the energy you'd need to make a comeback)**: The practical advice society gives you.
- **別の事頑張ればいいじゃん (You might as well just work hard at something else)**: "Give up on your unrealistic dreams and channel that energy into a normal job."
- **まぁ、この地獄の沙汰も金次第で (Well, even the judgments in this hell depend on money)**: A play on a Japanese proverb. Money controls everything, even how well you survive in this modern hell.
- **どこまでも左右出来るわけだし？ (And it can influence things to any extent, right?)**: Wealth buys freedom, stability, and success.
- **アンタが抜け出せるわけがないよ (There is no way someone like you can break out)**: The voice of society (or their own depression) telling them they are trapped.
- **それで話はおしまい？ (Is that where the story ends?)**: The creator snapping back, refusing to let that bleak conclusion be their final note.
- **ならば もう (If that's the case, then...)**: Preparing to deliver a final, defiant rejection.

### Pre-Chorus

- **こないからねー (I'm not coming back, you know!)**: A mocking, playful kiss-off to the old life, refusing to conform to expectations.

### Chorus

- **豪快さにかまけた人生は (A life spent depending on sheer recklessness)**: Living wildly and throwing caution to the wind for the sake of art.
- **きっと燃やされてしまうらしい (Will apparently just end up getting burned down)**: Realizing this chaotic lifestyle leads straight to severe burnout or public criticism.
- **じゃあワタシなど要らないと (Deciding "Then someone like me isn't needed")**: A moment of severe self-doubt, feeling disposable to the world.
- **蹴った果てにいた付和雷同 (And at the end of that kick were blind followers)**: When they lash out or hit rock bottom, they notice the audience is just mindlessly following the trend without truly understanding.
- **シタイだけ探した冒険tongue (An adventurous tongue that only searched for what it wanted to do / for dead bodies)**: A double meaning. "Shitai" means "want to do," but also "corpse." Singing and searching for meaning in a dead, corporate world.
- **どうか消えるまでスタンド・バイ・ミー (Please stand by me until I disappear)**: A desperate, lonely plea for genuine human connection amidst the internet chaos.
- **撒いたエラーすら読んじゃいない (They don't even read the errors I've scattered)**: The audience dances to the upbeat music but ignores the cry-for-help "error messages" hidden in the lyrics.
- **人間の思う事は知らないね！ (I have no idea what humans are thinking!)**: Stepping into the persona of Kasane Teto (the Vocaloid synthesizer), expressing a total disconnect from human society.

### Post-Chorus

- **アンタが書いた杜撰なコード (The sloppy code that you wrote)**: Referring to the song itself. The creator views their own viral track as a messy, flawed piece of work.
- **ばっか持てはやすユーザーよ (Oh, you users who do nothing but praise it)**: Addressing the internet audience who mindlessly hypes up the song as a viral meme template.
- **吐いた言葉の裏なんて (The deeper meaning behind the words I spat out)**: The heavy, dark, personal struggles hidden beneath the bright pop exterior.
- **知る由もないだろう (You wouldn't have any way of knowing, would you)**: A final, biting realization that internet fame is shallow; millions listen, but almost no one truly understands.

### Outro

- **哀れ、あはれ (Pity, how pitiful / How beautiful and fleeting)**: A double meaning using classical Japanese. It expresses pity for the shallow state of the world, while simultaneously acknowledging the tragic, fleeting beauty of trying to survive it.

## visual imagery interpretation

The music video (MV) for **"Override,"** illustrated by [Sisia](https://www.billboard.com/music/music-news/yoshida-yasei-interview-override-1235865852/) and directed by Yoshida Yasei, uses a fast-paced, chaotic collage of **vintage 2000s internet aesthetics, retro gaming references, and coding iconography** to visually mask deep existential dread as an energetic joke. \[[1](https://www.billboard.com/music/music-news/yoshida-yasei-interview-override-1235865852/), [2](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z-rPmxZexPI), [3](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=b-txRRwL2rE)]

Here is a breakdown of the primary visual imagery and how it interprets the song's themes:

### 1. The Retro Gaming and Character Parallel

- **The _Tamagotchi_ Reference:** During the line _"Konai kara ne—"_ (I'm not coming back!), the video visualizes two contrasting characters modeled directly after Memetchi and Mametchi from the 2005 Nintendo DS game [_Tamagotchi no Puchi Puchi Omisetchi_](https://utaten.com/specialArticle/index/8505). In the original game, this bitter phrase is thrown at the player if they give the poorest possible service. \[[1](https://utaten.com/specialArticle/index/8505)]
- **The Meaning:** Visually, this captures a complete, mocking severing of ties. It represents the creator giving a "failing grade" to his former mundane lifestyle and refusing to return to it. It also doubles as a meta-commentary on the Vocaloid community, drawing a visual contrast between the long-established industry titans like Hatsune Miku and the historical "underdog" or newcomer status of Kasane Teto. \[[1](https://utaten.com/specialArticle/index/8505)]

### 2. The Programming and Error Screen Interface

- **The Glitch and Blue Screen Imagery:** Windows-style dialogue boxes, command line text, and "Sloppy Code" system errors constantly populate the screen. Teto is often framed directly inside or flashing alongside these user interfaces. \[[1](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=shyRW65dvn0)]
- **The Meaning:** This visualizes the human mind being processed as a rigid computer program. When the chorus asks to "override" a "half-baked function of a life," the visuals mirror a system crash. The colorful, flashing error alerts emphasize that the creator's hidden cries for help and life "bugs" are being broadcast publicly, yet treated by the viewer merely as dynamic, flashy graphics. \[, [2](https://projectsekai.fandom.com/wiki/Override)]

### 3. The Erratic "Meme-Like" Choreography

- **The Spasmodic Dancing:** Kasane Teto moves with highly repetitive, jagged, and exaggerated motions, switching sharply between static retro-sprites and expressive frame animations. \[[1](https://www.billboard.com/music/music-news/yoshida-yasei-interview-override-1235865852/)]
- **The Meaning:** Yoshida Yasei explicitly requested this style from Sisia to create a "tongue-in-cheek" buffer. The visuals deliberately weaponize internet meme culture. By matching heavy, biting lyricism about alienation and systemic failure with an infectious, absurdly danceable visual loop, the MV creates a coping mechanism. It forces the audience to engage in a joyous dance over what is fundamentally an artistic and personal crisis. \[[1](https://www.billboard.com/music/music-news/yoshida-yasei-interview-override-1235865852/), [2](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z-rPmxZexPI)]

### 4. The Separation of "Creator" and "User"

- **The Post-Chorus Visual Shifts:** During the lines criticizing users who mindlessly praise "sloppy code," the visual framing shifts toward rapid-fire assets, floating internet commentary boxes, and disconnected imagery. \[[1](https://projectsekai.fandom.com/wiki/Override)]
- **The Meaning:** It starkly visualizes a processing disconnect. Teto/The Creator stands isolated on one side of the screen while an avalanche of internet noise, templates, and superficial user consumption takes over the rest. It emphasizes the absolute irony of virality: a song born from deep isolation is consumed so rapidly that its actual core message is completely buried by its own visual hype. \[[1](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z-rPmxZexPI), [2](https://projectsekai.fandom.com/wiki/Override)]

## programming puns

Because Yoshida Yasei was a software engineer before becoming a full-time musician, **"Override" is packed with literal programming terms used as existential puns**. The lyrics treat human life, fate, and social interactions like broken or rigid software code. \[[1](https://utaten.com/specialArticle/index/8505)]

Here are the specific programming puns hidden in the lyrics and how they connect to the song's deeper meaning:

### 1. デフォ (Default / Default Settings)

- **The Lyric:** _「だけでバッドライフがデフォとか」 (Just because of that, a bad life is the default...)_
- **The Coding Meaning:** A **Default** setting is the pre-configured value or behavior a program automatically falls back on if the user doesn't manually change it.
- **The Pun:** The speaker is born in a bad environment ("bad land"). They feel like society has hard-coded their life's program so that poverty, misery, or failure is their "factory setting" or unchangeable default destination.

### 2. エスケープ (Escape)

- **The Lyric:** _「エスケープ・フロム・デンエン」 (Escape from Den-en / countryside)_
- **The Coding Meaning:** An **Escape Character** or **Escape Sequence** (like hitting the `Esc` key, or using a backslash `\` in code) breaks a program out of its current routine, changes how text is interpreted, or terminates a looping process.
- **The Pun:** While it literally means running away from a rural hometown, it also functions as code. The creator is executing an "escape command" to break out of the mundane loop of daily life and prevent their system from crashing.

### 3. 関数 (Function)

- **The Lyric:** _「半端な生命の関数を」 (This half-baked function of a life...)_
- **The Coding Meaning:** A **Function** is a reusable block of code designed to take an input, process it through specific rules, and return a predictable output.
- **The Pun:** The speaker describes their own life as an incomplete, poorly written "function." They feel like a piece of flawed logic that just processes inputs and blindly spits out the same unrewarding, rigid outputs every day.

### 4. オーバーライド (Override)

- **The Lyric:** _「少々ここらでオーバーライド」 (I'll override it a bit right here)_
- **The Coding Meaning:** **Method Overriding** occurs in object-oriented programming when a subclass completely rewrites and replaces a behavior it inherited from a parent class.
- **The Pun:** This is the core theme of the song. The creator is attempting to "override" the inherited rules of their life. They are manually overwriting the flawed programming forced upon them by society or their upbringing to declare their own independent logic. \[[1](https://biz.kddi.com/content/glossary/o/override/), [3](https://utaten.com/specialArticle/index/8505)]

### 5. スタンド・バイ・ミー (Stand by Me / Standby)

- **The Lyric:** _「どうか消えるまでスタンド・バイ・ミー」 (Please stand by me until I disappear)_
- **The Coding Meaning:** **Standby Mode** keeps a device or application powered on in a low-energy, passive state, waiting for a prompt to wake up or terminate.
- **The Pun:** While it sounds like a heartfelt plea for someone to "stay by my side" (referencing the classic song _Stand by Me_), the computer context implies a state of frozen waiting. The speaker is holding on in "standby mode," floating in a low-energy state of depression, just waiting for their system to completely power down or disappear.

### 6. エラー (Error)

- **The Lyric:** _「撒いたエラーすら読んじゃいない」 (They don't even read the errors I've scattered)_
- **The Coding Meaning:** An **Error Log** displays warnings or exception messages when a program fails, detailing exactly what broke and why the code is crashing.
- **The Pun:** The "errors" are the cry-for-help messages, dark lyrics, and personal struggles the creator hid in the song. The pun targets the viral audience: millions of listeners are happily dancing along to the flashy, upbeat music, entirely ignoring the alarming "system error logs" written right into the lyrics.

### 7. 杜撰なコード (Sloppy Code / Bad Code)

- **The Lyric:** _「アンタが書いた杜撰なコード ばっか持てはやすユーザーよ」 (Oh, you users who do nothing but praise the sloppy code you wrote...)_
- **The Coding Meaning:** **Sloppy Code** (or spaghetti code) is poorly written, unoptimized text filled with bugs that somehow manages to run but is incredibly unstable.
- **The Pun:** "You" refers to the creator's past self, and the "users" are the internet audience. The composer looks back at his viral song and views it as unpolished, rushed "spaghetti code." He finds it profoundly ironic that the internet community mindlessly worships and hypes up a glitchy, unstable emotional breakdown. \[[1](https://utaten.com/specialArticle/index/8505)]

## background

The background of **"Override" (オーバーライド)** is rooted in a real-life story of extreme personal desperation, geographic isolation, and a high-stakes career gamble by its creator, **Yoshida Yasei**. \[[1](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z-rPmxZexPI), [2](https://www.billboard.com/music/music-news/yoshida-yasei-interview-override-1235865852/)]

The track was born out of a critical turning point in the producer's life.

### 1. From IT Engineer to Desperate Musician

Before finding major success as a Vocaloid producer, Yoshida Yasei worked as a regular software engineer. He eventually left his programming job to pursue music full-time. \[[1](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z-rPmxZexPI), [2](https://www.billboard.com/music/music-news/yoshida-yasei-interview-override-1235865852/)]

"Override" was composed in October 2023, more than a year after he quit his job. At that point, he was running out of money, lacked stability, and was feeling deeply anxious about his future. In interviews, Yoshida described the track as a **"physical embodiment of desperation"** that was written impulsively to break out of a dark period. \[, [2](https://www.billboard.com/music/music-news/yoshida-yasei-interview-override-1235865852/)]

### 2. The Metaphor of "The Function" and Fate

Yoshida used his professional IT background to form the conceptual spine of the song. He views human existence through the lens of programming logic: \[, [2](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z-rPmxZexPI)]

- **Life as a Program:** He explains that humans are like a chained series of input-and-output "functions". Your birth supplies the input conditions, and your death is the output. \[]
- **The Rigged System:** He felt that certain unchangeable "functions" inherently work against people—such as luck, family wealth, or where a person is raised. \[]

The word **"Override"** was chosen precisely because it means using a new function to completely rewrite inherited, pre-existing rules. The song was his literal attempt to rewrite the trajectory of his own life. \[, [2](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z-rPmxZexPI)]

### 3. Escaping the Countryside ("Den-en")

The lyric _「エスケープ・フロム・デンエン」_ (Escape from Den-en / countryside) reflects Yoshida's real life. He originally lived in **Hokkaido**, where he felt isolated from the creative capital and resources heavily concentrated in Tokyo. \[, [2](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z-rPmxZexPI), [3](https://projectsekai.fandom.com/wiki/Category:Composer:%5FYoshida%5FYasei), [4](https://www.billboard.com/music/music-news/yoshida-yasei-interview-override-1235865852/)]

He faced a downward spiral of feeling like he lacked access to everything. In June 2023, he physically "escaped" by packed up and moving to Tokyo. The raw, frantic frustration of trying to force a breakthrough right after this relocation is what fueled the track's tense, urgent tone. \[]

### 4. Intentional Meme Culture as a Buffer \[[1](https://www.billboard.com/music/music-news/yoshida-yasei-interview-override-1235865852/)]

When "Override" was released in November 2023 featuring the synthetic voice [Kasane Teto](https://world-dai-star.fandom.com/wiki/Override), it exploded across YouTube and Nico Nico Douga, eventually surpassing hundreds of millions of views. \[[1](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z-rPmxZexPI), [2](https://vocaloidlyrics.miraheze.org/wiki/Yoshida%5FYasei)]

This massive virality was intentionally engineered by Yoshida. He purposely paired his heavy, bitter lyrics with highly energetic, upbeat music and nostalgic internet meme references (like the _Tamagotchi_ references in the music video). He did this to create a "joke-like tone". The bright, frantic visual overlay acted as a coping mechanism, masking his genuine psychological distress behind something fun and highly shareable. \[[1](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z-rPmxZexPI), [2](https://www.billboard.com/music/music-news/yoshida-yasei-interview-override-1235865852/)]

# (d) オーバーライド song meaning

**“Override” (オーバーライド) by Yoshida Yasei** is a song about <mark style="margin: 0px; text-decoration-style: solid; text-decoration-color: rgb(230, 232, 240); border-bottom: 0px none rgb(230, 232, 240);"><strong>rebellion against fate, overcoming personal limitations, and rewriting the "programming" of a painful life</strong></mark>**.** While its high-tempo, meme-heavy music video makes it feel lighthearted, the lyrics carry a sharp, deeply personal undercurrent of frustration and desperation. \[[1](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z-rPmxZexPI), [2](https://www.billboard.com/music/music-news/yoshida-yasei-interview-override-1235865852/)]

The song's core themes break down into the following distinct meanings:

### The IT Metaphor (Rewriting Life)

- **Programming definition**: In computer science, to "override" means to replace an existing function or property with a new one.
- **Personal reflection**: Creator Yoshida Yasei wrote the song after quitting his programming job. He felt that life often feels like a rigid, predetermined program.
- **Breaking the loop**: The song uses programming jargon to illustrate the protagonist's desire to forcibly rewrite their own miserable routine and rewrite their fate. \[[1](https://www.tiktok.com/@vocab.like.children/video/7502022155891232007), [2](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z-rPmxZexPI), [3](https://www.billboard.com/music/music-news/yoshida-yasei-interview-override-1235865852/)]

### Pushing Past Human Limits

- **Double meaning**: The song focuses on pushing through exhaustion and mental blocks to break free from limitations.
- **Frustration with reality**: The harsh, "barbed" lyrics detail a strong discontent with society and the current state of the world.
- **The "Override" act**: It represents a desperate, aggressive surge of energy to change one's circumstances when everything feels hopeless. \[[1](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z-rPmxZexPI), [2](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=shyRW65dvn0)]

### The Mask of Internet Culture

- **Contradictory tone**: The music features bright melodies, danceable beats, and heavy usage of Japanese internet memes.
- **Emotional defense mechanism**: Yoshida Yasei has stated that mixing dark, sharp lyrics with bright joke-like elements balances out the bitterness. It allows the listener to digest heavy themes of existential dread through a fun, accessible medium. \[[1](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z-rPmxZexPI)]

# オーバーライド song meaning

The viral Vocaloid song **"Override" (オーバーライド)** by [Yoshida Yasei](https://www.billboard.com/music/music-news/yoshida-yasei-interview-override-1235865852/) featuring Kasane Teto is <mark style="margin: 0px; text-decoration-style: solid; text-decoration-color: rgb(230, 232, 240); border-bottom: 0px none rgb(230, 232, 240);">a deeply personal track about <strong>defying fate, overcoming life's frustrations, and pushing past human limitations</strong></mark>. \[[1](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z-rPmxZexPI), [2](https://www.billboard.com/music/music-news/yoshida-yasei-interview-override-1235865852/)]

While the upbeat music and colorful music video inspired countless internet memes, the core meaning of the song stems from the creator's real-life transition from a software engineer to a full-time musician. \[[1](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z-rPmxZexPI)]

### Core Themes and Meanings

- **Programming Metaphor**: In IT, "override" means rewriting pre-existing rules, settings, or functions to make them do something entirely different. The composer used this coding background to express a desire to "overwrite" his predetermined lifestyle and rewrite his own destiny.
- **Escaping a Dead-End Life**: The song captures the raw frustration, desperation, and angst of feeling trapped in a mundane routine. It paints life like a rigid computer system that needs to be broken out of.
- **Pushing Beyond Limits**: It carries a double meaning of pushing oneself to the absolute limit to force a change in circumstances, regardless of the emotional or physical toll.
- **Biting Lyrics vs. Playful Tone**: The creator purposely paired dark, cynical lyrics about life with chaotic, meme-heavy visuals and upbeat melodies. This creates a coping mechanism where serious existential dread is masked as a giant, energetic joke. \[[1](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z-rPmxZexPI), [2](https://www.billboard.com/music/music-news/yoshida-yasei-interview-override-1235865852/), [3](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=shyRW65dvn0)]

## You said: visual imagery interpretation

The music video (MV) for **"Override,"** illustrated by [Sisia](https://www.billboard.com/music/music-news/yoshida-yasei-interview-override-1235865852/) and directed by Yoshida Yasei, uses a fast-paced, chaotic collage of **vintage 2000s internet aesthetics, retro gaming references, and coding iconography** to visually mask deep existential dread as an energetic joke. \[[1](https://www.billboard.com/music/music-news/yoshida-yasei-interview-override-1235865852/), [2](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z-rPmxZexPI), [3](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=b-txRRwL2rE)]

Here is a breakdown of the primary visual imagery and how it interprets the song's themes:

### 1. The Retro Gaming and Character Parallel

- **The _Tamagotchi_ Reference:** During the line _"Konai kara ne—"_ (I'm not coming back!), the video visualizes two contrasting characters modeled directly after Memetchi and Mametchi from the 2005 Nintendo DS game [_Tamagotchi no Puchi Puchi Omisetchi_](https://utaten.com/specialArticle/index/8505). In the original game, this bitter phrase is thrown at the player if they give the poorest possible service. \[[1](https://utaten.com/specialArticle/index/8505)]
- **The Meaning:** Visually, this captures a complete, mocking severing of ties. It represents the creator giving a "failing grade" to his former mundane lifestyle and refusing to return to it. It also doubles as a meta-commentary on the Vocaloid community, drawing a visual contrast between the long-established industry titans like Hatsune Miku and the historical "underdog" or newcomer status of Kasane Teto. \[[1](https://utaten.com/specialArticle/index/8505)]

### 2. The Programming and Error Screen Interface

- **The Glitch and Blue Screen Imagery:** Windows-style dialogue boxes, command line text, and "Sloppy Code" system errors constantly populate the screen. Teto is often framed directly inside or flashing alongside these user interfaces. \[[1](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=shyRW65dvn0)]
- **The Meaning:** This visualizes the human mind being processed as a rigid computer program. When the chorus asks to "override" a "half-baked function of a life," the visuals mirror a system crash. The colorful, flashing error alerts emphasize that the creator's hidden cries for help and life "bugs" are being broadcast publicly, yet treated by the viewer merely as dynamic, flashy graphics. \[, [2](https://projectsekai.fandom.com/wiki/Override)]

- **The Spasmodic Dancing:** Kasane Teto moves with highly repetitive, jagged, and exaggerated motions, switching sharply between static retro-sprites and expressive frame animations. \[[1](https://www.billboard.com/music/music-news/yoshida-yasei-interview-override-1235865852/)]

By matching heavy, biting lyricism about alienation and systemic failure with an infectious, absurdly danceable visual loop, the MV creates a coping mechanism. It forces the audience to engage in a joyous dance over what is fundamentally an artistic and personal crisis. \[[1](https://www.billboard.com/music/music-news/yoshida-yasei-interview-override-1235865852/), [2](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z-rPmxZexPI)]

- **The Post-Chorus Visual Shifts:** During the lines criticizing users who mindlessly praise "sloppy code," the visual framing shifts toward rapid-fire assets, floating internet commentary boxes, and disconnected imagery. \[[1](https://projectsekai.fandom.com/wiki/Override)]
- **The Meaning:** It starkly visualizes a processing disconnect. Teto/The Creator stands isolated on one side of the screen while an avalanche of internet noise, templates, and superficial user consumption takes over the rest. It emphasizes the absolute irony of virality: a song born from deep isolation is consumed so rapidly that its actual core message is completely buried by its own visual hype. \[[1](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z-rPmxZexPI), [2](https://projectsekai.fandom.com/wiki/Override)]

## You said: programming puns

Because Yoshida Yasei was a software engineer before becoming a full-time musician, **"Override" is packed with literal programming terms used as existential puns**. The lyrics treat human life, fate, and social interactions like broken or rigid software code. \[[1](https://utaten.com/specialArticle/index/8505)]

Here are the specific programming puns hidden in the lyrics and how they connect to the song's deeper meaning:

### 1. デフォ (Default / Default Settings)

- **The Lyric:** _「だけでバッドライフがデフォとか」 (Just because of that, a bad life is the default...)_
- **The Coding Meaning:** A **Default** setting is the pre-configured value or behavior a program automatically falls back on if the user doesn't manually change it.
- **The Pun:** The speaker is born in a bad environment ("bad land"). They feel like society has hard-coded their life's program so that poverty, misery, or failure is their "factory setting" or unchangeable default destination.

### 2. エスケープ (Escape)

- **The Lyric:** _「エスケープ・フロム・デンエン」 (Escape from Den-en / countryside)_
- **The Coding Meaning:** An **Escape Character** or **Escape Sequence** (like hitting the `Esc` key, or using a backslash `\` in code) breaks a program out of its current routine, changes how text is interpreted, or terminates a looping process.
- **The Pun:** While it literally means running away from a rural hometown, it also functions as code. The creator is executing an "escape command" to break out of the mundane loop of daily life and prevent their system from crashing.

### 3. 関数 (Function)

- **The Lyric:** _「半端な生命の関数を」 (This half-baked function of a life...)_
- **The Coding Meaning:** A **Function** is a reusable block of code designed to take an input, process it through specific rules, and return a predictable output.
- **The Pun:** The speaker describes their own life as an incomplete, poorly written "function." They feel like a piece of flawed logic that just processes inputs and blindly spits out the same unrewarding, rigid outputs every day.

### 4. オーバーライド (Override)

- **The Lyric:** _「少々ここらでオーバーライド」 (I'll override it a bit right here)_
- **The Coding Meaning:** **Method Overriding** occurs in object-oriented programming when a subclass completely rewrites and replaces a behavior it inherited from a parent class.
- **The Pun:** This is the core theme of the song. The creator is attempting to "override" the inherited rules of their life. They are manually overwriting the flawed programming forced upon them by society or their upbringing to declare their own independent logic. \[[1](https://biz.kddi.com/content/glossary/o/override/), [3](https://utaten.com/specialArticle/index/8505)]

### 5. スタンド・バイ・ミー (Stand by Me / Standby)

- **The Lyric:** _「どうか消えるまでスタンド・バイ・ミー」 (Please stand by me until I disappear)_
- **The Coding Meaning:** **Standby Mode** keeps a device or application powered on in a low-energy, passive state, waiting for a prompt to wake up or terminate.
- **The Pun:** While it sounds like a heartfelt plea for someone to "stay by my side" (referencing the classic song _Stand by Me_), the computer context implies a state of frozen waiting. The speaker is holding on in "standby mode," floating in a low-energy state of depression, just waiting for their system to completely power down or disappear.

### 6. エラー (Error)

- **The Lyric:** _「撒いたエラーすら読んじゃいない」 (They don't even read the errors I've scattered)_
- **The Coding Meaning:** An **Error Log** displays warnings or exception messages when a program fails, detailing exactly what broke and why the code is crashing.
- **The Pun:** The "errors" are the cry-for-help messages, dark lyrics, and personal struggles the creator hid in the song. The pun targets the viral audience: millions of listeners are happily dancing along to the flashy, upbeat music, entirely ignoring the alarming "system error logs" written right into the lyrics.

### 7. 杜撰なコード (Sloppy Code / Bad Code)

- **The Lyric:** _「アンタが書いた杜撰なコード ばっか持てはやすユーザーよ」 (Oh, you users who do nothing but praise the sloppy code you wrote...)_
- **The Coding Meaning:** **Sloppy Code** (or spaghetti code) is poorly written, unoptimized text filled with bugs that somehow manages to run but is incredibly unstable.
- **The Pun:** "You" refers to the creator's past self, and the "users" are the internet audience. The composer looks back at his viral song and views it as unpolished, rushed "spaghetti code." He finds it profoundly ironic that the internet community mindlessly worships and hypes up a glitchy, unstable emotional breakdown. \[[1](https://utaten.com/specialArticle/index/8505)]

## You said: background

The background of **"Override" (オーバーライド)** is rooted in a real-life story of extreme personal desperation, geographic isolation, and a high-stakes career gamble by its creator, **Yoshida Yasei**. \[[1](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z-rPmxZexPI), [2](https://www.billboard.com/music/music-news/yoshida-yasei-interview-override-1235865852/)]

The track was born out of a critical turning point in the producer's life.

### 1. From IT Engineer to Desperate Musician

Before finding major success as a Vocaloid producer, Yoshida Yasei worked as a regular software engineer. He eventually left his programming job to pursue music full-time. \[[1](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z-rPmxZexPI), [2](https://www.billboard.com/music/music-news/yoshida-yasei-interview-override-1235865852/)]

"Override" was composed in October 2023, more than a year after he quit his job. At that point, he was running out of money, lacked stability, and was feeling deeply anxious about his future. In interviews, Yoshida described the track as a **"physical embodiment of desperation"** that was written impulsively to break out of a dark period. \[, [2](https://www.billboard.com/music/music-news/yoshida-yasei-interview-override-1235865852/)]

### 2. The Metaphor of "The Function" and Fate

Yoshida used his professional IT background to form the conceptual spine of the song. He views human existence through the lens of programming logic: \[, [2](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z-rPmxZexPI)]

- **Life as a Program:** He explains that humans are like a chained series of input-and-output "functions". Your birth supplies the input conditions, and your death is the output. \[]
- **The Rigged System:** He felt that certain unchangeable "functions" inherently work against people—such as luck, family wealth, or where a person is raised. \[]

The word **"Override"** was chosen precisely because it means using a new function to completely rewrite inherited, pre-existing rules. The song was his literal attempt to rewrite the trajectory of his own life. \[, [2](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z-rPmxZexPI)]

### 3. Escaping the Countryside ("Den-en")

The lyric _「エスケープ・フロム・デンエン」_ (Escape from Den-en / countryside) reflects Yoshida's real life. He originally lived in **Hokkaido**, where he felt isolated from the creative capital and resources heavily concentrated in Tokyo. \[, [2](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z-rPmxZexPI), [3](https://projectsekai.fandom.com/wiki/Category:Composer:%5FYoshida%5FYasei), [4](https://www.billboard.com/music/music-news/yoshida-yasei-interview-override-1235865852/)]

He faced a downward spiral of feeling like he lacked access to everything. In June 2023, he physically "escaped" by packed up and moving to Tokyo. The raw, frantic frustration of trying to force a breakthrough right after this relocation is what fueled the track's tense, urgent tone. \[]

### 4. Intentional Meme Culture as a Buffer \[[1](https://www.billboard.com/music/music-news/yoshida-yasei-interview-override-1235865852/)]

When "Override" was released in November 2023 featuring the synthetic voice [Kasane Teto](https://world-dai-star.fandom.com/wiki/Override), it exploded across YouTube and Nico Nico Douga, eventually surpassing hundreds of millions of views. \[[1](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z-rPmxZexPI), [2](https://vocaloidlyrics.miraheze.org/wiki/Yoshida%5FYasei)]

This massive virality was intentionally engineered by Yoshida. He purposely paired his heavy, bitter lyrics with highly energetic, upbeat music and nostalgic internet meme references (like the _Tamagotchi_ references in the music video). He did this to create a "joke-like tone". The bright, frantic visual overlay acted as a coping mechanism, masking his genuine psychological distress behind something fun and highly shareable. \[[1](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z-rPmxZexPI), [2](https://www.billboard.com/music/music-news/yoshida-yasei-interview-override-1235865852/)]
