criticism on patriotism

6:13 pm

Criticism of patriotism often focuses on its potential to foster irrationality, exclusion, and moral compromise. While it is frequently seen as a virtue, critics argue it can be manipulated by elites to justify violence or suppress internal dissent.

Core Ethical & Philosophical Objections

- **Arbitrariness of Birth**: Critics like those featured on [Eurozine](https://www.eurozine.com/the-trouble-with-patriotism/) argue that national identity is a "mere accident of birth" \[12]. Feeling intense pride for a country simply because one was born there is often viewed as irrational, as the individual played no role in the country's achievements \[2].
- **Moral Corruption & "Blind Loyalty"**: Patriotism can lead to "non-reflective" views of history, where citizens ignore or minimize their nation's past crimes to maintain a positive self-image \[1]. This "blind loyalty" can cause individuals to support unjust government actions because questioning authority is labeled "unpatriotic" \[5, 10].
- **Exclusion and "Us vs. Them"**: By prioritizing one's own nation above others, patriotism is inherently divisive. It creates an [exclusionary mentality](https://askfilo.com/user-question-answers-smart-solutions/disadvantages-of-patriotism-and-sacrifice-3337343130353936) that can fuel xenophobia, racism, and hostility toward outsiders \[2, 5, 11].

Political and Ideological Critiques

- **Marxist Perspective**: From a Marxist viewpoint, patriotism is seen as a tool used by the ruling class to [oppress the working class](https://link.springer.com/rwe/10.1007/978-3-319-54484-7%5F13). It encourages workers to align with their domestic oppressors (capitalists) rather than with fellow workers abroad, undermining international solidarity \[9].
- **Manipulation for War**: Critics often point out that leaders may "bang the drums of war" to whip up [patriotic fervor](https://encyclopedia.uia.org/problem/lack-patriotism), convincing citizens to sacrifice their lives and rights for national interests that may not truly benefit them \[11].
- **Elite Exploitation**: Some argue that patriotism is a [defensive concept cultivated by elites](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Nwocu2NT3tc) through education and media to maintain social control and discourage structural change \[2].

Summary of Disadvantages

| Aspect           | Major Criticisms                                                                                   |
| ---------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Social**       | Promotes intolerance, xenophobia, and internal division \[5].                                      |
| **Intellectual** | Suppresses dissent and discourages critical thinking about policies \[5, 10].                      |
| **Moral**        | Validates "my country, right or wrong," leading to the minimization of historical atrocities \[1]. |
| **Global**       | Fuels national rivalries and justifies international conflict and violence \[5, 11].               |

Ultimately, many critics advocate for alternatives like **cosmopolitanism** (identifying as a citizen of the world) or "ironic patriotism," where one maintains a healthy skepticism and can "turn off" national loyalty when it conflicts with universal justice \[1].

criticism on patriotism in china

6:13 pm

Criticism of patriotism in

China

generally falls into three categories: internal state regulations, domestic societal debates, and international or academic critiques.

1\. State-Led Criticisms: "Low-Level Red" and "High-Level Black"

The Chinese government and state media have increasingly criticized certain forms of extreme or commercialised patriotism that they believe undermine official narratives:

- **Commercialisation of Patriotism**: State media outlets, such as CCTV, have warned that "patriotism is not a business." They criticize vloggers and "Big V" influencers who manufacture outrage—such as misidentifying shopping mall decorations as Japanese motifs—solely to gain traffic and profit.
- **"Low-Level Red" (diji hong)**: This term refers to poorly executed or "vulgar" propaganda that is so over-the-top or illogical that it inadvertently mocks the state or invites public ridicule.
- **"High-Level Black" (gaoji hei)**: This describes sophisticated political satire that appears to praise the state on the surface but is actually intended to be critical or cynical.
- **Ultra-Short War Dramas**: Regulatory bodies like the [National Radio and Television Administration (NRTA)](https://orcasia.org/article/1309/the-propaganda-trap-of-chinas-viral-patriotism) have restricted "anti-intellectual" short films that depict historical wars with outrageous, slapstick plots, citing their negative impact on youth values.

2\. Domestic Societal Debates

Within Chinese society, critics highlight how extreme patriotism can stifle rational discourse:

- **"Little Pinks" (Xiao Fen Hong)**: This younger generation of online nationalists is often criticized by liberal-leaning Chinese intellectuals for being "blindly patriotic" and aggressive toward anyone—domestic or foreign—perceived as slighting China.
- **Stifling Diversity of Thought**: Critics argue that making patriotism a "moral duty" in textbooks leaves no room for citizens to disagree on the country's direction or to maintain a critical distance from state actions.
- **"Wolf Warrior" Tendencies**: Some citizens express concern that "wolf warrior" diplomacy and extreme online populism lead to a total rejection of foreign cultures and an inability to view international relations rationally.

3\. Academic and Human Rights Criticisms

- **Indoctrination vs. Education**: International observers and dissidents often label China’s [Patriotic Education Law](https://www.dw.com/en/why-is-china-promoting-patriotic-education/a-66562563) and school curricula as "brainwashing" intended to ensure absolute loyalty to the Communist Party (CCP) rather than genuine love for the country.

- **Link to Nationalism**: Academic research suggests that Chinese patriotism is often indistinguishable from "malign nationalism," which promotes superiority and zero-tolerance for dissent.

- **The "Victimhood" Narrative**: Critics argue that the state’s focus on past humiliations at the hands of foreign powers (like Japan) is used as political capital to reinforce the Party's legitimacy, sometimes preventing sensible foreign policy rapprochement.

criticism on chinese patriotism

Criticism of Chinese patriotism often centers on its transformation from a unifying sentiment into a tool for state legitimacy and commercial profit, frequently leading to extreme nationalism or social division.

Key Areas of Criticism

- **Conflation of Party and Country**: A major point of domestic and international criticism is the deliberate blurring of lines between "loving the country" and "loving the Communist Party".
  - State-led campaigns, such as the [Patriotic Education Law](https://www.cnn.com/2024/01/06/china/china-patriotic-education-law-intl-hnk), legally mandate that institutions like schools and museums instill loyalty specifically to the state and its ideology.
  - Critics argue this forces an "inward-looking" patriotism that demands obedience and treats dissent as "unpatriotic" or "traitorous".

- **Rise of Extreme and "Irrational" Nationalism**:
  - **"Little Pinkos" (Xiao Fen Hong)**: This term describes young, digitally active nationalists often criticized for aggressive online behavior, such as swarming the social media accounts of those perceived to have "slighted" China.
  - **"Wolf Warrior" Diplomacy**: Critics point to increasingly aggressive rhetoric from officials and influencers, such as former _Global Times_ editor\
    [Hu Xijin](https://www.google.com/search?q=hu+xijin\&kgmid=%2Fm%2F0qs8qm4\&sa=X\&ved=2ahUKEwj2rve85o2UAxWMamwGHUbhMsAQ3egRegQIBRAI\&gl=us)\
    , whose "bloodthirsty" talk regarding international disputes is seen as stoking dangerous, senseless nationalism.
  - **Historical Grievance**: The "Century of Humiliation" narrative is used to unite the public against perceived external threats, but critics argue this fosters a sense of victimization that can lead to [revanchism](https://eng.globalaffairs.ru/articles/patriotism-internationalism-skosyrev/) and xenophobia.

- **Commercialization of Patriotism**: Patriotism has become a profitable business on Chinese social media.
  - **"Patriotic Big Vs"**: Influencers use sensationalist, anti-foreign content to attract traffic and monetization.
  - **Double Standards**: Critics highlight "two-faced" patriots—influencers who publish anti-Western content for profit while living in or sending family to Western countries.
  - **Market Exploitation**: The "Guo Chao" (national wave) trend encourages buying domestic products like Xiaomi or local cosmetics to "have face". While supporting the economy, critics worry this serves to further isolate China and alienate foreign firms.

- **Social and Cultural Suppression**:
  - **Marginalization of "Unpatriotic" Groups**: Individuals who do not fit the traditional "ideal citizen" image—such as feminists or the LGBT+ community—are often labeled unpatriotic and Westernized, leading to public marginalization.
  - **"High-Level Black" vs. "Low-Level Red"**: These state-coined labels are used to delegitimize "undesired" patriotic content—either satire that subtly mocks the state ("high-level black") or overly crude propaganda that inadvertently makes the state look bad ("low-level red").

- **Censorship of Legitimate Criticism**: Even well-meaning critiques of patriotic works—such as reviews of [patriotic films](https://www.thinkchina.sg/society/can-chinese-criticise-their-patriotic-movies) that find them artistically lacking—are frequently deleted, raising questions about whether patriotic themes are being used to shield lower artistic or intellectual standards from scrutiny.
