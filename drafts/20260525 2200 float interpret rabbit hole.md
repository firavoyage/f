1\. The English Idiom ("Go down the rabbit hole")

In everyday English, to "go down the rabbit hole" means to **become so deeply absorbed in a topic or situation that you lose track of time and cannot easily get out**.

- **Internet/Media Binging:** Clicking one video on YouTube or TikTok, then another, and suddenly realizing you have spent hours watching random clips.
- **Deep Research:** Becoming hyper-focused on a specific niche, conspiracy theory, or hobby, and continuously digging for more information.
- **Complex Situations:** Entering a bizarre, chaotic, or confusing situation that gets more complicated the further you go.

**The Origin:**\
This phrase comes from Lewis Carroll’s 1865 novel, _Alice's Adventures in Wonderland_. In the story, Alice follows the White Rabbit down its rabbit hole and tumbles into a surreal, nonsensical world where normal logic does not apply.

The Core Theme

The song is told from the perspective of a cynical, detached narrator (Miku) who prefers shallow, casual hookups. She is mocking and scolding her partner, who has caught real, serious feelings ("pure love") for her. To her, love is just a game to relieve loneliness, and getting emotionally attached is incredibly uncool.
