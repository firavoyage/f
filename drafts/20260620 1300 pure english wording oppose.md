### Verbs Meaning "To Be Against"

- **Oppose**: To actively disagree with a plan, law, or idea.
- **Resist**: To strive or fight against an opposing force.
- **Defy**: To openly refuse to obey a rule, person, or law.
- **Object**: To express disapproval or opposition to something.
- **Contradict**: To express the opposite of a statement. \[[1](https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/against), [2](https://dictionary.cambridge.org/thesaurus/be-against), [3](https://www.merriam-webster.com/thesaurus/oppose), [4](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/american%5Fenglish/against), [5](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/oppose)]

### Common Phrasal Verbs Using "Against"

- **Go against**: To violate a principle, or to oppose someone.
- **Speak out against**: To publicly criticize or oppose an issue.
- **Run up against**: To encounter a problem, difficulty, or obstacle.
- **Turn against**: To stop supporting someone and become hostile to them. \[[1](https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/against), [2](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/go%20against), [3](https://dictionary.cambridge.org/us/dictionary/english/go-against), [4](https://dictionary.cambridge.org/thesaurus/be-against), [5](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/IwbC-jPeiJs), [6](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/turn%20against)]
