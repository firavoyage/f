what is link rot

---

Link rot is a quiet, ordinary thing that happens over time.

It means a link on the internet stops working.
You click it, expecting a page… and instead you find nothing. The page was moved, changed, or deleted.

Websites don’t stay still.
People redesign them, shut them down, or forget old pages. When that happens, the links pointing to those pages slowly decay. Like paper left in the sun, they fade.

Link rot matters because it breaks trust and memory.
Articles lose their sources. Research loses its trail. Conversations lose context.

In a gentle way, link rot reminds us of something human:
the internet feels permanent, but it isn’t.
It needs care, attention, and sometimes preservation—like saving copies, using archives, or updating links.
