For USDT (Tether), the technical fee is almost entirely determined by which "network" you choose to use. Because USDT is a token that lives on many different blockchains, you can send the exact same $100 for as little as a fraction of a cent or as much as $30.

| **Ethereum (ERC-20)**      | **$5.00 – $30.00+** | High-value, high-security transfers only. |

The "Tron Paradox"

[Tron (TRC-20)](https://tron.network/) is actually the most widely used network for USDT, handling over 45-52% of all transfers. Even though its fees ($1–$3) are higher than Solana’s, it remains the standard because it is supported by almost every global exchange and merchant.

Why Fees Still Feel High

If you are still seeing high fees for USDT, it is likely due to **Platform Withdrawal Fees** rather than the blockchain itself:

- **Exchange Markups**: Exchanges like [Coinbase](https://help.coinbase.com/en/exchange/trading-and-funding/exchange-fees) or [Binance](https://www.binance.com/en/fee/cryptoFee) often charge a flat "withdrawal fee" (e.g., 1–10 USDT) to cover their own costs and the network gas.
- **Address Activation**: On the Tron network, if you send USDT to a brand-new wallet for the first time, the fee is roughly double (~$3.90) because the network has to "activate" that new account.
- **Convenience Fees**: Buying USDT directly with a credit card usually carries a 2–6% processing fee, which is far higher than the technical network fee.

the history of solana crypto

Solana was founded in 2017 by

**[Anatoly Yakovenko](https://www.google.com/search?q=anatoly+yakovenko\&kgmid=%2Fg%2F11j%5F6fwcll\&sa=X\&ved=2ahUKEwiF7J-RvYmUAxUzxzgGHTGsEHwQ3egRegQIAhAC\&gl=us)**

, a former Qualcomm engineer, with the goal of creating a high-performance blockchain capable of internet-level speeds. Its defining innovation is **Proof of History (PoH)**, a cryptographic clock that allows the network to process transactions much faster than traditional blockchains like Ethereum.

Timeline of Key Events

- **2017–2018: Conceptualization**
  - **Whitepaper Released**: Yakovenko published the [Solana Whitepaper](https://solana.com/solana-whitepaper.pdf) in November 2017, introducing Proof of History.
  - **Founding Team**: Co-founders Raj Gokal, Greg Fitzgerald, and Stephen Akridge joined to form Solana Labs in San Francisco.
  - **Early Prototype**: In February 2018, the team released the first prototype, originally called "Loom," demonstrating 10,000 transactions in half a second.
- **2019–2020: Launch & Early Growth**
  - **Mainnet Beta Launch**: The network officially went live on **March 16, 2020**.
  - **Public Auction**: SOL tokens debuted via a [CoinList](https://coinlist.co/solana) auction at approximately **$0.22** per token.
  - **Ecosystem Expansion**: The [Solana Foundation](https://solana.org/) was established in June 2020 to fund decentralisation and community initiatives.
- **2021: "Solana Summer"**
  - **Explosive Growth**: Solana became a leading competitor to Ethereum during the DeFi and NFT boom.
  - **First Major Peak**: Driven by projects like Degenerate Ape Academy, the price of SOL reached a peak of roughly **$260** in November 2021.
- **2022–2023: Resilience and Challenges**
  - **Network Outages**: The platform faced several notable outages due to transaction spikes and consensus bugs.
  - **FTX Collapse**: Solana’s association with Sam Bankman-Fried and [FTX](https://ftx.com/) caused a price crash in late 2022, with SOL bottoming near **$8**.
  - **Strategic Pivot**: In 2023, Solana launched the Saga Smartphone and partnered with [Visa](https://usa.visa.com/about-visa/newsroom/press-releases.releaseId.19881.html) for USDC stablecoin settlement.
- **2024–2025: Institutional Adoption**
  - **New Highs**: In **January 2025**, SOL reached a new all-time high of approximately **$294**.
  - **Mainstream Integration**: High retail activity was fueled by a memecoin craze on platforms like [pump.fun](https://pump.fun/) and the launch of spot Solana ETFs by major institutions like [Fidelity](https://www.fidelity.com/) and [Bitwise](https://bitwiseinvestments.com/).
