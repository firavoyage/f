ruma kairiki bear meaning

The word **"Ruma" (ルマ)** in Kairiki Bear's hit Vocaloid song primarily means <mark style="margin: 0px; text-decoration-style: solid; text-decoration-color: rgb(230, 232, 240); border-bottom: 0px none rgb(230, 232, 240);"><strong>"Maru" (マル - meaning a correct mark, a perfect score, or a circle) spelled backward</strong></mark>. By reversing the word for a perfect score, the title symbolizes a **"faulty," imperfect life or failing grades**. \[[1](https://utaten.com/specialArticle/index/5813), [2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kairiki%5FBear), [3](https://jujutsu-kaisen.fandom.com/wiki/Marulu%5FVal%5FVol%5FYelvori), [4](https://genius.com/Genius-english-translations-kairiki-bear-mare-mare-ft-kizuna-akari-english-translation-lyrics)]

The song was originally written for the Japanese utaite singer Riinu (from Strawberry Prince) and later released as a Hatsune Miku self-cover. The deeper meaning of the song can be broken down through its lyrical themes and wordplay: \[[1](https://utaten.com/specialArticle/index/4638), [2](https://utaten.com/specialArticle/index/5813), [3](https://genius.com/Genius-english-translations-kairiki-bear-mare-mare-ft-kizuna-akari-english-translation-lyrics)]

1\. Rejecting Societal "Correctness"

The song revolves around a protagonist who has given up trying to achieve a perfect score or a flaw-free life ("_There is no life with full marks, or even any brilliant answer_"). They are heavily criticized by society, represented by the repeated symbol of getting "X-marks" (failures). Instead of conforming to society's standards to get a "Maru" (circle/correct mark), the protagonist chooses to embrace their "Ruma" (backward/imperfect) reality. \[[1](https://open.spotify.com/track/72UA2UPUO3eYGZ45UG4LqW), [2](https://projectsekai.fandom.com/wiki/Ruma), [3](https://utaten.com/specialArticle/index/5813), [4](https://genius.com/Genius-english-translations-kairiki-bear-mare-mare-ft-kizuna-akari-english-translation-lyrics), [5](https://hellokitty.fandom.com/wiki/Bad%5FBadtz-Maru)]

2\. Triple Wordplay (Batten, Karma, and Barking)

Fans and lyric analysts have identified multiple layers of wordplay intentionally woven into the title and lyrics: \[[1](https://genius.com/Genius-english-translations-kairiki-bear-mare-mare-ft-kizuna-akari-english-translation-lyrics)]

- **The "X" Mark (Batten):** If you take the Japanese characters for Ruma (**ルマ**), flip them upside down, and cross them over, they visually resemble the English letter "X" or a cross mark. This symbolizes the "wrong answer" or "failed" mark mentioned throughout the lyrics.
- **Karma (カルマ):** The lyrics state "_Before it leads to karma (カルマ)..._". Dropping the "Ka" leaves "Ruma," hinting that the song deals with escaping a cycle of bad fate or external judgment.
- **Animal Motifs (Woof / Waon):** Because the song was written for Riinu (who has a dog avatar), the lyrics are filled with dog-like howling ("_Waon!_", "_Bark bark bark_"). The title "Ruma" sounds like the end of the word _Karuma_, but it also ties into the wild, uninhibited animalistic instinct of refusing to be a "good, tamed pet" for society. \[[1](https://open.spotify.com/track/72UA2UPUO3eYGZ45UG4LqW), [2](https://w.atwiki.jp/hmiku/pages/40213.html), [3](https://projectsekai.fandom.com/wiki/Ruma), [4](https://note.com/koyan97/n/n018fb8b3c37b), [5](https://www.reddit.com/r/ProjectSekai/comments/1oo0626/ruma%5Fby%5Fkairiki%5Fbear%5Ffeat%5Fmore%5Fmore%5Fjump%5Fkagamine/)]

3\. Staying True to One's Ego

Ultimately, the song is an anthem about **defiance and individuality**. The chorus commands the listener to "_persist with your ego_" and "_crawl back up with your mental state burned to the ground_". It suggests that even if society gives you a failing grade ("X"), you should proudly reject their version of a perfect answer and live your own life. \[[1](https://utaten.com/specialArticle/index/5813), [2](https://projectsekai.fandom.com/wiki/Ruma), [3](https://genius.com/Genius-english-translations-kairiki-bear-mare-mare-ft-kizuna-akari-english-translation-lyrics)]

Here is a detailed, section-by-section breakdown of the lyrics, exposing the hidden wordplay, dog motifs, and the emotional descent from despair into pure defiance.

Verse 1: Rejecting Society's Expectations

> **満点な人生も 秀才な解答もございません**\
> _(There is no life with full marks, nor any brilliant answer.)_\
> **有害な評論も 見え透いた同情も 聞きたくはないな**\
> _(I don't want to hear toxic criticism, nor transparent sympathy.)_

- **Meaning:** The protagonist immediately establishes that they do not fit into society's mold of a perfect, "full marks" life. They are completely exhausted by outsiders judging them (toxic criticism) or pretending to care (transparent sympathy) about their failures.

Verse 2: Refusing the Standard Path

> **壮観な表彰も 平凡な真っ当もございません**\
> _(There are no spectacular awards, nor any ordinary honesty.)_\
> **暗転な将来も 傲慢な快晴も 見たくはないな**\
> _(I don't want to see a pitch-black future, nor an arrogant, clear sky.)_

- **Meaning:** They accept that they will never win awards or live a peaceful, normal life. However, they also refuse to succumb to total despair (a pitch-black future). The "arrogant, clear sky" represents the blinding, toxic positivity of successful people who don't understand struggle.

Pre-Chorus 1: The Mental Breakdown (The Dog Begins to Show)

> **はいはい 俯いちゃってヤンヤンヤン クラクラリ 彷徨って**\
> _(Yeah, yeah, looking down, annoying-noying-noying, dizzy, wandering around)_\
> **正答 失っちゃって わーんわーんわーん だって！**\
> _(Losing the correct answer, crying "wah-wah-wah" (barking), because...)_\
> **心は満たされない！**\
> _(My heart cannot be satisfied!)_

- **Meaning:** Under the weight of failing, the protagonist experiences a dizzying mental breakdown.
- **Wordplay & Motifs:** "わーん" (_waan_) doubles as the sound of a human crying and a dog barking ("woof"). "ヤンヤン" (_yan-yan_) mimics a whining, agitated state. They have lost the "right path" and feel completely empty.

Chorus 1: Devouring the Failing Grades

> **愛を頂戴** _(Give me love)_\
> **感情熱唱メッタッタッタ 声枯らせ** _(Sing passionately with raw emotion, slash it all up, wither your voice)_\
> **全然わかんない ×点 喰らい尽くせ** _(I don't understand any of it, devour all the X-marks)_\
> **心臓血漿ラッタッタッタ 踊り舞え** _(Heart plasma, rat-tat-tat-tat, dance away)_\
> **正解なんてバイバイ提唱だダダダダ (ﾜｵｰﾝ！)** _(Say bye-bye to correct answers, advocate it, da-da-da-da (WOOF!))_

- **Meaning:** Out of loneliness, they scream for love. Since they don't understand what society wants from them, they decide to violently "devour" and destroy all the "X-marks" (failing grades) they've been given.
- **Wordplay & Motifs:** "メッタッタ" (_metta-tta_) comes from _metameta_ (to slash/destroy). Instead of fixing their wrong answers, they dance on them and say "bye-bye" to correctness, ending with a feral dog howl (_waon!_).

Verse 3 & 4: Stagnation and Despair

> **満点な答案も 優秀な成功もございません** _(There are no test papers with full marks, nor excellent success.)_\
> **後悔な人生も 停滞の殺到も 見たくはないな** _(I don't want to see a life of regret, nor a flood of stagnation.)_

- **Meaning:** A parallel to the first verses. They know they aren't excellent, but they desperately want to escape the suffocating feeling of being stuck in one place forever doing nothing.

Pre-Chorus 2: Trapped with No Exit

> **毎回 躓いちゃって きゃんきゃんきゃん フラフラリ 悴んで**\
> _(Tripping up every single time, yelping "kyan-kyan-kyan," unsteady, freezing up)_\
> **正答 無くなっちゃって わーんわーんわーん 待って！**\
> _(The correct answer is completely gone, crying/barking, wait!)_\
> **出口も見当たらない？** _(Is there no exit in sight?)_

- **Meaning:** The struggle intensifies.
- **Wordplay & Motifs:** "きゃんきゃん" (_kyan-kyan_) is explicitly the Japanese onomatopoeia for a small dog yelping in pain when stepped on or hurt. They are losing their footing, freezing from anxiety, and realize they are trapped in a maze with no "right" way out.

Chorus 2: Laughing at the Failure

> **(愛を頂戴... 声枯らせ)** _(Give me love... wither your voice)_\
> **断然止まんない ×点 笑い倒せ** _(Absolutely unstoppable, laugh your head off at the X-marks)_\
> **心臓血漿マッカッカッカ 踊り舞え** _(Heart plasma, blood red-red-red, dance away)_\
> **正解なんて無い無い提唱だダダダダ (ﾜｵｰﾝ！)** _(Advocate that there are no right answers at all, da-da-da-da (WOOF!))_

- **Meaning:** Instead of devouring the failures like in the first chorus, they have now gone mad with defiance. They look at their failing scores ("X") and completely mock them, laughing right in the face of judgment.
- **Wordplay & Motifs:** "マッカッカ" (_makkakka_) means bright, burning red, showing that their heart and adrenaline are pumping violently as they bark at the world.

Bridge: The Climax of Individuality ("Ruma" Realized)

> **愛を頂戴** _(Give me love)_\
> **感情裂傷メッタッタッタ 詩歌え** _(Emotions lacerated, slash it up, recite the poem/sing)_\
> **全然染まんない「我」貫き通せ** _(Completely unstained, push through with your "Ego")_\
> **心境全焼落下ッカッカ 這い上がれ** _(Mental state entirely burned to ash, falling down, laugh it off, crawl back up)_\
> **正解なんて曖昧立証だダダダダ** _(Proving that "correct answers" are entirely ambiguous, da-da-da-da)_

- **Meaning:** This is the core thesis of the song. Their emotions are physically wounded ("lacerated"), and their mental state is completely burned to the ground. Yet, they command themselves to **"push through with your Ego."** They will not be "stained" or dyed by society's colors. They prove that what society calls a "correct answer" is actually fake and ambiguous.

Final Chorus: Ultimate Defiance

> **正解なんてバイバイ推奨だダダダダ** _(Recommending a bye-bye to correct answers)_\
> **正解なんて無い無い提唱だダダダダ** _(Advocate that there are no right answers at all)_\
> **だ だダダダダ (ﾜｵｰﾝ！)** _(Da, da-da-da-da (WOOF!))_

- **Meaning:** The song spirals into a manic chant. The protagonist formally recommends that everyone throw away the idea of a perfect life ("Maru"). They finish the song not as a defeated human, but as a wild animal howling (_waon_) in absolute freedom, completely unbothered by the "X" marks.

You said: wordplay

The brilliance of Kairiki Bear’s writing in **"Ruma"** lies in how he packs layers of sonic, visual, and conceptual wordplay into tiny, repeating fragments. \[[1](https://w.atwiki.jp/hmiku/pages/40213.html)]

By analyzing the lyrics closely, we can uncover **four major linguistic tricks** used throughout the track:

***

1\. Rhyme Schemes and Alliteration (The "-atta" Explosion)

Kairiki Bear deliberately uses repetitive, percussive sounds to make the chorus feel chaotic, frantic, and physically breathless. He relies on **rhyming nouns with matching 4-beat sound effects**: \[[1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kairiki%5FBear), [2](https://whale-write.com/ruma-kairikibear)]

- **Verse Phase 1:**
  - _Kanjou Nesshou_ (感情熱唱 - Passionate singing) \\(\rightarrow \\) **Mettattatta** (メッタッタッタ - from _metameta_, meaning to slash or hack wildly).
  - _Shinzou Kesshou_ (心臓血漿 - Heart plasma) \\(\rightarrow \\) **Rattattatta** (ラッタッタッタ - mimics a rapid, machine-gun heartbeat). \[[1](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fRtjXP77xL4), [2](https://w.atwiki.jp/hmiku/pages/40213.html), [3](https://vocaloid.fandom.com/wiki/%E3%83%AB%E3%83%9E%5F%28Ruma%29), [4](https://whale-write.com/ruma-kairikibear), [5](https://vocaloid.fandom.com/wiki/%E3%83%AB%E3%83%9E%5F%28Ruma%29)]
- **Verse Phase 2:**
  - _Kanjou Nesshou_ (感情熱唱) \\(\rightarrow \\) **Mettattatta**.
  - _Shinzou Kesshou_ (心臓血漿) \\(\rightarrow \\) **Makkakkakka** (マッカッカッカ - from _makkaka_, meaning burning, bright red, signifying adrenaline). \[[1](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fRtjXP77xL4), [2](https://w.atwiki.jp/hmiku/pages/40213.html), [3](https://vocaloid.fandom.com/wiki/%E3%83%AB%E3%83%9E%5F%28Ruma%29)]
- **The Climax (The twist):**
  - \*Kanjou **Resshou\*** (感情**裂傷** - Lacerated/torn emotions) \\(\rightarrow \\) **Mettattatta**. He swaps "singing" (_nesshou_) for "wounds" (_resshou_), but it sounds nearly identical when sung fast.
  - \*Shinkyou **Zenshou\*** (心境**全焼** - Mental state entirely burned down) \\(\rightarrow \\) **Rakkakkakka** (落下ッカッカ - from _rakka_, to plummet down, combined with manic laughter "kah-kah-kah"). \[[1](https://vocaloid.fandom.com/wiki/%E3%83%AB%E3%83%9E%5F%28Ruma%29), [2](https://w.atwiki.jp/hmiku/pages/40213.html)]

***

2\. The Multi-Layered Suffix System (Teishou, Suishou, Risshou)

In the last line of every chorus, the song plays on words ending in **"-shou" (しょう)**, altering the protagonist's mindset from simple rejection to absolute philosophical defiance:

- **Chorus 1:** _Baibai **Teishou** だ_ (バイバイ**提唱**だ) \\(\rightarrow \\) "**Advocating** for saying bye-bye to correct answers."
- **Chorus 2:** _Nainai **Teishou** だ_ (無い無い**提唱**だ) \\(\rightarrow \\) "**Advocating** that correct answers don't even exist."
- **Bridge:** _Aimai **Risshou** だ_ (曖昧**立証**だ) \\(\rightarrow \\) "**Proving/Demonstrating** that correct answers are completely ambiguous."
- **Outro:** _Baibai **Suishou** だ_ (バイバイ**推奨**だ) \\(\rightarrow \\) "**Recommending** that everyone say bye-bye to correct answers." \[[1](https://w.atwiki.jp/hmiku/pages/40213.html), [2](https://vocaloid.fandom.com/wiki/%E3%83%AB%E3%83%9E%5F%28Ruma%29)]

***

3\. Dual-Meaning Onomatopoeia (The Dog Persona)

Because this song was custom-tailored for the singer Riinu (who uses a canine avatar), the breakdown lyrics use words that seamlessly blend human suffering with dog sounds: \[[1](https://genius.com/Genius-english-translations-kairiki-bear-ruma-ft-hatsune-miku-english-translation-lyrics), [2](https://whale-write.com/ruma-kairikibear)]

- **ヤンヤン (Yan-yan):** Slang for a whining, anxious mental state (_yandere_ / _yamu_), but sounds like a puppy whimpering.
- **わーん (Waan):** The common Japanese sound effect for a human crying hysterically ("Wahhh!"), but also the phonetic sound of a large dog barking ("Woof!"). \[[1](https://genius.com/Genius-english-translations-kairiki-bear-ruma-ft-hatsune-miku-english-translation-lyrics)]
- **きゃんきゃん (Kyan-kyan):** Mimics an unsteady human stumbling around (_furafurari_), but explicitly translates to the yelping sound a small dog makes when it is hurt or stepped on. \[[1](https://www.smule.com/song/kairiki-bear-%E3%83%AB%E3%83%9E-luma-2-romaji-karaoke-lyrics/10834052%5F10834052/arrangement)]

***

4\. The Visual Cross-Mark Puns

Kairiki Bear frequently uses the word **×点 (Batten)**, which means a "failing grade" or "cross mark" on a test paper. \[[1](https://w.atwiki.jp/hmiku/pages/40213.html)]

- In Japanese, a perfect score is marked with a circle (**○ - Maru**).
- An incorrect score is marked with an **X (× - Batten)**.
- By spelling "Maru" backward to create **"Ruma,"** the title itself visually turns a perfect circle into an intersecting "X." The lyrics _"devour the bad marks"_ and _"laugh the bad marks down"_ mean the protagonist is actively chewing up and mocking the physical "X" marks society brands them with. \[[1](https://w.atwiki.jp/hmiku/pages/40213.html), [2](https://genius.com/Genius-english-translations-kairiki-bear-ruma-ft-hatsune-miku-english-translation-lyrics)]
