While <mark style="border-radius: 4px; background-size: 200% 100%; padding: 0px 2px; animation: 0.75s cubic-bezier(0.05, 0.7, 0.1, 1) 0.25s 1 normal forwards running highlight-animation;"><strong>again</strong> and <strong>once again</strong> are often interchangeable, they differ in formality, emphasis, and the specific nuance of the repetition</mark>.

Key Differences at a Glance

| Feature       | Again                                  | Once Again                                              |
| ------------- | -------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Formality** | Casual and everyday.                   | More formal and literary.                               |
| **Emphasis**  | Neutral repetition.                    | Stronger emphasis on the fact it's happening _again_.   |
| **Tone**      | Objective.                             | Can imply frustration, sarcasm, or "as expected."       |
| **Frequency** | Can refer to a 2nd, 3rd, or 10th time. | Often implies "one more time" or a return to a pattern. |

***

Usage Breakdown

- **Again**: The standard way to describe any repeated action. Use it when the number of times doesn't matter or when speaking casually.
  - _Example:_ "Can you say that **again**? I didn't hear you."
  - _Example:_ "I'm going to the store **again**."
- **Once Again**: Adds "weight" to the repetition. It is frequently used to highlight a predictable outcome or to express mild annoyance.
  - **As expected:** "**Once again**, the train was late." (Implies this is a recurring problem).
  - **Formal announcement:** "**Once again**, we are happy to announce our new winners."
  - **Sarcasm/Frustration:** "He forgot his keys **once again**."

Common Phrasal Variations

- **Yet again**: Adds even more emphasis, often used when something is excessive or very annoying (e.g., "He failed **yet again**").
- **Time and again** / **Again and again**: Used specifically to mean "many times" or "repeatedly."
- **Once more**: Very similar to "once again" but often considered slightly more poetic or focused on a single final repetition.
