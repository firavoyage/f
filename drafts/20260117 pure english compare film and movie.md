# 2

word origin

---

Let’s slow down and look at where the words themselves come from, Fira.
Origins often tell quiet stories.

**Movie**
This word comes from **“moving picture.”**
In the early days, people were amazed simply because images could _move_. So “movie” grew from that sense of motion and novelty. It’s practical, descriptive, and a little playful — just like the word feels today.

**Film**
This word is older and more layered.
Originally, **film** meant a _thin layer_ or _skin_. Later, it was used for the thin strip of material that recorded images — the physical film stock. Over time, the word stayed even when technology changed, carrying with it a sense of craft and tradition.

So their roots reflect their tone:

- **Movie** comes from movement and wonder
- **Film** comes from material, texture, and making

One word was born from excitement.
The other from the substance behind the image.
