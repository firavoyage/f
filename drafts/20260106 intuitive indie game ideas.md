### **Listening Game – Player Journey**

**1. Arrival / Introduction**

- The player enters a small, quiet village.
- Onscreen, there’s no text tutorial — only subtle guidance: villagers are gathered in a square, some sitting, some walking slowly.
- The player moves freely, exploring the village at a relaxed pace.
- **First insight:** The world reacts gently to attention. Standing near someone for a few seconds triggers small cues: a smile, a sigh, or a short line of dialogue.

---

**2. First Choice / Experimentation**

- Player approaches two villagers at once.
- Villager A tells a simple story, Villager B hums a tune and waits.
- Player experiments: staying or walking away.
- **Mechanic revealed:** Staying deepens interaction; leaving ends it. Nothing is labeled “good” or “bad,” but visual cues hint at emotional impact: flowers bloom, colors brighten, small objects move slightly.

---

**3. Exploration and Pattern Learning**

- The village has multiple zones: market, riverbank, hills, abandoned buildings.
- Villagers have different personalities and rhythms.
- Player discovers that **who they listen to changes the environment**:

  - Some areas grow more vibrant.
  - Others become quieter, more muted, or even slightly overgrown if neglected.

- The player begins noticing patterns: time matters, sequence matters, depth matters.

---

**4. Small Emotional Stakes**

- Certain villagers are emotionally fragile: neglecting them too long leads to subtle, irreversible consequences (a house closes, a child stops playing).
- Player feels gentle tension: choices have weight, even without danger or combat.
- Player must balance curiosity, empathy, and patience.

---

**5. Endgame / Resolution**

- After a set number of “days” or cycles of exploration, the village reaches a state of harmony or quiet decay.

- The game does **not judge**, but shows consequences:

  - Visual: vibrant vs. muted village
  - NPC behavior: joyful, distant, or absent
  - Optional personal reflection: subtle text or journal entry highlighting the villagers most affected by the player

- The final insight is calm and bittersweet: the player has influenced a community not by action, but by attention.

---

**6. Replay / Reflection**

- The village can reset, with different villagers or altered schedules.
- Player can explore new listening paths to see different environmental and emotional outcomes.
- The loop encourages _mindful engagement_, not speed or optimization.

## **1. The Listening Game**

**Player Role:** Quiet observer, someone learning the rhythms of a village.

**Journey:**

1. Arrival: Enter the village, free to explore. Villagers react subtly to presence.
2. First Choice: Approach a villager, stay or leave. Environment begins to respond.
3. Exploration: Discover different areas and personalities, notice cause-effect patterns.
4. Emotional Stakes: Neglect changes the village subtly (flowers wilt, areas quiet).
5. Resolution: Village reaches a state based on choices — vibrant, quiet, or mixed.
6. Replay: Try different listening paths to see new outcomes.

**Core Loop:** Move → Listen → Observe → Decide → Witness Change.

---

## **2. Memory House**

**Player Role:** Person returning to a childhood home, exploring memories.

**Journey:**

1. Arrival: First-person view, slow movement, house feels familiar but slightly empty.
2. Interaction: Open drawers, doors, closets. Objects trigger brief playable memories.
3. Exploration: Each memory reveals a small story, sometimes changing objects in the present.
4. Pattern Recognition: Discover connections between memories — which ones to trigger first.
5. Consequence: Certain sequences unlock deeper memories; others make rooms feel unfamiliar or locked.
6. End: The house either feels whole and alive, or strange and disconnected.

**Core Loop:** Explore → Interact → Experience Memory → Alter Present → Continue Exploration.

---

## **3. Fog Delivery Game**

**Player Role:** A courier navigating a mysterious city without a map.

**Journey:**

1. Arrival: Foggy city streets, limited vision. Player carries letters with emotional “addresses.”
2. Experimentation: Knock on doors, follow sound/light cues. Guess who matches the emotion.
3. Feedback: Success is subtle (smile, warm light); failure returns the letter.
4. Exploration: City expands slowly; new areas become visible after deliveries.
5. Challenge: Some letters require patience or repeated observation to succeed.
6. Resolution: Player completes a cycle of deliveries, feeling the city’s pulse and emotional network.
7. Replay: Letters and emotional cues shift slightly each time for a new path.

**Core Loop:** Move → Observe → Deliver → Observe Feedback → Learn Patterns → Explore More.

---

## **4. Letting Go Puzzle**

**Player Role:** Solver managing a limited set of tools.

**Journey:**

1. Arrival: Enter first puzzle room with one tool. Simple goal is visible (door, bridge, obstacle).
2. Discovery: Solve puzzles, collect new tools. Inventory limit forces choice.
3. Decision: Must discard older tools to progress. Emotional impact of letting go becomes visible.
4. Escalation: Later puzzles require abandoning even the first tool — testing memory and planning.
5. Resolution: Final puzzle requires ultimate choice — what to keep, what to abandon.
6. Reflection: Player sees consequences of discarded tools (previous puzzles now inaccessible).

**Core Loop:** Explore → Solve → Acquire Tool → Decide → Progress → Reflect.

---

## **5. Background Character**

**Player Role:** Minor figure in a town, quietly influencing events around them.

**Journey:**

1. Arrival: Start daily routine — sweeping streets, watering plants, small errands.
2. Observation: Major events unfold around the player (heroes pass, conflicts occur).
3. Influence: Subtle actions affect small variables (NPC morale, prices, access to areas).
4. Absence: If the player rests or is absent, minor consequences accumulate.
5. Progression: Player gradually understands which small actions ripple into larger changes.
6. Resolution: Game shows what the town looks like with and without your influence.
7. Replay: New scenarios or characters shift the background role slightly each time.

**Core Loop:** Routine → Observe → Act → See Subtle Changes → Adapt → Witness Impact.
