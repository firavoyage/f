how is the 37signals company

12:45 PM

37signals is a privately held web software company known for its <mark style="margin: 0px; text-decoration-style: solid; text-decoration-color: rgb(230, 232, 240); border-bottom: 0px none rgb(230, 232, 240);">unconventional and influential approach to business, productivity, and remote work</mark>. Founded in 1999 and based in Chicago, the company has famously maintained profitability for **25 consecutive years** while operating with a relatively small team of approximately 80 employees. \[[1](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/lennyrachitsky%5F37signals-is-a-very-different-kind-of-company-activity-7142214613126840320-vo8F), [2](https://pitchbook.com/profiles/company/56325-70), [3](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/37signals), [4](https://basecamp.com/), [5](https://zhiminzhan.medium.com/37signals-remarkable-profitability-and-unique-employee-profit-sharing-model-06dd71f7c6aa), [6](https://x.com/dhh/status/1888162121474605110)]

Core Philosophy & Work Culture

The company is widely recognized for its "anti-startup" model, prioritizing sustainable profit over rapid growth and venture capital. \[[1](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/0fl5nBWZZb0), [2](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/GZOMqMN0zNU)]

- **"Calm" Work Environment**: They advocate for 40-hour work weeks, minimal meetings, and a low-distraction atmosphere.
- **Remote-First**: 37signals is a fully distributed company with employees in over 15 countries.
- **Employee Compensation**: They pay in the **top 10%** of the industry based on San Francisco salary levels, regardless of where an employee lives, and offer a $70,000 salary floor.
- **Management Style**: The company operates with a flat structure and has moved away from full-time managers, encouraging "managers of one" who can work independently. \[[1](https://37signals.com/podcast/everybody-works/), [2](https://www.glassdoor.com/Reviews/37signals-Reviews-E7262045.htm), [3](https://37signals.com/jobs), [4](https://basecamp.com/handbook/how-we-work), [5](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/consider-full-problem-37signals-e2dtc), [6](https://zhiminzhan.medium.com/interesting-comparisons-between-two-chicago-based-software-companies-thoughtworks-and-37signals-43a5dacf1bd1), [7](https://world.hey.com/dhh/we-once-more-have-no-full-time-managers-at-37signals-f8611085), [8](https://www.inc.com/magazine/20110401/jason-fried-why-i-run-a-flat-company.html)]

Major Products & Innovations

37signals focuses on simple, opinionated software that avoids "feature creep". \[[1](https://www.nateberkopec.com/blog/37signals-is-not-smarter-than-you/), [2](https://www.promptloop.com/directory/what-does-37signals-do)]

- **Basecamp**: Their flagship project management and internal communication platform.
- **HEY**: A reimagined email service designed to reduce noise and provide more user control.
- **[Ruby on Rails](https://rubyonrails.org/)**: The popular web development framework was originally created by CTO David Heinemeier Hansson (DHH) for internal use at the company before being released as open-source in 2004.
- **Books**: Founders Jason Fried and DHH have authored several best-selling business books, including _Rework_, _Remote_, and _It Doesn’t Have to Be Crazy at Work_. \[[1](https://medium.com/the-lindberg-interviews/interview-with-david-heinemeier-hansson-the-secrets-behind-37signals-success-cb20b04751e7), [2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/37signals), [3](https://www.promptloop.com/directory/what-does-37signals-do), [4](https://basecamp.com/)]

Controversies & Challenges

Despite its success, the company has faced criticism and internal upheaval: \[[1](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=35268865)]

- **Internal Discourse Policy (2021)**: After the company banned "societal and political discussions" on internal forums, approximately **one-third of the employees resigned**.
- **Public Perception**: The founders are known for being outspoken and sometimes "arrogant," which has led to both a cult-like following and vocal detractors in the tech industry.
- **Pricing Criticisms**: Some analysts argue their flat-rate pricing models can sometimes alienate very small teams. \[[1](https://paulsyng.com/blog/37signals-the-company-that-owns-control-and-doesnt-know-it/), [2](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=35268865), [3](https://www.reddit.com/r/programming/comments/6bdjh/why%5Fis%5F37signals%5Fso%5Farrogant/), [4](https://www.reddit.com/r/rails/comments/1juuyax/rare%5Fjob%5Fopening%5Ffor%5Fa%5Fjunior%5Fprogrammer%5Fat%5F37/), [5](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/37signals)]

the history of 37signals

The history of 37signals is <mark style="margin: 0px; text-decoration-style: solid; text-decoration-color: rgb(230, 232, 240); border-bottom: 0px none rgb(230, 232, 240);">a 25-year evolution from a boutique design firm into a major influencer in the software industry</mark>, marked by radical transparency and a commitment to independence. \[[1](https://paulsyng.com/blog/37signals-the-company-that-owns-control-and-doesnt-know-it/)]

1999–2003: The Web Design Era

- **Founding (1999):** The company was launched in Chicago by **Jason Fried**, **Carlos Segura**, and **Ernest Kim** as a web design agency. The name "37signals" refers to 37 extraterrestrial radio signals identified by astronomer Paul Horowitz.
- **The Manifesto:** Early on, the founders published a [37signals Manifesto](https://signalvnoise.com/posts/1125-the-early-days-how-37signals-built-buzz-out-of-the-gate) that outlined their philosophy of simplicity and user-centered design.
- **Transition:** While designing sites for clients like Panera and Gawker Media, the team found themselves increasingly disorganized and began building internal tools to manage their projects. \[[1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/37signals), [2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/37signals), [3](https://world.hey.com/jason/37signals-hello-again-117eae60), [4](https://37signals.com/podcast/whats-in-a-name/), [5](https://signalvnoise.com/posts/1125-the-early-days-how-37signals-built-buzz-out-of-the-gate), [6](https://www.taskade.com/blog/basecamp-history), [7](https://medium.com/@jasonfried/basecamp-the-origin-story-f509fdd725f8), [8](https://stackingthebricks.com/9-years-ago-37signals-had-no-products/)]

2004–2013: Software and Ruby on Rails \[[1](https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/jason-fried-37signals-makers-of-basecamp-hey-and-once/id1836497887?i=1000749812872)]

- **Basecamp Launch (2004):** Their first software product, **Basecamp**, was released in February 2004. It quickly became more profitable than their design work, prompting the company to pivot exclusively to software by 2005.
- **Ruby on Rails (2004):** CTO **David Heinemeier Hansson (DHH)** extracted the code used to build Basecamp and released it as the [Ruby on Rails](https://rubyonrails.org/foundation/37signals) open-source framework, which later powered companies like GitHub and Airbnb.
- **Investment (2006):** Jeff Bezos purchased a minority stake in the company via Bezos Expeditions, which remains the company’s only outside investment. \[[1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/37signals), [2](https://www.stacksync.com/blog/seven-years-before-slack-the-origin-story-of-campfire-37signals), [4](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/37signals), [5](https://rubyonrails.org/foundation/37signals)]

2014–2021: The "Basecamp" Rebrand \[]

- **Product Consolidation (2014):** Seeking to eliminate the "dilution" of managing multiple products, the company **renamed itself Basecamp**. They spun off or ceased signups for secondary products like Highrise, Backpack, and Campfire.
- **HEY and Return to Diversity (2020):** After years of focusing solely on Basecamp, they launched **HEY**, a radical new email service. Its success led the founders to realize they were once again a "multi-product" company. \[[1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/37signals), [2](https://world.hey.com/jason/37signals-hello-again-117eae60), [3](https://www.creativebloq.com/business/37signals-becomes-basecamp-21410584), [5](https://www.stacksync.com/blog/seven-years-before-slack-the-origin-story-of-campfire-37signals), [6](https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/jason-fried-37signals-makers-of-basecamp-hey-and-once/id1836497887?i=1000749812872)]

2022–Present: Return to 37signals \[[1](https://www.stacksync.com/blog/seven-years-before-slack-the-origin-story-of-campfire-37signals), [2](https://37signals.com/podcast/whats-in-a-name/), [3](https://37signals.com/podcast/whats-in-a-name/)]

- **Reverting Name (2022):** The company [renamed itself back to 37signals](https://world.hey.com/jason/37signals-hello-again-117eae60) to better represent its expanding product lineup.
- **ONCE (2024):** They introduced a new product line called **ONCE**, offering self-hosted, "buy once, own forever" software as an alternative to the subscription (SaaS) model. \[[1](https://37signals.com/podcast/whats-in-a-name/), [3](https://www.stacksync.com/blog/seven-years-before-slack-the-origin-story-of-campfire-37signals)]

company info

37signals is a privately held [SaaS software company](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/37signals) known for its "calm" work philosophy and for creating [Ruby on Rails](https://rubyonrails.org/foundation/37signals). Founded in 1999 and headquartered in Chicago, the company has remained remarkably lean and independent, with its only outside investment being a minority stake purchased by Jeff Bezos in 2006. \[[1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/37signals), [2](https://www.promptloop.com/directory/what-does-37signals-do), [3](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/37signals), [4](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/37signals), [5](http://taggedwiki.zubiaga.org/new%5Fcontent/d1510104d29ff7e320cde46f0c7789e3)]

Quick Facts

- **Headquarters:** [Chicago, Illinois](https://pitchbook.com/profiles/company/56325-70) (though the company operates with a [fully remote workforce](https://4dayweek.io/company/37signals)).
- **Founders:** [Jason Fried (CEO)](https://www.linkedin.com/in/jason-fried), Carlos Segura, and Ernest Kim.
- **Key Leadership:** [David Heinemeier Hansson (CTO)](https://www.datanyze.com/companies/37signals/24434238), who created Ruby on Rails while building the company's first product.
- **Company Status:** Private; has been profitable for over 25 years.
- **Employee Count:** Approximately [80 employees](https://zhiminzhan.medium.com/37signals-remarkable-profitability-and-unique-employee-profit-sharing-model-06dd71f7c6aa) (estimates vary between 51 and 200 depending on source). \[[1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/37signals), [2](https://4dayweek.io/company/37signals), [3](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/37signals), [4](https://podcasts.apple.com/ag/podcast/jason-fried-37signals-makers-of-basecamp-hey-and-once/id1836497887?i=1000749812872), [5](https://system.privco.com/company/37signals-llc)]

Financial Highlights (2024 Estimates)

While the company does not publicly disclose exact earnings, independent analysis and internal profit-sharing data suggest a highly efficient operation:

- **Estimated Revenue:** Reached roughly [$80.8 million in 2024](https://getlatka.com/companies/hey.com).
- **Profitability:** Estimated net profit exceeded $66 million in 2024, translating to over $1 million in revenue generated per employee.
- **Profit Sharing:** The company allocates 10% of its annual profits to be distributed among its staff. \[[1](https://getlatka.com/companies/hey.com), [2](https://zhiminzhan.medium.com/37signals-remarkable-profitability-and-unique-employee-profit-sharing-model-06dd71f7c6aa)]

Core Offerings

- **Basecamp:** A project management and team communication tool.
- **HEY:** A reimagined, privacy-focused email service.
- **ONCE:** A new line of [self-hosted software](https://www.davidsenra.com/episode/jason-fried) where customers pay once to own the product forever, challenging the standard SaaS subscription model. \[[1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/37signals), [2](https://icopilots.com/37signals-the-microgiant/), [3](https://www.promptloop.com/directory/what-does-37signals-do)]
