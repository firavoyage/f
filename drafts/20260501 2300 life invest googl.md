1\. Use a DEX Aggregator for Minimal Network Fees \[[1](https://www.mexc.com/news/793892)]

Aggregators automatically route your trade through multiple liquidity pools to find the best price and lowest swap fees. \[[1](https://www.mexc.com/news/793892)]

- **On Solana:** Use [Jupiter Exchange](https://jup.ag/) or [Jumper.exchange](https://jumper.exchange/). These platforms are known for extremely low transaction (gas) fees and highly optimized routing.
- **On Ethereum:** Use 1inch Network or [Uniswap](https://uniswap.org/). These aggregators help minimize the impact of high gas fees on Ethereum by finding the most efficient path. \[[1](https://www.reddit.com/r/CryptoIndia/comments/1n85ii7/how%5Fdo%5Fyou%5Fguys%5Ftradeswaps%5Fin%5Fwallets%5Fwith%5Flowest/), [2](https://www.mexc.com/news/793892), [3](https://castlerock.org/how-to-swap-tokens-on-uniswap-secure-decentralized-exchange-2/)]

- **Check Liquidity:** Low liquidity causes "slippage," where the actual price you get is worse than the quoted price. Swap on high-volume exchanges like [Gate.io](https://www.gate.io/) to minimize this.

The **Nasdaq Composite** and **Nasdaq-100** are both [market capitalization-weighted indexes](https://www.marketbeat.com/learn/nasdaq-100-vs-nasdaq-composite/) tracking companies on the [Nasdaq exchange](https://www.nasdaq.com/), but they differ significantly in scope and sector exposure. Key Comparison

- **Breadth of Holdings**
  - **Nasdaq Composite**: Tracks [more than 3,000 securities](https://www.bankrate.com/investing/what-is-nasdaq-composite/), including common stocks, ADRs, and REITs.
  - **Nasdaq-100**: Limited to the 100 largest non-financial firms. It accounts for roughly 80% of the Composite's total index weighting.
- **Sector Diversification**
  - **Nasdaq Composite**: Heavily tech-oriented but includes [financial firms, industrials, and small-caps](https://www.nasdaq.com/articles/what-is-the-nasdaq-composite-and-what-companies-are-in-it-2021-05-12).
  - **Nasdaq-100**: Completely [excludes financial companies](https://www.finder.com/uk/share-trading/invest-in/stock-market-indices/nasdaq-composite-vs-nasdaq-100) like banks and insurance firms. It is even more concentrated in large-cap technology and growth sectors.
- **Performance and Volatility**
  - **Performance**: Historically, the Nasdaq-100 has outperformed the broader Composite over the last decade, driven by the dominance of "Big Tech."
  - **Risk**: The Composite is susceptible to [small-cap volatility](https://www.stl.news/the-nasdaq-100-and-nasdaq-composite-guide/), whereas the Nasdaq-100 faces higher **concentration risk**—a major drop in a few mega-cap stocks like Apple or Microsoft can significantly impact the entire index.
- **Accessibility for Investors**
  - **Nasdaq-100**: Widely tracked by popular ETFs like the Invesco QQQ Trust (QQQ).
  - **Nasdaq Composite**: Fewer directly tracking ETFs exist, but the [Fidelity Nasdaq Composite Index ETF (ONEQ)](https://fundresearch.fidelity.com/mutual-funds/summary/315912709) is a common way to gain exposure. \[[1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nasdaq%5FComposite), [2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nasdaq%5FComposite), [3](https://www.bankrate.com/investing/what-is-nasdaq-composite/), [4](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/graph/?g=192n), [5](https://www.investopedia.com/terms/n/nasdaq100.asp)]
