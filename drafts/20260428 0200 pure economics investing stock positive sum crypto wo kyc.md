is polymarket zero sum (with slightly fee negative sum) and stock market positive sum

2:46 am

In financial theory, **Polymarket** and the **stock market** operate on fundamentally different sum-based models due to how they settle and create value.

Polymarket: Zero-Sum / Negative-Sum

Polymarket is structurally a **zero-sum game** because it is a closed system where wealth is redistributed, not created.

- **Mechanical Zero-Sum**: Every contract is fully collateralised by $1.00. For every "Yes" share that settles at $1.00, a "No" share settles at $0.00. One participant's gain is mathematically equal to another's loss.
- **Negative-Sum (After Fees)**: While some categories like **Geopolitics** are fee-free, most active markets (Crypto, Sports, Finance) charge a **taker fee**. This friction means the total pool of money returned to all participants is less than the amount put in, making it **negative-sum** for the aggregate group.
- **The "Maker" Exception**: Market makers can receive **rebates** (20-50% of taker fees), which can turn the game into a positive-sum experience for them specifically, even as it remains negative-sum for the broader market.

Stock Market: Positive-Sum

The stock market is considered a **positive-sum game** over long horizons because it is an open system linked to real-world economic growth.

- **Value Creation**: Stocks represent ownership in companies that generate profits, pay dividends, and innovate. As companies grow their earnings, the total "pie" of market capitalisation expands.
- **Wealth Accumulation**: Multiple investors can profit simultaneously from a rising market without requiring a corresponding loss from another participant.
- **Short-Term Nuance**: On short timeframes (day trading) or in derivatives (options/futures), the market behaves more like a zero-sum or negative-sum game because participants are competing for relative outperformance (alpha) and paying high transaction costs.

| Feature             | Polymarket                                   | Stock Market (Long-term)                 |
| ------------------- | -------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| **Sum Type**        | **Zero-Sum** (Negative-sum after fees)       | **Positive-Sum**                         |
| **Source of Value** | Redistribution of other participants' stakes | Corporate earnings and economic growth   |
| **Fees**            | Taker fees (up to 1.80%) on many markets     | Commissions, spreads, and expense ratios |
| **Outcome**         | One side must be wrong for the other to win  | Many can win as company valuations rise  |
