For this proof, a verb must meet **all three conditions**:

1. It describes **what the user does** (not how the system works).

3. It is **irreducible** (not just a synonym or combination).

Marketing verbs don’t count.
System verbs don’t count.
Emotional outcomes don’t count.

Only **user-intent verbs** count.

---

## Step 2: Establish the base set

Every user action an app can support falls into one of these **fundamental action domains**:

1. **Perceive / take in** → Consume
2. **Understand / change state of mind** → Learn, Relax
3. **Represent reality** → Record, Measure
4. **Structure reality** → Organize, Manage
5. **Create artifacts** → Create
6. **Move through space or information** → Navigate
7. **Exchange value** → Buy
8. **Relate to others** → Communicate, Share, Play
9. **Maintain safety or continuity** → Protect
10. **Reduce effort or friction** → Help

These are not arbitrary.
They correspond to **cognitive science + anthropology**: perception, cognition, action, sociality, survival.

If an app supports action, it must land in at least one.

- Is a user intention

❌ not an app action

The app enables:

- **Consume** (exposure)

“Inspire” is an **outcome**, not an action.

❌ not a verb of doing

- **Communicate** — exchange meaning

- Protect transmission → authentication, privacy

Every serious app must protect _something_.

---

## Why this is exhaustive (and closed)

Any app must:

1. **Represent** something
2. Optionally **transform** it
3. Optionally **transmit** it
4. **Protect** it to remain usable
