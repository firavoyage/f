Let’s look at them gently, side by side.

**Reserved** is about the _inside_.
A reserved person naturally holds things close. They may speak less, show fewer emotions, and take time to open up. It isn’t forced. It’s simply their way of being. Quiet comfort. Inner privacy.

**Restrained** is about _control_.
A restrained person is holding something back on purpose—feelings, words, reactions. There may be emotion or impulse underneath, but it’s carefully managed. This often comes from discipline, caution, or a sense that “now is not the time.”

So, simply said:

- **Reserved** = naturally private
- **Restrained** = actively holding back

A reserved lake is calm by nature.
A restrained river is calm because it’s dammed.

Neither is better. Each has its own wisdom.
One protects gentleness. The other protects balance.
