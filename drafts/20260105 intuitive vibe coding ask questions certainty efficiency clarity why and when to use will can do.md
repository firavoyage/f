Ah, Fira… Chinese handles these differently from English, and it’s helpful to approach them not just as words but as _modes of possibility or politeness_. Let’s go slowly and clearly. I’ll focus on the most natural, conversational forms.

---

### 1. **Would**

English “would” often shows politeness, hypothetical situations, or habitual past actions.

- **Polite request:** 我 **想要** (wǒ xiǎng yào) or 我 **会** (wǒ huì)

  - “I would like some tea.” → 我想要一些茶 (Wǒ xiǎng yào yìxiē chá)

- **Hypothetical:** 会 (huì) often works for “would” in “If…” statements.

  - “I would go if I had time.” → 如果我有时间，我会去 (Rúguǒ wǒ yǒu shíjiān, wǒ huì qù)

---

### 2. **Could**

English “could” expresses possibility, ability, or polite requests.

- **Ability:** 能 (néng)

  - “I could help you.” → 我能帮你 (Wǒ néng bāng nǐ)

- **Polite suggestion:** 可以 (kěyǐ)

  - “Could I open the window?” → 我可以开窗吗？(Wǒ kěyǐ kāi chuāng ma?)

---

### 3. **Might**

“Might” expresses possibility, usually uncertain.

- **Possibility:** 可能 (kěnéng)

  - “It might rain tomorrow.” → 明天可能下雨 (Míngtiān kěnéng xià yǔ)

---

### 4. **Would like to**

This is almost always translated as **想 (xiǎng)** or **想要 (xiǎng yào)**.

- “I would like to eat dumplings.” → 我想吃饺子 (Wǒ xiǎng chī jiǎozi)
- Politer: 我想要吃饺子 (Wǒ xiǎng yào chī jiǎozi)

---

### 5. **Do (modal)**

English “do” in questions is tricky in Chinese because Chinese doesn’t use auxiliary verbs like “do.” Instead:

- You just add **吗 (ma)** at the end of a statement:

  - “Do you like tea?” → 你喜欢茶吗？(Nǐ xǐhuān chá ma?)

- Or use the verb directly in a question with **不 (bù)** for yes/no:

  - “Do you eat meat?” → 你吃不吃肉？(Nǐ chī bù chī ròu?)

---

### 6. **Will**

English “will” usually shows future action. In Chinese:

- 会 (huì) for future or ability:

  - “I will come tomorrow.” → 我明天会来 (Wǒ míngtiān huì lái)

- 要 (yào) can also imply strong intention:

  - “I will go now.” → 我现在要走 (Wǒ xiànzài yào zǒu)

---

### 7. **Can**

- 能 (néng) → ability: “I can swim.” → 我能游泳 (Wǒ néng yóuyǒng)
- 可以 (kěyǐ) → permission: “Can I borrow this?” → 我可以借这个吗？(Wǒ kěyǐ jiè zhège ma?)

---

### 8. **May (permission)**

- 可以 (kěyǐ) is the standard for “may” (asking politely):

  - “May I sit here?” → 我可以坐这里吗？(Wǒ kěyǐ zuò zhèlǐ ma?)

- 允许 (yǔnxǔ) is more formal: “You are allowed to…” → 你被允许… (Nǐ bèi yǔnxǔ…)

---

### A gentle pattern to remember:

- **Ability / possibility:** 能 (néng), 会 (huì), 可能 (kěnéng)
- **Polite requests / permission / “would like”:** 想 (xiǎng), 想要 (xiǎng yào), 可以 (kěyǐ)
- **Future / intention:** 会 (huì), 要 (yào)
- **Questions:** Use **吗** or the **V + 不 + V** structure instead of “do.”
