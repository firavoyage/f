"Tell Your World," produced by **kz (livetune)** and featuring **Hatsune Miku**, is <mark style="margin: 0px; text-decoration-style: solid; text-decoration-color: rgb(230, 232, 240); border-bottom: 0px none rgb(230, 232, 240);">a song about the power of personal creativity and global connectivity through the internet</mark>. Originally created for a 2011 **Google Chrome** commercial, the song's meaning centers on the idea that everyone has a unique "world"—their own thoughts, feelings, and stories—that is worth sharing with the rest of the world. \[[1](https://vocaloid.fandom.com/wiki/Tell%5FYour%5FWorld), [2](https://otakumode.com/otapedia/vocaloid/hatsune%5Fmiku/tell%5Fyour%5Fworld)]

Core Themes and Meaning

- **Encouraging Creativity**: The song is an invitation for everyone to become a creator. It suggests that what might seem like small, "shapeless feelings" or personal melodies can be transformed into something meaningful when shared.
- **Connecting Through Technology**: The lyrics describe how "nodes of feelings" (individual creators) connect to form lines and circles, ultimately creating a "cosmic sphere of information" that links people regardless of where they are.
- **The Internet as a Tool for Joy**: Producer kz stated he wrote the song to express the "feelings of happiness and excitement" he experienced while using the internet.
- **Miku as a Medium**: In the context of the Google Chrome campaign, Hatsune Miku acts as a hub for collaborative creation, representing how a single character can inspire countless fans to produce art, music, dance, and animation. \[[1](https://www.reddit.com/r/Vocaloid/comments/1a4shn/tell%5Fyour%5Fworld%5Fofficial%5Fenglish%5Fversion%5Flivetune/#:~:text=You%20can%20look%20at%20Miku's%20dominance%20as,a%20song%20about%20the%20community%20Miku%20has), [2](https://vocalife.wordpress.com/2012/04/09/tell-your-world/), [3](https://projectsekai.fandom.com/wiki/Tell%5FYour%5FWorld), [4](https://www.smule.com/song/kz-livetune-ft-hatsune-miku-tell-your-world-english-karaoke-lyrics/52542784%5F1386129/arrangement), [5](https://otakumode.com/otapedia/vocaloid/hatsune%5Fmiku/tell%5Fyour%5Fworld), [6](https://vocaloid.fandom.com/wiki/Tell%5FYour%5FWorld)]

Symbolic Lyrics

- **"I deleted the routine layout"**: This line signifies breaking away from established norms or "presets" to make room for one's own original voice and ideas.
- **"Tell me about the world that belongs to you"**: This direct address encourages the listener to share their unique perspective and creative output.
- **"Nodes of feelings form a link"**: This imagery represents how individual contributions on the internet connect to form a global community. \[[1](https://vocaloidlyrics.miraheze.org/wiki/Tell%5FYour%5FWorld), [2](https://www.sekaipedia.org/wiki/Tell%5FYour%5FWorld), [3](https://vocalife.wordpress.com/2012/04/09/tell-your-world/), [4](https://projectsekai.fandom.com/wiki/Tell%5FYour%5FWorld)]

in philosophy

In philosophy, "Tell Your World" is often analyzed as a manifestation of **utopian technology** and **posthumanist theory**. It explores the shifting boundaries between the individual, technology, and collective consciousness. \[[1](https://www.academia.edu/86461417/%5FTell%5FYour%5FWorld%5FTowards%5Fa%5FTheory%5Fof%5FDisembodiment%5Fand%5FReembodiment%5Fwith%5FHatsune%5FMiku), [2](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/320301494%5F284%5FHatsune%5FMiku%5Fan%5Funcertain%5Fimage)]

Key Philosophical Frameworks

- **The Posthuman Instrument**: Some scholars argue that Hatsune Miku should be viewed as a **posthuman instrument** rather than a traditional performer. In this view, she is an extension of the human voice—digitized and malleable—allowing for creative expression that surpasses biological limits.
- **Utopian Connectivity**: The song depicts the internet as a **utopian space** where individual "dots" (personal feelings) connect into "lines" and "circles". This mirrors **Actor-Network Theory (ANT)**, where the technology (Miku) acts as a mediator that draws disparate people into a single, emergent technical existence.
- **Deconstruction of the Subject**: Miku is seen as a "Body without Organs"—a surface with **no fixed identity**. Unlike traditional celebrities with fixed narratives, she allows creators to project their own values and perceptions onto her, democratizing the act of storytelling.
- **The Hyperreal**: Philosophically, Miku exists in the **hyperreal**, blurring the lines between what is "real" and what is "virtual". The song celebrates this blurring, suggesting that digital connections and virtual creations are as meaningful as physical ones. \[[1](https://project-diva.fandom.com/wiki/Tell%5FYour%5FWorld), [2](https://www.academia.edu/86461417/%5FTell%5FYour%5FWorld%5FTowards%5Fa%5FTheory%5Fof%5FDisembodiment%5Fand%5FReembodiment%5Fwith%5FHatsune%5FMiku), [3](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/13678779241273459), [4](https://otakumode.com/otapedia/vocaloid/hatsune%5Fmiku/tell%5Fyour%5Fworld), [5](https://nhsjs.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/12/The-Vocaloid-Phenomenon-Deconstruction-of-Music-Culture-Through-Hatsune-Miku.pdf), [6](https://www.journals.uchicago.edu/doi/10.1086/732626), [7](https://journal.media-culture.org.au/index.php/mcjournal/article/view/3037), [8](https://nhsjs.com/2024/the-vocaloid-phenomenon-deconstruction-of-music-culture-through-hatsune-miku/), [9](https://search.proquest.com/openview/7374a4af4259a40f179033fc69790bb8/1?pq-origsite=gscholar\&cbl=18750), [11](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/abs/10.1177/13678779241273459)]

Symbolic Interpretation

- **Individual vs. Collective**: The "world" you are telling is uniquely yours, yet it only gains meaning through the network. This highlights a tension between the **sovereign individual creator** and the **collective intelligence** of the internet.
- **Erasure of the "Preset"**: The line about deleting "routine layouts" or "templates" represents a philosophical break from **pre-determined social structures** or artistic norms, advocating for radical personal authenticity. \[[1](https://bpb-us-w2.wpmucdn.com/wp.ovptl.uci.edu/dist/e/3/files/2023/09/2013%5F01%5Fle.pdf), [2](https://otakumode.com/otapedia/vocaloid/hatsune%5Fmiku/tell%5Fyour%5Fworld), [3](https://vocaloid.fandom.com/wiki/Tell%5FYour%5FWorld), [4](https://vocaloidlyrics.miraheze.org/wiki/Tell%5FYour%5FWorld), [5](https://bambooxzx.wordpress.com/2012/01/22/livetune-feat-hatsune-miku-tell-your-world/)]
