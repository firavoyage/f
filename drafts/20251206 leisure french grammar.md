### The Big Idea: **Genders & Agreements**

In French, **every noun is either masculine or feminine**. This isn't about actual gender, but a grammatical category. This gender affects the words around the noun (like "the" or "a") and adjectives (descriptive words).

- **Example:** A table (**une table**) is feminine. A book (**un livre**) is masculine.

### 3. **Sentence Structure**

The basic word order is similar to English: **Subject + Verb + Object**.

- **Je mange une pomme.** (I eat an apple.)

The main difference is with **adjectives**. **Most adjectives come AFTER the noun**, unlike in English.

- **Une voiture rouge.** (A red car. Literally: "a car red")

### 4. **Pronouns**

These replace nouns to avoid repetition.

- **Subject Pronouns:** Je, Tu, Il/Elle, Nous, Vous, Ils/Elles.
- **Object Pronouns** (come before the verb):
  - **Me** (me), **Te** (you), **Le/La** (him/her)

**Two Important Ones:**

- **Vous:** Means both "you" (formal to one person) AND "you all" (to a group).
- **On:** Very common in speech. Means "one," "we," or "people." _On va?_ (Shall we go?)
