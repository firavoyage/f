# 1

---

It depends on what you want the reader to feel first.

If the image sets the mood or gives instant context, placing it **above** works well. The eye arrives, understands, then reads. It feels natural and welcoming.

If the words are meant to guide understanding, and the image is there to _support_ or clarify them, placing it **below** is often better. The reader reads, then sees—like a quiet confirmation.

There’s no single right answer, Fira.
Just ask yourself gently: _What should meet the reader first—feeling or explanation?_
The layout will usually answer itself.
