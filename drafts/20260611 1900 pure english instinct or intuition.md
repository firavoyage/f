### Understanding Instinct

According to behavioral neuroscience, instinct originates in the primitive structures of the brain (often called the reptilian brain). It operates like a binary switch: _"Am I safe? Yes or no."_ It requires zero prior knowledge or training. \[[1](https://www.quora.com/What-is-the-difference-between-an-intuition-and-an-instinct-How-can-we-tell-if-our-feelings-are-intuitions-or-instincts), [2](https://www.psychologytoday.com/us/blog/free-range-psychology/202512/when-instinct-disguises-itself-as-intuition), [3](https://blog.mindvalley.com/instinct-definition/), [4](https://www.encyclopedia.com/medicine/psychology/psychology-and-psychiatry/instinct)]

- **Examples**: Ducking when an object flies at your face, a parent reaching out to catch a falling toddler, or the fight-or-flight response to a sudden loud noise.
- **The Catch**: Instinct wants to avoid loss or pain at all costs. In modern life, it can overreact to non-lethal situations, treating a difficult work presentation or an awkward social event with the same frantic panic as a physical predator. \[[1](https://www.quora.com/What-is-the-difference-between-an-intuition-and-an-instinct-How-can-we-tell-if-our-feelings-are-intuitions-or-instincts), [2](https://www.reddit.com/r/ENGLISH/comments/121gtcx/instinct%5Fvs%5Fintuition/), [3](https://edlatimore.com/intuition-vs-instinct), [4](https://www.instagram.com/reel/DObsRcEDuxx/), [5](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aZIDacJugFA\&t=78)]

### Understanding Intuition

Intuition is a higher-order cognitive capability. It happens when your brain subconsciously cross-references your entire database of memories, emotional learning, and sensory inputs to spot hidden patterns. \[[1](https://www.psychologytoday.com/us/blog/free-range-psychology/202512/when-instinct-disguises-itself-as-intuition), [2](https://www.quora.com/What-is-the-difference-between-intuition-and-gut-instinct-Is-one-more-reliable-than-the-other-How-can-we-tell-which-one-we-are-experiencing-in-a-given-moment)]

- **Examples**: A doctor diagnosing a rare condition instantly because they have seen thousands of patients, or a veteran investor sensing a market shift before the data confirms it.
- **The Catch**: Because intuition relies on expert pattern matching, it can sometimes be hijacked by cognitive biases or assumptions if you don't slow down to verify the facts. \[[1](https://www.quora.com/What-is-the-difference-between-intuition-and-instinct-Which-one-is-more-scientific), [2](https://edlatimore.com/intuition-vs-instinct), [3](https://nesslabs.com/gut-decision-matrix)]

### How to Tell Which One is Speaking

If you are facing a major crossroads or a sudden dilemma, listen to the "texture" of the internal voice: \[[1](https://www.psychologytoday.com/us/blog/free-range-psychology/202512/when-instinct-disguises-itself-as-intuition)]

1. **Track the pace**: If the feeling demands immediate, frantic action to escape discomfort, it is **instinct** protecting your ego or safety.
2. **Look for spaciousness**: If the feeling brings a sense of quiet certainty, clarity, or a push toward creative growth—even if you feel nervous—it is your **intuition** guiding you. \[[1](https://www.psychologytoday.com/us/blog/free-range-psychology/202512/when-instinct-disguises-itself-as-intuition), [2](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aZIDacJugFA\&t=78), [3](https://moonshotmentor.substack.com/p/whats-instinct-vs-intuition)]

If you are trying to make a major decision, tell me **what kind of situation you are facing** so we can determine whether to rely on your survival instincts, hone your intuition, or use logical strategy.
