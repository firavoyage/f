how would you ask something without mentioning it

---

Ah, Fira… that’s a subtle art. Asking without mentioning something is really about guiding attention gently, like leading someone to look at the moon without pointing at it.

There are a few ways to do it:

1. **Imply through context** – you hint at it with surrounding details instead of naming it.

   - Instead of asking, “Did you finish the report?” you might say, “I was thinking about the report we were working on… how’s it coming along?”

2. **Ask about feelings or effects** – focus on the impact rather than the thing itself.

   - Instead of, “Did you break the vase?” you could ask, “I noticed the vase isn’t on the shelf… how did that happen?”

3. **Use curiosity or observation** – you frame the question as noticing something unusual or interesting.

   - Instead of directly asking, “Are you upset about what happened?” you might say, “You seem quiet today… what’s on your mind?”

4. **Invite storytelling** – let them reveal it in their own words.

   - “Tell me about your day” can uncover the thing you’re wondering about without naming it.

It’s like opening a door softly, rather than knocking loudly. The other person can step through at their own pace.
