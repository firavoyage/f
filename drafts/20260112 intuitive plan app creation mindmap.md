# 3

How might a small team change.

---

In a small team, the roles and responsibilities often overlap, and individuals may wear multiple hats. Here's how the structure of app creation might change when you're working with fewer people, while still covering the key tasks:

### 1. **Idea and Conceptualization**

- **Roles**:

  - **Product Manager (PM)**: Often the sole person driving the vision, understanding the market, and gathering feedback from stakeholders.
  - **Designer/UX/UI Specialist**: Can assist in defining the user experience early on.

- **Deliverables**:

  - **Problem Statement**
  - **Business Case**
  - **Initial Feature List**
  - **Feasibility Report**
  - Sometimes these will be sketched out collaboratively as opposed to distinct, formal reports.

### 2. **Planning and Design**

- **Roles**:

  - **Product Manager**: Still owns the project vision but is also more hands-on with documenting requirements.
  - **Designer/Developer (Full-Stack)**: May handle both the design (UI/UX) and the development of the front end. In smaller teams, a single person can design and code.

- **Deliverables**:

  - **User Stories/Requirements Document**
  - **Wireframes & Prototypes**
  - **System Architecture (Simplified)**: Often just basic diagrams and a lean tech stack to keep things manageable.
  - **Project Roadmap**: May be more flexible and subject to adjustments as the team evolves.

### 3. **Development**

- **Roles**:

  - **Full-Stack Developer**: One person might take responsibility for both front-end and back-end development. This person needs to be well-versed in both.
  - **Designer/Developer**: The person handling both design and front-end development may continue to handle the entire user interface.
  - **QA (might not be separate)**: In small teams, the developer often handles testing, or it may be an informal process.

- **Deliverables**:

  - **Complete Codebase**: Full-stack development from front-end to back-end
  - **Integrated System**
  - **Test Reports**: Usually limited to unit tests and integration testing, as there's less resource to handle extensive QA.
  - **Deployment Scripts**: All code is deployed by a single or small group, streamlining the process.

### 4. **Deployment**

- **Roles**:

  - **Developer (Full-Stack/DevOps)**: This person may handle both the deployment of the app and maintaining its infrastructure.
  - **Product Manager**: Can still oversee the release process and handle communications.

- **Deliverables**:

  - **Deployment Plan**: Less complex, often just a manual or semi-automated process
  - **App Store Submission**: A single person handles this if it‚Äôs a mobile app
  - **Production Environment Setup**: A lean setup that‚Äôs simple but functional, often just using cloud services with easy scaling.

### 5. **Marketing and User Engagement**

- **Roles**:

  - **Product Manager**: Plays a central role in marketing strategy, because there may not be a dedicated marketing team.
  - **Designer**: Handles visual assets like promotional material, banners, etc.
  - **Developer**: Sometimes involved in marketing features like in-app banners or integrated social media sharing.

- **Deliverables**:

  - **Marketing Campaign**: Often grassroots‚Äîusing social media, blogs, and personal networks
  - **Onboarding Flow**: Built into the app by the developer
  - **User Retention Strategies**: Simple in-app notifications or email campaigns, usually done by the product manager.

### 6. **Maintenance and Updates**

- **Roles**:

  - **Full-Stack Developer**: Regular maintenance, bug fixes, and improvements are handled by this person.
  - **Product Manager**: Decides on priorities based on user feedback.

- **Deliverables**:

  - **Bug Fixes**: Regular patching, often reactive based on user reports.
  - **Performance Optimizations**: Smaller-scale fixes and optimizations based on need.
  - **Feature Updates**: Updates are often rolled out in batches to reduce overhead.

### 7. **Feedback and Iteration**

- **Roles**:

  - **Product Manager**: Gathers and analyzes feedback, decides on changes, and drives iteration.
  - **Developer/Designer**: Might both be responsible for incorporating feedback directly into the app.

- **Deliverables**:

  - **User Feedback**: Simple surveys or customer support channels.
  - **Usage Analytics**: Using simple tools like Google Analytics or Firebase to track user behavior.
  - **Iteration Plan**: Can be informal but focused on high-priority feedback.

---

### Key Adjustments in a Small Team:

- **More Overlap**: One person often wears multiple hats‚Äîdesigning and coding, developing and deploying.
- **Lean Documentation**: Documentation is often less formal, but still focuses on key deliverables like wireframes, user stories, and test cases.
- **Agility**: A smaller team can pivot faster and may not follow strict formalities in each phase.
- **More Communication**: Everyone has to be in sync, as fewer people means more direct collaboration, with fewer handoffs between departments.
