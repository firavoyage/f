google dont be evil

<mark style="border-radius: 4px; background-size: 200% 100%; padding: 0px 2px; animation: 0.75s cubic-bezier(0.05, 0.7, 0.1, 1) 0.25s 1 normal forwards running highlight-animation;"><span>"Don't be evil" is the famous former motto of Google</span></mark>, first introduced into its corporate code of conduct around 2000. While it became a symbol of the company's early idealistic culture, its prominence has been significantly reduced in recent years.

History and Evolution

- **Origin**: The phrase was suggested by an engineer in an early meeting to reflect the company's values, specifically the idea of prioritizing users and long-term good over short-term financial gains.
- **IPO Manifesto**: It was prominently featured in Google's 2004 IPO prospectus as a commitment to unbiased search results and transparent advertising.
- **Shift to Alphabet**: When Google reorganized under the parent company [Alphabet Inc.](https://abc.xyz/) in 2015, Alphabet adopted the motto "**Do the right thing**".
- **Demotion in 2018**: In early 2018, Google removed the motto from the preface of its Code of Conduct, moving it to the very last sentence: _"And remember… don’t be evil, and if you see something that you think isn’t right – speak up!"_.

Criticism and Controversies

The motto is frequently cited by critics who argue Google's actions have contradicted its original philosophy:

- **Privacy and Surveillance**: Changes in 2012 to track users across all services prompted widespread concerns about data mining and surveillance capitalism.

- **Military Contracts**: Involvement in projects like [Project Maven](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Side%5Fproject%5Ftime) (AI for the military) led to mass employee protests and resignations, with many claiming such work was "doing evil".

- **Antitrust and Censorship**: Allegations of monopolistic practices, search manipulation, and efforts to develop censored search engines for markets like China (Project Dragonfly) have been used to mock the motto.

- **Retaliation Lawsuits**: In 2021, former employees sued Google, claiming their firing was retaliation for organizing against "evil" projects in accordance with the company's own code.
