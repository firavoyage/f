gboard suggestion strip meaning

The **Gboard suggestion strip** is <mark style="margin: 0px; text-decoration-style: solid; text-decoration-color: rgb(230, 232, 240); border-bottom: 0px none rgb(230, 232, 240);">the horizontal bar located directly above the letter keys on Google’s keyboard app (<span style="margin: 0px; text-decoration-style: solid; text-decoration-color: rgb(230, 232, 240); border-bottom: 0px none rgb(230, 232, 240);"><a rel="noopener" href="https://support.google.com/gboard/answer/7068415?hl=en&amp;co=GENIE.Platform%3DAndroid&amp;gl=us" style="margin: 0px; text-decoration-thickness: 1px; text-decoration-style: solid; text-decoration-color: rgb(153, 195, 255); border-bottom: 0px none rgb(153, 195, 255);">Gboard</a></span>)</mark>. Its primary purpose is to speed up typing and reduce spelling errors. \[[1](https://www.yahoo.com/tech/disable-androids-predictive-text-feature-004210464.html), [2](https://www.androidpolice.com/i-turned-off-these-gboard-features-typing-feels-better/), [3](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=%5FeFjGGxWaxI)]

Core Functions

- **Predictive Text**: As you type, Gboard predicts the current word you are spelling or the next word you might want to use, allowing you to tap the word instead of typing it out fully.
- **Autocorrect Previews**: It displays real-time spelling corrections. Tapping the middle word usually accepts the corrected spelling.
- **Quick Tools & Features**: When you are not actively typing, this strip transforms into a shortcut menu providing fast access to emojis, clipboard history, settings, one-handed mode, and voice typing.
- **Smart Shortcuts**: It dynamically surfaces relevant text shortcuts, such as quick punctuation (like `?`, `!`, `;`) or even your email address when it detects a login field. \[[1](https://www.reddit.com/r/gboard/comments/x9egr9/punctuation%5Fin%5Fsuggestion%5Fstrip/), [2](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dQmvKcB2FUk), [3](https://android.stackexchange.com/questions/166498/get-rid-of-setting-bar-above-gboard-keyboard), [4](https://support.google.com/pixelphone/thread/377891271/predictive-text-quit-working-standard-gboard?hl=en\&gl=us), [5](https://www.androidpolice.com/i-turned-off-these-gboard-features-typing-feels-better/), [6](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=%5FeFjGGxWaxI), [7](https://www.yahoo.com/tech/disable-androids-predictive-text-feature-004210464.html)]

Turning the Gboard suggestion strip on or off directly changes your typing speed, screen space, and access to keyboard tools.

That makes perfect sense! You ran into what lexicographers call **homographs**—words that are spelled exactly the same but have completely different origins and meanings.

Because they aren't related, dictionaries split them into entirely separate numbered entries (like **Strip¹** and **Strip²**) rather than grouping them under one definition.

Why you had to scroll

- **Entry 1 (The Verb)**: Usually comes first. It comes from an old Germanic word meaning "to strip bare" or "to peel." The slang noun for dancing evolved directly from this verb, so it gets grouped here.
- **Entry 2 (The Noun)**: Usually comes second. It comes from a completely different historical root meaning a "shred" or "small piece." This is where your "long narrow piece" and the "Gboard suggestion strip" live.

When a word has multiple entries like that, dictionary apps can be really bad at showing you that a second entry even exists until you scroll all the way past the first one.
