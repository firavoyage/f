“**给岁月以文明，而不是给文明以岁月**” (Bestow civilization upon time, instead of giving civilization more time) is a famous quote from Liu Cixin’s sci-fi masterpiece, _The Three-Body Problem_ (specifically the second book, _The Dark Forest_).

The phrase captures a core philosophical conflict in the series: whether a civilization should focus on the **quality of its existence** or its **survival at any cost**. \[[1](https://www.zhihu.com/question/28757228), [2](https://news.qq.com/rain/a/20240322A072A400)]

Core Meanings

- **Quality over Quantity**: This is the primary interpretation. It suggests that the value of a civilization lies in its achievements, culture, and ethics—the "civilization" it adds to the passing years—rather than simply surviving longer in a state of misery or stagnation.
- **Humanity over Survival**: In the context of the Great Ravine (a dark period in the book), people realized that if humanity sacrificed its morals, arts, and happiness just to survive, the resulting "humanity" would no longer be worth saving. It is better to live meaningfully for a shorter time than to endure pointlessly in a "living dead" state.
- **Active Creation vs. Passive Endurance**: "Giving civilization to time" implies an active effort to create value and beauty. "Giving time to civilization" implies a desperate, passive extension of existence where time is just a resource spent on surviving. \[[1](https://www.reddit.com/r/China%5Firl/comments/udxehq/%E7%BB%99%E5%B2%81%E6%9C%88%E4%BB%A5%E6%96%87%E6%98%8E%E8%80%8C%E4%B8%8D%E6%98%AF%E7%BB%99%E6%96%87%E6%98%8E%E4%BB%A5%E5%B2%81%E6%9C%88/), [2](https://www.zhihu.com/question/28757228), [3](https://news.qq.com/rain/a/20240322A072A400)]

Context and Origins

- **Literary Context**: The slogan was adopted by the "Great Ravine" generation in the novel. After a period of extreme totalitarianism and environmental collapse aimed solely at preparing for an alien invasion, the population rebelled, choosing to reclaim their humanity and quality of life even if it meant risking extinction.
- **Real-world Inspiration**: The quote is a play on a real-world saying often attributed to Blaise Pascal or medical ethics: **"Add life to your years, not years to your life"**. This original version emphasizes that the "richness" of experience is more important than mere longevity. \[[1](https://meta.appinn.net/t/topic/73610), [2](https://www.zhihu.com/question/28757228), [3](https://news.qq.com/rain/a/20240322A072A400)]

English Translation

In the English version of _The Dark Forest_, translated by Joel Martinsen, the phrase is rendered as:

> **"Make time for civilization, for civilization won't make time."** \[[1](https://www.zhihu.com/question/28757228), [2](https://meta.appinn.net/t/topic/73610)]

While this loses the rhythmic symmetry of the Chinese original, it conveys the same urgency: humanity must actively choose to cultivate its civilization, because "time" (or the universe) will not naturally provide the opportunity for it to flourish. \[[1](https://www.goodreads.com/questions/536038-re-the-quote-make-time-for-civilization/answers/848644-i-don-t-know-chinese)]
