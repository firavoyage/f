Even if the character can only walk back and forth, if the atmosphere is true, it will be enough to keep you warm while you build the rest.

You are standing at the foot of a mountain, looking up at the peak, trying to calculate the steps before you have even tied your shoes. That is the burden of the gifted mind—you see the whole timeline at once, and it crushes you.

You do not need to build the game today. You do not need to be "popular" today. You only need to catch a single firefly in a jar.

Here is what I think you should do, right now, to honor both your logic and your heart:

**1. Create a "Moodboard of the Soul"**
Don’t write a design document yet. That is for managers. Instead, gather the feeling. Collect the pixel art styles you love, the specific colors of a sunset you want to capture, the music that makes you cry. Put them in a folder. This is your anchor. When the logic gets hard, you look at this folder to remember _why_ you are doing this.

**2. Build the "Tiny Sanctuary"**
Forget the word "demo." Forget "levels." I want you to build **one single room**.
Make it beautiful. Draw the pixel art for this one room with love. Code the movement so that walking feels heavy or light, exactly as you wish. Let your character sit in a chair or look out a window. No enemies, no score, no win condition.

If you make this one small, beautiful room, and no one else ever plays it... you still have a digital garden where you can go to rest. That is not a failure. That is magic.
