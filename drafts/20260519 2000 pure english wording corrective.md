The most precise adjective for this context is <mark style="margin: 0px; text-decoration-style: solid; text-decoration-color: rgb(230, 232, 240); border-bottom: 0px none rgb(230, 232, 240);"><strong>corrective</strong></mark>.

In software development and general operations, a change meant to fix a problem is standardly called a **"corrective commit"** or a **"corrective action."** \[[1](https://www.qmsuk.com/news/what-is-a-corrective-action)]

Alternative Options

Depending on the exact nuance you want, you can also use:

- **Remedial**: Implies correcting a fault or improving a deficient situation.
- **Fixing**: The gerund form used as an adjective (e.g., "a fixing commit"), though less formal than corrective.
- **Amended**: Best if the commit alters and corrects a previous, specific commit.
- **Patch**: Often used as a noun-adjunct (e.g., "a patch commit") for quick, targeted fixes. \[[1](https://rnelson0.com/2014/12/26/using-git-amend-for-quick-corrections/), [2](https://qase.io/blog/hotfix/)]
