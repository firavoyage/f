what does a proof of concept mean

---

A proof of concept is a small, simple test to see if an idea actually works in real life.

It’s not the final product. It’s not polished. It’s not perfect.
It’s just a way of answering one honest question:

“Can this idea truly work?”

Imagine you have an idea for a new app. Before spending months building it, you create a very basic version — maybe something rough and incomplete — just to see if the main function works. If it does, that’s your proof of concept.

Or think of it like planting a seed in a small pot before planting a whole field. You’re checking if it can grow at all.

A proof of concept is about reducing risk. It helps people avoid investing too much time, money, or energy into something that might not be possible.

It’s a quiet, practical step between dreaming and building.
