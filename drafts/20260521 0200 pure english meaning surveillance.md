surveillance meaning

**Surveillance** is <mark style="margin: 0px; text-decoration-style: solid; text-decoration-color: rgb(230, 232, 240); border-bottom: 0px none rgb(230, 232, 240);">the <strong>close, systematic observation and monitoring</strong> of a person, group, population, or location to gather information, manage data, or detect specific activities</mark>. \[[1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Surveillance)]

Core Contexts & Common Uses

- **Security & Law Enforcement**: The careful watching of suspects or places to prevent or investigate crimes. Examples include [police stake-outs](https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/surveillance) and CCTV camera networks.
- **Public Health**: The ongoing collection and analysis of health data to monitor disease outbreaks or track patient conditions without active treatment.
- **Digital & Electronic**: Tracking online data, tracking geolocations, monitoring phone lines, or managing internet traffic. \[[1](https://dictionary.cambridge.org/us/dictionary/english/surveillance), [2](https://www.britannica.com/dictionary/surveillance), [3](https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/surveillance), [4](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Closed-circuit%5Ftelevision), [5](https://www.cancer.gov/publications/dictionaries/cancer-terms/def/surveillance), [6](https://www.cancer.gov/publications/dictionaries/genetics-dictionary/def/surveillance)]

Standard Types

- **Overt Surveillance**: Monitoring conducted openly with the clear knowledge of those being watched, such as visible security guards or public signs.
- **Covert Surveillance**: Monitoring conducted secretly without the knowledge of the target, such as hidden microphones or undercover operations. \[[1](https://dictionary.nolo.com/surveillance-term.html), [2](https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/surveillance)]

Language & Origin

- **Word Root**: The term comes from the French word _surveiller_, meaning "to watch over".
- **Synonyms**: Observation, scrutiny, supervision, vigilance, and monitoring. \[[1](https://www.merriam-webster.com/thesaurus/surveillance), [2](https://www.etymonline.com/word/surveillance), [3](https://www.thefreedictionary.com/surveillance), [4](https://www.collinsdictionary.com/us/dictionary/english/surveillance), [5](https://www.thesaurus.com/browse/surveillance)]
