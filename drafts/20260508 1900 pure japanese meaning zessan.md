绝赞 jp meaning

The Japanese term **绝赞** (read as **Zessan** / ぜっさん) literally translates to **"high praise," "rave reviews,"** or **"great admiration"**. \[[1](http://www.romajidesu.com/dictionary/meaning-of-%E7%B5%B6%E8%B3%9B.html), [2](https://www.nihongomaster.com/japanese/dictionary/word/69110/zessan-%E7%B5%B6%E8%AE%83-%E7%B5%B6%E8%B3%9B-%E3%81%9C%E3%81%A3%E3%81%95%E3%82%93)]

In modern usage, especially in subculture and marketing, it has two primary layers:

- **Rave Reviews / Acclaim**: It is frequently used in advertising to say a product is "receiving critical acclaim". For example, **絶賛発売中** (Zessan hatsubai-chū) means "now on sale to rave reviews".
- **Emphasis (Slang/Jocular)**: It is often used as a "snowclone" (a flexible template phrase) like **绝赞……中** to emphasize that something is happening intensely or right now.
  - **絶賛公開中** (Zessan kōkai-chū): Currently in theaters (playing now).
  - **絶賛放置中** (Zessan hōchi-chū): Currently being ignored/neglected (humorous slang).
  - **絶賛残業中** (Zessan zangyō-chū): Currently (unhappily) working overtime. \[[1](https://jisho.org/search/%E7%B5%B6%E8%B3%9B), [2](https://mazii.net/ja-JP/search/word/jaen/%25E7%25B5%25B6%25E8%25B3%259B), [3](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E7%B5%B6%E8%B3%9B), [4](https://japanese.stackexchange.com/questions/56098/what-does-%E7%B5%B6%E8%B3%9B-in-%E7%B5%B6%E8%B3%9B%E4%BB%95%E4%B8%8A%E3%81%92%E4%B8%AD-mean)]

Essentially, while it formally means "ultimate praise," in casual contexts it often just acts as an **intensifier** for whatever action is currently in progress. \[[1](https://jisho.org/search/%E7%B5%B6%E8%B3%9B), [2](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E7%B5%B6%E8%B3%9B)]
