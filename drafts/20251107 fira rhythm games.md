list rhythm games

Of course! Rhythm games are a diverse genre, spanning from classic arcade titles to unique indie experiences. Here is a comprehensive list of rhythm games, categorized for clarity.

### Mainstream & Arcade Classics

These are the games that often define the genre for many people.

- **Dance Dance Revolution (DDR):** The iconic arcade game where you step on arrows to the beat. A cornerstone of the genre.
- **Guitar Hero:** The series that brought plastic instrument games to the masses. Focuses on rock and metal music.
- **Rock Band:** Similar to _Guitar Hero_ but expanded to include drums and vocals, creating a full band experience.
- **Beatmania IIDX:** A complex and highly respected arcade game from Bemani (Konami) featuring a turntable and 7-key keyboard.
- **Taiko no Tatsujin:** A colorful and energetic drumming game where you hit the drum's face and rim to the rhythm.
- **osu!:** A free-to-play PC game inspired by _Osu! Tatakae! Ouendan_. It features multiple game modes, with the primary one being a circle-clicking mode using a mouse or tablet.

### Mobile Games

Easy to pick up and play on the go.

- **Arcaea:** A 3D lane-based game with a unique "Sky/Floor" note system and a compelling story. Known for its high difficulty.
- **Cytus / Cytus II:** A popular series where you tap notes as they cross a horizontal line. _Cytus II_ has a strong narrative and a wide variety of music.
- **Deemo:** A beautiful, story-driven game from the creators of _Cytus_ where you play piano notes to make a tree grow.
- **Lanota:** A game where you rotate a wheel to align with notes, set in a world where music has been lost.
- **Phigros:** A free, non-commercial game known for its innovative "judgment lines" that move, rotate, and disappear.
- **Project Sekai: Colorful Stage! (Hatsune Miku: Colorful Stage):** A popular mobile game featuring Vocaloids, with an original story and music from popular Japanese producers.
- **Groove Coaster:** A unique "on-rails" style game where you hit notes along a winding path, mimicking a roller coaster.
- **Beatstar:** A well-known game where you tap, hold, and swipe notes as they scroll up the screen, featuring a large catalog of popular pop and rock songs.

### PC & Console Highlights

A mix of major releases and deeply influential titles.

- **A Dance of Fire and Ice (ADOFI):** A strict rhythm game where you control two orbiting planets down a single-line path. Very pure and challenging.
- **AUDIOSURF:** A classic where you "ride" your own music library on a colorful, generated track.
- **BIT.TRIP RUNNER:** A hybrid platformer/rhythm game where all the obstacles and jumps are synced to the music.
- **Crypt of the NecroDancer:** A brilliant roguelike where every move you and your enemies make must be to the beat. Its successor is _Cadence of Hyrule_.
- **DJMax Respect:** A highly regarded series on PC and consoles, known for its tight gameplay, fantastic music, and challenging 6-key and 8-key modes.
- **Everhood:** An RPG heavily inspired by _Guitar Hero_'s combat, but with a surreal and philosophical story.
- **Fuser:** A DJ game where you mix tracks from popular songs in real-time to create your own mashups.
- **Hi-Fi RUSH:** A recent hit that seamlessly blends character action combat with rhythm; all attacks, dodges, and environmental interactions are synced to the soundtrack.
- **Just Dance:** The leading dance franchise where you mimic on-screen dancers to pop hits.
- **Muse Dash:** A colorful, side-scrolling "runner" rhythm game where you hit enemies and obstacles to the music.
- **Thumper:** A "rhythm violence" game described as a "nightmarish roller coaster." It's fast, intense, and features a dark, abstract aesthetic.
- **VOEZ:** From the developers of _Cytus_ and _Deemo_, featuring lanes that shift and move dynamically with the music.

### Unique & Niche Games

These games use rhythm in innovative or unconventional ways.

- **PaRappa the Rapper:** The classic PS1 game that helped pioneer the genre with its "You gotta believe!" call-and-response gameplay.
- **Rez Infinite / Tetris Effect:** Synesthesia experiences from Tetsuya Mizuguchi where the music, visuals, and controller vibrations create a powerful rhythmic trance.
- **Rhythm Heaven (Rhythm Paradise):** A series of quirky, short minigames that test your sense of rhythm and timing with simple, repetitive inputs.
- **Sayonara Wild Hearts:** A "pop album video game" that is an interactive music album. While not a strict rhythm game, its gameplay is entirely driven by and synchronized to its incredible synth-pop soundtrack.
- **Spin Rhythm XD:** A unique game that combines a scrolling lane with a rotary dial, offering a fresh and tactile way to play.
- **Vib-Ribbon:** A classic PS1 game where the entire black-and-white world is generated from the audio of any CD you put in the console.

This list should give you a great starting point for exploring the wonderful world of rhythm games!

### Mobile (iOS / Android)

This is where the rhythm game scene is most active and accessible.

- **Project Sekai: Colorful Stage! (feat. Hatsune Miku)**

  - **What it is:** A top-tier mobile rhythm game featuring Vocaloids. It follows the "lanes with falling notes" formula but adds flick and hold notes.
  - **Why play it:** Huge selection of Vocaloid and Japanese pop songs, high-quality 3D music videos, a deep gacha system for cards and costumes, and multiple engaging stories.
  - **Similar Games:** _BanG Dream! Girls Band Party_, _D4DJ Groovy Mix_, _Love Live! School Idol Festival_.

- **Arcaea**

  - **What it is:** A 4-key lane game with a revolutionary twist: a third dimension. Notes come on the floor _and_ in the sky, tracked by a separate "trace" note.
  - **Why play it:** Extremely high skill ceiling, stunning abstract visuals, a fantastic original soundtrack (OST) focusing on electronic and hardcore genres, and a compelling partner/score system.

- **Muse Dash**

  - **What it is:** A 2-lane runner where you control two characters, one attacking notes on the top lane and one on the bottom. It's deceptively simple but becomes incredibly fast and chaotic.
  - **Why play it:** Very affordable (buy once, get all base songs), incredibly cute and vibrant art style, and a huge library of music through DLC, mostly from Asian indie artists.

- **Cytus / Cytus II**

  - **What it is:** A classic by Rayark Games. You tap notes as a scanning line passes over them. Cytus II adds a deep social media-style story and multiple in-game artists with distinct styles.
  - **Why play it:** Pioneering gameplay, incredible and diverse soundtrack, and (for Cytus II) a very immersive and well-told story.

- **Deemo**

  - **What it is:** Also from Rayark, this is a vertical falling-note game played on a piano keyboard layout. It's known for its beautiful, melancholic story and piano-heavy soundtrack.
  - **Why play it:** A touching narrative, beautiful piano music, and a more relaxed, atmospheric vibe than many other games on this list.

- **Phigros**
  - **What it is:** A free, non-commercial game with a unique "judgment line" that moves, rotates, and disappears. The number of lanes and note types are dynamic.
  - **Why play it:** Completely free (no ads, no energy), incredibly creative and unpredictable charting, and a fantastic collection of original songs.

---

### PC & Console

These range from hardcore challenges to unique experiences.

- **osu!**

  - **What it is:** The quintessential free PC rhythm game. Based on _Osu! Tatakae! Ouendan_, you click, drag, and spin circles to the beat of any song you can imagine.
  - **Why play it:** An absolutely limitless library of community-created maps for virtually any song. It's free, has multiple game modes (osu!standard, Taiko, Catch the Beat, Mania), and a massive competitive scene.

- **DJMax Respect V**

  - **What it is:** The definitive version of the classic "keyboardmania" style game. Notes fall vertically on 4 to 8 lanes, and you must hit them in sync.
  - **Why play it:** Incredibly polished, a massive library of licensed K-Pop, J-Pop, and electronic music, and a very satisfying skill progression.

- **Beat Saber (VR)**

  - **What it is:** The killer app for VR rhythm games. You slash colored blocks with corresponding lightsabers in time with the music.
  - **Why play it:** The most intuitive and physically immersive rhythm experience. Excellent for exercise and has a massive library of custom songs on PC.

- **A Dance of Fire and Ice**
