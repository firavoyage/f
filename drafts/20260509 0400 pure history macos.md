macos history

1:26 PM

The history of macOS spans over four decades, beginning with the revolutionary graphical interface of the original Macintosh and evolving into the modern, Unix-based powerhouse it is today. This evolution is generally divided into two distinct eras: the **"Classic" Mac OS** (1984–2001) and the modern **macOS** (formerly Mac OS X and OS X, 2001–present). \[[1](https://www.britannica.com/technology/macOS), [2](https://www.tataneu.com/pages/electronics/consumer-electronics/checklist-is-mac-os-based-on-linux-key-insights), [3](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MacOS%5Fversion%5Fhistory), [4](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MacOS), [5](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Classic%5FMac%5FOS)]

The Classic Era (1984–2001)

The original Macintosh software launched in 1984 as "System Software". It was the first mass-market operating system to feature a mouse-driven **Graphical User Interface (GUI)** with icons, windows, and folders. \[[1](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=%5FK5e8dJtMgE), [2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mac%5Foperating%5Fsystems), [3](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Classic%5FMac%5FOS)]

- **System 1 to 6 (1984–1988):** Focused on establishing the desktop metaphor and basic utilities. System 5 introduced "MultiFinder" for early multitasking.
- **System 7 (1991):** A major leap that introduced 32-bit addressing, color, and built-in cooperative multitasking.
- **Mac OS 8 & 9 (1997–1999):** In 1997, Apple officially rebranded the software as "Mac OS". Mac OS 9 was the final major release of the classic architecture, later dubbed the "Classic Environment" for compatibility on newer systems. \[[1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MacOS), [2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mac%5Foperating%5Fsystems), [3](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=%5FK5e8dJtMgE), [4](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mac%5Foperating%5Fsystems), [5](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Classic%5FMac%5FOS)]

The Modern Era: Mac OS X & OS X (2001–2015) \[[1](https://www.macworld.com/article/341801/macos-mac-apple-silicon-m1-influence-iphone-ios-development-apps.html), [2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Classic%5FMac%5FOS)]

Facing architectural limitations with the classic system, Apple acquired Steve Jobs's company, **NeXT**, in 1997. This brought the Unix-based **NeXTSTEP** architecture to Apple, forming the foundation of Mac OS X. \[[1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MacOS%5Fversion%5Fhistory), [2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MacOS), [3](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mac%5Foperating%5Fsystems)]

- **Big Cat Era (2001–2012):** Starting with **10.0 Cheetah**, versions were famously named after big cats. Key milestones included **10.4 Tiger** (transition to Intel processors) and **10.7 Lion** (integration of iOS-inspired features).
- **California Landmark Era (2013–2015):** Beginning with **10.9 Mavericks**, Apple shifted to naming releases after California locations and moved to a completely free upgrade model. \[[1](https://setapp.com/how-to/full-list-of-all-macos-versions), [2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MacOS%5Fversion%5Fhistory), [3](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=%5FK5e8dJtMgE)]

Standardization & Unified Branding (2016–Present)

In 2016, Apple renamed the system to **macOS** to align with iOS, tvOS, and watchOS. \[[1](https://intellipaat.com/blog/what-is-macos/), [2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MacOS)]

- **The Big Sur Shift (2020):** **macOS 11 Big Sur** marked the most significant change in decades, introducing support for **Apple silicon (M1 chips)** and shifting the major version number from 10 to 11.
- **Recent Versions:**
  - **macOS 15 Sequoia (2024):** Introduced **Apple Intelligence** and iPhone Mirroring.
  - **macOS 26 Tahoe (2025):** The current latest release as of 2026, which unified version numbering across all Apple products and introduced the "Liquid Glass" design. \[[1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MacOS%5FSequoia), [2](https://www.macworld.com/article/672681/list-of-all-macos-versions-including-the-latest-macos.html), [3](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MacOS%5FTahoe), [4](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MacOS%5Fversion%5Fhistory), [5](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MacOS), [6](https://setapp.com/how-to/full-list-of-all-macos-versions)]
