### 🗺️ Host Nations & Cities

This historic tournament is the first World Cup to be jointly hosted by three nations—the **United States, Mexico, and Canada**. Matches are being played across **16 host cities**: \[[1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026%5FFIFA%5FWorld%5FCup)]

- **United States (11)**: New York/New Jersey, Los Angeles, Dallas, Miami, Atlanta, Seattle, Houston, Philadelphia, Kansas City, Boston, and the San Francisco Bay Area.
- **Mexico (3)**: Mexico City, Guadalajara, and Monterrey.
- **Canada (2)**: Toronto and Vancouver. \[[1](https://www.britannica.com/event/2026-FIFA-World-Cup)]

### ⚽ Tournament Format Expansion

The 2026 edition features a massive structural overhaul, expanding from 32 teams to a record **48 teams** competing across a total of **104 matches** over **39 days**. \[[1](https://www.ucfb.ac.uk/news/the-48-team-world-cup-how-the-new-group-stage-works/), [2](https://www.britannica.com/event/2026-FIFA-World-Cup)]

- **The Group Stage**: The 48 teams are divided into **12 groups of four**. Teams play a round-robin format within their groups. \[[1](https://www.usatoday.com/story/sports/soccer/worldcup/2026/06/18/fifa-world-cup-team-country-abbreviations-2026/90608462007/), [2](https://www.ucfb.ac.uk/news/the-48-team-world-cup-how-the-new-group-stage-works/)]
- **Knockout Progression**: The top two teams from each group, alongside the **eight best third-place finishers**, advance to a brand-new **Round of 32** single-elimination phase. \[[1](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ak30dLNw7zU), [2](https://www.usatoday.com/story/sports/soccer/worldcup/2026/06/18/fifa-world-cup-team-country-abbreviations-2026/90608462007/)]
- **The Long Road**: Because of the extra knockout round, the eventual champion will now have to play **eight matches** total to lift the trophy, up from seven in previous tournaments. \[[1](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/7cGLfqeKAU8), [2](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ak30dLNw7zU)]
