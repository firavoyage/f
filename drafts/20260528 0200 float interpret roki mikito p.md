**"Roki" (ロキ) is a massive hit Japanese Vocaloid song released in 2018 by the producer Mikito-P, featuring the synthetic voicebank Kagamine Rin alongside Mikito-P’s own human vocals.** The title "Roki" is <mark style="margin: 0px; text-decoration-style: solid; text-decoration-color: rgb(230, 232, 240); border-bottom: 0px none rgb(230, 232, 240);">a clever, multi-layered play on words that primarily revolves around the word <strong>"Rock" (ロック, Rokku)</strong> and the phrase <strong>"Rock n' Roll"</strong></mark>. \[[1](https://argobdp.miraheze.org/wiki/Roki), [2](https://vocaloid.fandom.com/wiki/%E3%83%AD%E3%82%AD%5F%28Roki%29), [3](https://genius.com/Genius-english-translations-p-mikitop-roki-ft-kagamine-rin-english-translation-lyrics), [4](https://popnmusic.fandom.com/wiki/Roki%5F%28mikitoP%29), [5](https://theyetee.com/products/roki-12-vinyl-import)]

The Meaning Behind the Title

- **Rock 'n' Roll:** The title is a rhythmic abbreviation of "Rockin' Rock 'n' Roll" (ロキロキのロックンロール), which serves as the energetic driving catchphrase repeated throughout the chorus.
- **The Norse God Loki:** The title is spelled identically to Loki, the Norse god of mischief. This mirrors the cynical, tongue-in-cheek, and slightly mischievous tone of the lyrics.
- **The "P" Suffix:** In the Vocaloid community, the "-P" suffix stands for **"Producer"**. It is a title given to creators who write, arrange, and produce music using Vocaloid software. \[[1](https://www.yamaha.com/en/stories/the-key/003-01/), [2](https://www.smule.com/song/mikito-p-and-kagamine-rin-roki-english-lyrics-karaoke-lyrics/6142564%5F6142564/arrangement), [3](https://vocaloid.fandom.com/wiki/Producer), [4](https://popnmusic.fandom.com/wiki/Roki%5F%28mikitoP%29)]

Theme and Meaning of the Lyrics

The song acts as a sarcastic yet encouraging commentary on the modern indie music scene and internet culture. It follows a dialogue between an experienced, older musician (sung by Mikito-P) and a young, up-and-coming creator (sung by Kagamine Rin): \[[1](https://popnmusic.fandom.com/wiki/Roki%5F%28mikitoP%29), [2](https://www.smule.com/song/mikito-p-and-kagamine-rin-roki-english-lyrics-karaoke-lyrics/6142564%5F6142564/arrangement), [3](https://www.reddit.com/r/Vocaloid/comments/vey7dc/roki%5Fguy%5Fi%5Fdunno%5Fif%5Fhe%5Fhas%5Fa%5Fname/), [4](https://vocaloid.fandom.com/wiki/%E3%83%AD%E3%82%AD%5F%28Roki%29)]

- **Critique of "Clout" Culture:** The lyrics mock internet musicians who focus too much on online optics, hiding behind selfie filters, acting overly edgy or shy ("AT-Field" and "depression mode"), and worrying about view counts and fame over substance.
- **Anxiety vs. Ambition:** It highlights the intense pressure creators face, like needing throat medicine, getting cold feet before live shows, and balancing day jobs with music.
- **The Ultimate Message:** Despite the biting sarcasm, the song ends on a highly uplifting note. The recurring line _"Shinun ja nee zo"_ translates to **"Don't die out there"** or **"We've got to survive"**. It is a mutual pact between the veteran and the rookie to keep making music, ignore the superficiality of the internet, and just rock out. \[[1](https://www.smule.com/song/mikito-p-and-kagamine-rin-roki-english-lyrics-karaoke-lyrics/6142564%5F6142564/arrangement), [2](https://remywiki.com/Roki%5F%28mikitoP%29), [3](https://genius.com/Mori-calliope-roki-english-rap-cover-lyrics), [4](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nTY2dzlCvuE), [5](https://popnmusic.fandom.com/wiki/Roki%5F%28mikitoP%29)]

Verse 1: The Persona of the Indie Musician

This verse introduces the archetype of the modern internet/indie musician—anxious, image-conscious, and hiding behind a cool subculture facade.

- **さあ 眠眠打破 (Come on, Minmin Daha / _Drink down the anti-sleep medication_)**
  - _Meaning:_ "Minmin Daha" is a famous Japanese energy/caffeine drink. This sets the scene: the artist is pulling an all-nighter to write music or edit videos.
- **昼夜逆転 (Day and night reversal)**
  - _Meaning:_ Their sleep schedule is completely ruined. A classic trope of the nocturnal bedroom producer or indie artist.
- **VOX AC30w (VOX AC30w)**
  - _Meaning:_ A very famous, iconic British guitar amplifier. It establishes that they are trying to capture a classic, authentic rock sound.
- **テレキャスター背負ったサブカルボーイがバンド仲間にやっほー (A subculture boy with a Telecaster on his back says 'Yahoo!' to his bandmates)**
  - _Meaning:_ The Fender Telecaster is the ultimate stereotype weapon of "subculture" (indie/hipster) Japanese rock musicians. He is acting cool and casual in front of his peers.
- **アルバイトはネクラモード (At his part-time job, he is in 'gloomy/dark' mode)**
  - _Meaning:_ _Nekura_ means naturally dark, introverted, or gloomy. He hates his day job, puts his head down, and doesn't socialize.
- **対バンにはATフィールド (Against rival bands on the same stage, he puts up an AT Field)**
  - _Meaning:_ _Taiban_ refers to a live show where multiple indie bands share the ticket. An "AT Field" is an anime reference to _Neon Genesis Evangelion_—it means he puts up an emotional, defensive wall because he is intimidated by his rivals.
- **“人見知り”宣言で逃げる気か BOY (Are you planning to run away by declaring 'I’m just shy,' BOY?)**
  - _Meaning:_ The veteran narrator calls him out. The boy uses "social anxiety" or being "shy" as an excuse to avoid networking, taking risks, or facing the harsh realities of the music scene.

Pre-Chorus: The Cult of Personality

The narrator critiques how young artists get trapped by vanity, money, and chasing online gurus, reminding them where true creativity comes from.

- **ゆーて お坊ちゃん お嬢ちゃん お金も才能も (That said, young masters and young ladies, having money and talent...)**
  - _Meaning:_ _Yute_ is casual slang for "Even so..." The narrator addresses spoiled or privileged young creators.
- **なまじっかあるだけ厄介でやんす (...is actually a bit of a curse when you only have a half-baked amount of it)**
  - _Meaning:_ Having just _enough_ money and talent makes you arrogant but lazy. It prevents you from working truly hard.
- **Boys be ambitious (はいはい) (Boys be ambitious / _Yeah, yeah_)**
  - _Meaning:_ A famous historical quote in Japan used to motivate youth. The younger voice (Rin) dismisses it sarcastically with "Yeah, yeah," showing the apathy of the younger generation.
- **Like this old man (Like this old man)**
  - _Meaning:_ Mikito-P pointing to himself. He is the "old man" trying to give advice.
- **長い前髪 君 誰の信者 信者 信者 (With your long bangs, whose devotee/follower are you?)**
  - _Meaning:_ Long bangs covering the eyes is a staple look for trendy indie/Vocaloid musicians. The narrator asks: "Who are you copying? Whose online cult are you following?"
- **勘違いすんな 教祖はオマエだ (Don't get it twisted, YOU are the cult leader)**
  - _Meaning:_ Stop looking for a savior or a guru to copy. If you want to be an artist, you have to be your own leader and trust your own voice.

The chorus strips away the social commentary and demands pure, unadulterated passion.

- **ロキロキのロックンロックンロール (Roki-Roki Rock 'n' Rock 'n' Roll)**
  - _Meaning:_ A driving, rhythmic chant celebrating the raw, chaotic energy of rock music.
- **かき鳴らすエレクトリックギターは (The electric guitar you're strumming)**
  - _Meaning:_ The physical act of making noise and letting go.
- **Don't Stop! Don't Stop! (Don't Stop! Don't Stop!)**
  - _Meaning:_ Keep playing, don't let your anxieties freeze you.
- **さあ君の全てを 曝け出してみせろよ (Come on, show me everything you've got / bare your entire self)**
  - _Meaning:_ Throw away the internet persona, the filters, and the cool attitude. Show your real, messy, vulnerable self through your music.

Verse 2: Internet Haters vs. Fragile Egos

This verse deals directly with social media culture, internet anonymity, and the toxic cycle of checking one's status online.

- **さあ 日進月歩 (Come on, making rapid, steady progress)**
  - _Meaning:_ The industry moves incredibly fast; you have to evolve every day just to keep up.
- **いい曲書いてる？ 動員ふえてる？ (Are you writing good songs? Is your concert attendance growing?)**
  - _Meaning:_ The constant, anxiety-inducing questions every indie artist asks themselves or gets asked by management.
- **「知名度あるけど人気はそんなにないから色々大変ですね。」 ("You have some name recognition, but you aren't actually popular, so things must be tough for you.")**
  - _Meaning:_ A brutally passive-aggressive comment left by an online troll. It means people recognize the artist's name from an algorithm, but they don't have a loyal fanbase who buys tickets.
- **はっきり言うなよ 匿名アイコン (Don't say it so bluntly, anonymous icon)**
  - _Meaning:_ The artist's reaction to the hater. It stings because it's true, but it's infuriating because it comes from an anonymous online profile.
- **はっきり見せない 実写のアイコン (Not showing yourself clearly, filtering your real-life photo icon)**
  - _Meaning:_ Alternatively, the artist themselves is also hiding—using heavily edited, obscured, or anime photos instead of showing their real face to the world.
- **いい歳こいて自意識まだ BOY (You're at that age and still acting so self-conscious, BOY?)**
  - _Meaning:_ You're a grown adult, yet you're still completely paralyzed by what random people on the internet think of you.

Pre-Chorus 2: The Shelf-Life of Clout

A reality check about aging in the music industry and a vow of mutual survival.

- **ぶっちゃけどんだけ賢くあざとくやったって (To be completely honest, no matter how clever or calculating you act...)**
  - _Meaning:_ No matter how perfectly you play the social media algorithm or how trendy you try to be...
- **10年後にメイクは落ちてんだよ (...in 10 years, that makeup is going to melt off)**
  - _Meaning:_ Youth and internet trends fade. You can't fake it forever. Eventually, you will grow old, and only your actual music will matter.
- **Boys be ambitious（はいはい） / Like this old man（え？） (Boys be ambitious (Yeah yeah) / Like this old man (Huh?))**
  - _Meaning:_ Rin's "Huh?" shows comedic skepticism toward the veteran's advice.
- **生き抜くためだ キメろ / Take a "selfy" "selfy" "selfy" (It's just to survive, strike a pose / Take a "selfie, selfie, selfie")**
  - _Meaning:_ A cynical concession. Even though social media superficiality is annoying, the narrator admits you _have_ to do it to play the modern game and survive. So go ahead, take the selfie, promote yourself, do what you have to do.
- **死ぬんじゃねぇぞ お互いにな! (Don't die out there—both of us!)**
  - _Meaning:_ **The core thesis of the song.** The music industry and internet culture are brutal and mentally draining. The veteran and the rookie make a pact to look out for their mental health and stay alive.

Chorus 2: Target the Audience

- **ロキロキのロックンロックンロール / 薄っぺらいラブソングでもいい (Roki-Roki Rock 'n' Roll / Even a shallow, superficial love song is fine)**
  - _Meaning:_ Your music doesn't always have to be high art or a masterpiece. Even a simple, cliché pop song has value if it's honest.
- **Don't Stop! Don't Stop! / さあ目の前のあの子を 撃ち抜いてみせろよ (Don't Stop! Don't Stop! / Come on, pierce the heart of that kid right in front of you)**
  - _Meaning:_ Don't worry about appealing to millions of people online. Focus on the one person standing in front of you at the live venue, or the one person listening through their headphones. Make _them_ feel something.

Verse 3 & Bridge: Stage Fright and Excuses

This section exposes the physical and psychological anxiety of performing live.

- **お茶を濁してちゃ満足できない (You can't be satisfied just by beating around the bush / stalling)**
  - _Meaning:_ You can't make excuses forever; eventually, you have to step onto the stage.
- **スタジオに運ばれたスロートコートは (The Throat Coat tea brought into the studio...)**
  - _Meaning:_ "Throat Coat" is a famous herbal tea used by singers to soothe their vocal cords.
- **安心 不安心 プレッシャーでいっぱい (...is full of security, insecurity, and immense pressure)**
  - _Meaning:_ Staring at the tea, the singer is a nervous wreck before the show, relying on remedies to give them confidence.
- **「実は昨日から風邪で声が出ません」 ("Actually, I caught a cold yesterday and lost my voice...")**
  - _Meaning:_ The classic, cowardly excuse an artist makes on social media right before a performance to lower expectations in case they fail.
- **は？は？は？は？ はぁ… 寝言は寝て言え (Huh? Huh? Huh? Huh? _Sigh_... Save the talking for when you're asleep)**
  - _Meaning:_ The narrator has zero patience for this self-sabotaging attitude. "Stop making excuses and get on stage."
- **ベイビー ベイビー... (Baby, baby...)**
  - _Meaning:_ A classic rock 'n' roll ad-lib, building up energy.
- **死ぬんじゃねぇぞ お互いにな！ (Don't die out there—both of us!)**
