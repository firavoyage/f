### 1. The Real Leader: Xi Jinping

  - **Chairman** of the Central Military Commission (CMC)
  - **President** of the People's Republic of China (PRC)

- **Elimination of Term Limits (2018):** Perhaps the most significant factor, the Chinese constitution was amended in 2018 to abolish the two-term limit for the presidency. - **"Core Leader" Status:** In 2016, he was formally bestowed the title of "core leader" of the CCP, elevating his stature to the level of previous strongmen like Mao Zedong and Deng Xiaoping.

### 3. Contrast with Predecessors

The nickname highlights the contrast between Xi Jinping's rule and that of his two immediate predecessors, Jiang Zemin and Hu Jintao. They governed under a system of **collective leadership** with unwritten rules on retirement age and term limits, designed specifically to prevent the emergence of a cult of personality and one-man rule, a lesson learned from the Mao era. Xi Jinping has largely dismantled these institutional checks.

In summary, **"Emperor Xi"** is a critical, unofficial title used to describe Xi Jinping's profound consolidation of personal power, the move toward indefinite rule, and the systematic dismantling of post-Mao institutional norms in the Chinese Communist Party.
