will be not or will not be

<mark style="margin: 0px; text-decoration-style: solid; text-decoration-color: rgb(230, 232, 240); border-bottom: 0px none rgb(230, 232, 240);"><strong>"Will not be"</strong></mark> is the correct standard word order in English grammar. \[[1](https://forum.wordreference.com/threads/i-will-be-not-vs-i-will-not-be.2071351/), [2](https://english.stackexchange.com/questions/407810/what-is-correct-will-be-not-or-will-not-be), [3](https://www.quora.com/Which-sentence-is-correct-1st-will-be-not-and-2nd-will-not-be)]

When you make a future tense verb negative, the word **not** must come immediately after the helper verb (**will**) and before the main verb (**be**). \[[1](https://www.quora.com/Which-sentence-is-correct-1st-will-be-not-and-2nd-will-not-be), [2](https://english.stackexchange.com/questions/407810/what-is-correct-will-be-not-or-will-not-be)]

### Standard Usage Examples

- **Correct**: "I **will not be** attending the meeting tomorrow."
- _Incorrect_: "I _will be not_ attending..."
- **Correct**: "It **will not be** cold on Tuesday."
- _Incorrect_: "It _will be not_ cold..." \[[1](https://ludwig.guru/s/will+not+be+available), [2](https://www.talkenglish.com/speaking/lessondetails.aspx?ALID=4260), [3](https://www.quora.com/Which-sentence-is-correct-1st-will-be-not-and-2nd-will-not-be)]

### The Exception: "Will be not only..." \[[1](https://www.reddit.com/r/EnglishLearning/comments/18lxaw1/wont%5Fand%5Fwill%5Fnot/)]

The phrase "will be not" is only correct if it is part of a **"not only... but also"** sentence structure. In this case, "not" belongs to the description that follows, rather than modifying the word "will". \[[1](https://www.bbc.co.uk/learningenglish/course/towards-advanced/unit-20/session-1), [2](https://www.quora.com/Which-sentence-is-correct-1st-will-be-not-and-2nd-will-not-be)]

- **Example**: "The new library **will be not only** bigger, but also more modern." \[[1](https://www.quora.com/Which-sentence-is-correct-1st-will-be-not-and-2nd-will-not-be)]

### Quick Tip

In casual spoken English, native speakers almost always shorten "will not be" into the contraction **"won't be"**. \[[1](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iaBZ4J7sHxc\&t=5), [2](https://english.stackexchange.com/questions/407810/what-is-correct-will-be-not-or-will-not-be), [3](https://www.reddit.com/r/EnglishLearning/comments/18lxaw1/wont%5Fand%5Fwill%5Fnot/)]

- **Example**: "I **won't be** home until late tonight." \[[1](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/DIXqzWDN-fs), [2](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iaBZ4J7sHxc\&t=5)]

## You said: like "it will be absolutely not easy"

The grammatically correct phrasing is **"It will absolutely not be easy."**

In English, adverbs like "absolutely" and the negative word "not" must sit between the helper verb (**will**) and the main verb (**be**).

### Why the order matters

1. **Will** comes first as the helper verb.
2. **Absolutely not** comes next to modify the statement.
3. **Be** comes last as the main verb.

### Better Alternatives

Here are a few ways to say this that sound completely natural to native speakers:

- "It **will definitely not be** easy." (Highly natural)
- "It **is not going to be** easy at all." (Great for casual conversation)
- "It **won't be** easy." (Simple and direct)
