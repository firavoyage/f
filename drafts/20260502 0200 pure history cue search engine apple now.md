Cue search engine

**Cue** was <mark style="margin: 0px; text-decoration-style: solid; text-decoration-color: rgb(230, 232, 240); border-bottom: 0px none rgb(230, 232, 240);">a personal search engine and assistant app that gathered information from your online accounts—like email, social media, and calendars—to create a unified snapshot of your day</mark>. Originally launched as **Greplin** in 2010, it was designed to help users search across multiple platforms (Facebook, Gmail, Twitter, etc.) from a single location without having to log in to each individually. \[[1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cue%5F%28search%5Fengine%29), [2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daniel%5FGross%5F%28businessman%29)]

Key History and Features

- **Rebranding:** In 2012, Greplin rebranded as Cue and pivoted toward being a predictive personal assistant.
- **Personal Cloud:** It acted as a "personal cloud" index, turning scattered data into a smart timeline or daily agenda with relevant details like flight confirmations and restaurant reservations.
- **Acquisition:** Apple acquired Cue in 2013 for an estimated $40 million to $60 million to compete with services like Google Now.
- **Shutdown:** Shortly after the acquisition, Apple shut down the standalone Cue service. Its core technology and team, led by founder Daniel Gross, were integrated into Apple's machine learning and Siri efforts. \[[1](https://techcrunch.com/2013/10/03/cue-acquired-for-over-40m-likely-by-apple-to-compete-with-google-now/), [2](https://techcrunch.com/2013/10/02/cue-greplin/), [3](https://ycuniverse.com/cue-personal-information-management-service-acquired-by-apple-cue-co-founder-explains-product-video/), [4](https://techcrunch.com/2013/10/03/why-did-apple-buy-cue-because-google-now-eats-siris-lunch/), [5](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cue%5F%28search%5Fengine%29), [6](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daniel%5FGross%5F%28businessman%29)]

Modern Context (Eddy Cue)

You might also see "Cue" mentioned in recent search news regarding **Eddy Cue**, Apple’s Senior VP of Services. In May 2025, he testified in the U.S. Justice Department’s antitrust trial against Alphabet, stating that AI search engines from companies like OpenAI and Perplexity would likely replace traditional search engines like Google. \[[1](https://www.theverge.com/policy/662974/google-search-remedies-trial-eddy-cue-apple-deal-ai), [2](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/apple-looking-add-ai-search-165337780.html), [3](https://tech.slashdot.org/story/25/05/08/0314235/google-refutes-apples-claims-of-search-traffic-decline)]

what's google now

**Google Now** was <mark style="margin: 0px; text-decoration-style: solid; text-decoration-color: rgb(230, 232, 240); border-bottom: 0px none rgb(230, 232, 240);">a predictive personal assistant that proactively delivered information to users before they even asked for it</mark>. Launched in 2012 with Android 4.1 "Jelly Bean," it used "cards" to display relevant details based on your location, search history, and daily habits. \[[1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google%5FNow), [2](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PAs6yQUCkno\&t=3), [3](https://www.cnet.com/tech/mobile/how-to-get-started-with-google-now/)]

Key Features

- **Predictive Cards:** It displayed snippets of info called "cards" for weather, traffic for your commute, flight statuses, and package tracking.
- **Contextual Intelligence:** By scanning your Gmail and calendar, it could show a boarding pass at the airport or a reservation at a restaurant.
- **Voice Search:** It popularized the "OK Google" hands-free command to perform searches or set reminders.
- **Now on Tap:** A later feature (Android 6.0) that let you hold the home button to search for information based on what was currently on your screen. \[[1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google%5FNow), [2](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jov2lL6urOc\&t=9), [3](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PAs6yQUCkno\&t=3), [4](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZXtudZl5mzM\&t=1), [5](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1JEXDBWehvI\&t=21), [6](https://www.cnet.com/tech/mobile/the-difference-between-google-now-and-google-assistant/), [7](https://www.hillwebcreations.com/what-is-google-now-how-use-google-now-cards/)]

What happened to it?

Google began phasing out the "Now" branding in late 2015 to make way for **Google Assistant**, which offered a more conversational, two-way dialogue. \[[1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google%5FNow), [2](https://www.cnet.com/tech/mobile/the-difference-between-google-now-and-google-assistant/)]

- **Google Assistant:** Replaced the predictive assistant's core functions in 2016, integrating more deeply with Google Home and Pixel phones.
- **Google Discover:** The visual feed of interest-based news articles that you see today on the Google app's home screen is the direct evolution of the original Google Now cards.
- **Google Now Launcher:** The standalone home screen app was officially retired in **April 2023** after nearly a decade of support. \[[1](https://www.cnet.com/tech/mobile/the-difference-between-google-now-and-google-assistant/), [2](https://www.pcmag.com/encyclopedia/term/google-now), [3](https://www.hillwebcreations.com/what-is-google-now-how-use-google-now-cards/), [4](https://www.androidauthority.com/google-now-launcher-stop-working-3309516/), [5](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/290088/20230406/google-now-launcher-finally-goodbye-android-users-decade.htm), [6](https://www.androidheadlines.com/2023/04/google-now-launcher-getting-discontinued-10-years-after-launch.html)]
