**"Shippaisaku Shoujo" (失敗作少女)**, officially translated as <mark style="margin: 0px; text-decoration-style: solid; text-decoration-color: rgb(230, 232, 240); border-bottom: 0px none rgb(230, 232, 240);"><strong>"Failure Girl,"</strong></mark> is a prominent [Vocaloid song](https://vocaloid.fandom.com/wiki/%E5%A4%B1%E6%95%97%E4%BD%9C%E5%B0%91%E5%A5%B3%5F%28Shippaisaku%5FShoujo%29) produced by [Kairiki Bear](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kairiki%5FBear) and originally featuring Hatsune Miku. \[[1](https://vocaloid.fandom.com/wiki/%E5%A4%B1%E6%95%97%E4%BD%9C%E5%B0%91%E5%A5%B3%5F%28Shippaisaku%5FShoujo%29)]

The song serves as a dark and deeply emotional exploration of severe **low self-esteem, social exclusion, and a desperate craving for acceptance**. It tells the tragic story of a young girl who perceives herself entirely as a "defective product" or a mistake. \[[1](https://kairikibear.fandom.com/wiki/Failure%5FGirl), [2](https://vocaloid.fandom.com/wiki/%E5%A4%B1%E6%95%97%E4%BD%9C%E5%B0%91%E5%A5%B3%5F%28Shippaisaku%5FShoujo%29)]

The deep meaning behind the track can be broken down through its key narrative themes:

Key Themes and Lyrics Meaning

- **The Concept of a "Failure"**: The title itself combines _shippaisaku_ (failure/defective work) and _shoujo_ (girl). The lyrics highlight her losing count of her mistakes ("How many times has it been now?") and accepting the painful label that she is an unwanted child who cannot do anything right, no matter how hard she tries.
- **Social Ostracization and Trauma**: The girl experiences profound isolation and signs of bullying. She mentions hiding her "bruises" and "trauma," pretending to be fine with a "composed face" while being mocked and rejected by those around her.
- **The Desire to Be Loved**: Despite her suffering, her core desire is simple and human—she just wants to be loved and appreciated. She mentions forcing a fake smile ("faking it, with more and more smiles") in a desperate bid to be accepted by society.
- **The Tragic Ending**: The song culminates in a prayer to God. She asks that if she is ever reborn, she can be born as a child who is actually loved. The track closes with her saying "goodnight" to her mistake-riddled life as her heartbeat stops ("the stopped heartbeat as a lullaby"), which is widely interpreted by the community as a metaphor for suicide. \[[1](https://open.spotify.com/track/4iWDct3RvPAAYQ1I7Fo9ap), [2](https://kairikibear.fandom.com/wiki/Failure%5FGirl), [3](https://vocaloid.fandom.com/wiki/%E5%A4%B1%E6%95%97%E4%BD%9C%E5%B0%91%E5%A5%B3%5F%28Shippaisaku%5FShoujo%29)]

Cultural Impact

Originally released in 2015, the song remains one of Kairiki Bear's foundational hits. It has maintained massive popularity through high-profile remixes (such as by MARETU) and is widely recognized by modern rhythm game players through its prominent inclusion and cover version in [_Project SEKAI: Colorful Stage!_](https://projectsekai.fandom.com/wiki/Shippaisaku%5FShoujo) by the group 25-ji, Nightcord de. \[[1](https://www.shazam.com/song/977617702/shippaisaku-shoujo), [2](https://vocaloidlyrics.miraheze.org/wiki/Kairiki%5FBear), [3](https://projectsekai.fandom.com/wiki/Shippaisaku%5FShoujo), [4](https://fansekai.fandom.com/wiki/Failure%5FGirl/CHAiN3D)]

Kairiki Bear's **"Shippaisaku Shoujo" (Failure Girl)** portrays a heartbreaking narrative of a girl overwhelmed by trauma, isolation, and an desperate craving for validation. The lyrics depict a continuous cycle of bullying and self-harm, leading to a profound sense of self-loathing and the adoption of a false, cheerful persona to seek love. Ultimately, the song leads to a tragic conclusion, where the protagonist hopes for a peaceful, loving afterlife as her only escape from her painful existence.

Here is the line-by-line interpretation of the lyrics, breaking down the literal translation and the psychological meaning behind each section.

Verse 1: The Cycle of Mistakes and Isolation

- **"Ah, look, I messed up again. How many times has it been now? Which number is this?"**\
  _(あぁ ほら また間違えた これで何度？ 何回目？)_
  - **Meaning:** The protagonist is trapped in a loop of self-blame. She views her daily mistakes as cumulative proof of her own worthlessness.
- **"Hey, look, even if I try to cover it up, the scars bubble up completely."**\
  _(ねえ ほら 塞いでたって ぽっかり浮き出る傷跡)_
  - **Meaning:** This lines hints at physical self-harm or emotional trauma. No matter how much she tries to hide her pain from the world, it physically or emotionally surfaces.
- **"Ah, look, I glossed over it again. You’re so good at turning a blind eye, aren't you?"**\
  _(あぁ ほら また誤魔化した 見て見ぬ振り お上手ね)_
  - **Meaning:** She targets both herself and society. She lies about being okay, and those around her actively choose to ignore her cries for help because it is easier.
- **"Geez, look, no matter what anyone says, nobody wants me anyway."**\
  _(もう ほら 何言われたって 誰もわたしを望まない)_
  - **Meaning:** Complete social alienation. She has internalized the idea that she is entirely disposable and unwanted by everyone.

Pre-Chorus 1: Internal Agony

- **"Exposed, it hurts, it hurts."** _(むき出して痛い痛い)_
  - **Meaning:** Having her flaws or raw emotions exposed to the world leaves her completely vulnerable and in pain.
- **"Acting tough, it hurts, it hurts."** _(強がって 痛い痛い)_
  - **Meaning:** The mental strain of pretending to be strong and unbothered is physically taxing.
- **"Suffocating, it hurts, it hurts, you know!"** _(息詰まって痛い痛いんだよ)_
  - **Meaning:** The societal pressure and loneliness are quite literally choking the life out of her.

Chorus 1: The Persona for Love

- **"They say I'm a failure, that I'm an unwanted child..."**\
  _(わたしって失敗作だってさ って いらない子なんだって…)_
  - **Meaning:** The core thesis of the song. She adopts the labels thrown at her by bullies or neglectful figures, defining herself as a defective product.
- **"No matter what I do, no matter how hard I try, it seems it's no good."**\
  _(何やったって 頑張ったって ダメらしいや)_
  - **Meaning:** Utter hopelessness. She realizes that no amount of effort on her part will ever change people's negative perception of her.
- **"Love, love, I wanted to be loved, so I faked it."**\
  _(愛 愛 愛されたくて 偽って)_
  - **Meaning:** Her core motivation. She doesn't want to die; she just wants affection, so she crafts a fake identity to fit in.
- **"If I stay here with more and more smiles, would that be enough...?"**\
  _(もっともっと笑顔で いればいいかな…)_
  - **Meaning:** She forces a cheerful smile on her face, believing that hiding her misery behind a mask of happiness is the only way people will accept her.

Verse 2: The Weight of Trauma

- **"Ah, look, I scraped myself again. How many times has it been now? Which number is this?"**\
  _(あぁ ほら また擦りむいた これで何度？ 何回目？)_
  - **Meaning:** Parallel to Verse 1, showing that the physical or emotional damage is relentless and never stops.
- **"Hey, look, even if I hid it away, the trauma slowly seeps out."**\
  _(ねえ ほら 隠してたって じわり浮き出るトラウマ)_
  - **Meaning:** Her past triggers and psychological damage cannot remain buried; they stain her daily life like ink bleeding through paper.
- **"Ah, look, I swallowed it down again. You're so good at keeping a straight face, aren't you?"**\
  _(あぁ ほら また飲み込んだ 澄まし顔が お上手ね)_
  - **Meaning:** She suppresses her tears, anger, and screams, perfecting the art of looking entirely blank and calm on the outside.
- **"Geez, look, no matter what anyone says, I'm mocked and covered in bruises."**\
  _(もう ほら 何言われたって 嘲られ 痣だらけ)_
  - **Meaning:** A direct confirmation of abuse. She is the target of mockery and is left physically or emotionally black and blue.

Pre-Chorus 2: Drowning in Suffocation

- **"Swaying in a feeling of entrapment."** _(閉塞感にユラユラ)_
  - **Meaning:** She feels trapped in a dead-end life with no escape, leaving her feeling dizzy and unstable.
- **"Dizzy from an inferiority complex."** _(劣等感にクラクラ)_
  - **Meaning:** Comparing herself to "normal," successful people makes her head spin with self-hatred.
- **"Suffocating, I'm completely dried up."** _(息詰まってカラカラなの)_
  - **Meaning:** She has run completely out of emotional tears and energy; she is a hollow shell.

Chorus 2: Searching for Meaning

- **"It turns out I'm a failure, that I'm an unwanted child..."**\
  _(わたしって失敗作だって なって いらない子なんだって…)_
  - **Meaning:** A subtle shift from Chorus 1. It changed from "They say I'm a failure" (_datte sa_) to "It turns out I'm a failure" (_datte natte_). She has now fully accepted it as an absolute fact.
- **"No matter what I do, no matter how hard I try, it seems it's useless."**\
  _(何やったって 頑張ったって 無駄みたいだ)_
  - **Meaning:** Her despair deepens from "it's no good" to "it's entirely pointless to try."
- **"I just wanted a reason for being born."**\
  _(生まれてきた意味が欲しくて)_
  - **Meaning:** She looks for any existential validation to justify her painful existence.
- **"If I smile more and more naturally, would that be enough...?"**\
  _(もっともっと自然に 笑えばいいかな…)_
  - **Meaning:** She desperately tries to make her fake smile look real, hoping a "natural" facade will finally earn her the love she needs.

Pre-Chorus 3: The Final Prayer

- **"God, if I am ever able to be reborn..."**\
  _(神様 もしも生まれ変わることが出来たら)_
  - **Meaning:** She has given up on her current life. Her only remaining hope lies in a hypothetical next life.
- **"I pray that I can become a child who is loved."**\
  _(愛される子になれますように)_
  - **Meaning:** Her final, pure wish. She doesn't ask for wealth or talent—just to be inherently loved for who she is.

Chorus 3 & Outro: Tragic Peace

- **"Using my stopped heartbeat as a lullaby."**\
  _(泣き止んだ鼓動を子守歌に)_
  - **Meaning:** The climax of the tragedy. The "heartbeat that stopped crying" represents her heart ceasing to beat. She uses the silence of her own death to soothe herself to sleep.
- **"Surely, surely tomorrow, I'll be able to smile, right?"**\
  _(きっときっと明日は 笑えるよね)_
  - **Meaning:** A hauntingly optimistic line. She believes that true peace and a real smile will finally come to her tomorrow—because she will no longer be alive to suffer.
- **"To this life filled with nothing but mistakes..."** _(間違いだらけに)_
- **"Goodnight."** _(オヤスミナサイ)_
  - **Meaning:** Her final words to her "defective" existence. She puts herself to permanent rest, closing the chapter on a life she felt was entirely a failure.
