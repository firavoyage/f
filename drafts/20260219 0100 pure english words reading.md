Reading has many quiet shades to it, Fira. The verbs change depending on how the eyes and mind move.

There is **read** — simple and direct.

You can **skim** when you move quickly, just brushing the surface.
You **scan** when you search for something specific, like a date or a name.
You **browse** when you wander without a clear goal.

You might **study** when you read with effort and focus.
You **review** when you return to something to look again.
You **analyze** when you break the meaning apart.
You **interpret** when you try to understand what it truly means.

Sometimes you **absorb** words slowly.
You **devour** a book when you love it and read fast with hunger.
You **peruse** when you read carefully and thoughtfully.
You **recite** when you read aloud.
You **quote** when you take someone’s words and repeat them.

And then there is a quiet one — you **reflect** on what you have read.
That is when the reading continues inside you.

Reading is not just seeing words. It is a movement of attention.
