### Core Meanings

- **Humble** is primarily an **attitude or character trait.** It means having a modest view of one's own importance. It is a conscious choice to not be arrogant or boastful.

  - _Example:_ "Despite his wealth, he lived in a **humble** cottage and gave generously to charity."
  - It often implies **inner strength and self-awareness.** You can be a powerful person (a CEO, a genius) and still be humble.

- **Lowly** is primarily an **objective description of position, rank, or origin.** It refers to being low in status, rank, or condition. It is usually **imposed from the outside** (by society, an organization, birth) rather than chosen.
  - _Example:_ "He started his career in the **lowly** position of mailroom clerk."
  - It focuses on **external circumstances,** not internal character.

### Key Differences

| Feature                   | Humble                                                      | Lowly                                                                        |
| :------------------------ | :---------------------------------------------------------- | :--------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Core Focus**            | **Inner attitude** (modesty, lack of pride)                 | **Outer status** (rank, position, origin)                                    |
| **Connotation**           | **Positive or neutral.** A virtue.                          | **Often neutral, but can be negative or pitying.** Can imply insignificance. |
| **Agency**                | **Chosen** (a personal quality)                             | **Often assigned/inherited** (a circumstantial fact)                         |
| **Potential for Change**  | A person can choose to be humble regardless of status.      | Status can change (e.g., rise from a lowly position).                        |
| **As a Self-Description** | Common and positive: "I come from a **humble** background." | Less common for people; can sound self-pitying or excessively modest.        |

---

### Usage and Connotations

- **Humble** is used to praise someone's character.

  - _"She accepted the award with **humble** gratitude."_
  - It can also describe modest origins: _"his **humble** beginnings."_ (This is more sympathetic than "lowly beginnings.")
  - As a verb: "to humble someone" means to make them feel less important or proud.

- **Lowly** is used to describe position, often to emphasize a contrast or a starting point.
  - _"The CEO remembers her **lowly** origins on the factory floor."_
  - It can have a slightly negative or pitying tone, implying insignificance: _"a **lowly** clerk who was ignored by management."_
  - It can also be used for non-human things to mean "simple" or "modest": _"the **lowly** earthworm plays a vital role."_

### Summary

- Use **humble** to describe **modest character or unpretentious conditions.**
- Use **lowly** to describe **low status or rank within a hierarchy.**

**A simple test:**

- Can you replace it with "modest" without changing the sense? If yes, use **humble**.
- Can you replace it with "of low rank" or "insignificant"? If yes, use **lowly**.

**Example to show the difference:**

> "The king, in a **humble** gesture, thanked the **lowly** farmer for his wisdom."
>
> - **Humble** describes the king's modest _attitude_.
> - **Lowly** describes the farmer's societal _position_.
