Yes, you can use both phrases in casual, spoken English, but they have different grammatical rules and formality levels.

Here is the breakdown of how and when to use them.

### "Do like Joe does" (Informal)

- **Grammar Rule**: _Like_ is used as a conjunction linking two clauses.
- **Formality**: Highly informal and common in casual speech.
- **Traditional Correction**: Formal grammar rules state that **as** should be used instead of _like_ to connect clauses.
- **Formal Alternative**: "Do **as** Joe does."

### "Do like Joe" (Casual / Standard)

- **Grammar Rule**: _Like_ is used as a preposition followed by a noun object (_Joe_).
- **Formality**: Acceptable in everyday conversation and widely used.
- **Traditional Correction**: Some strict grammarians still prefer _as_ for comparisons of action.
- **Formal Alternative**: "Do **as** Joe \[does]."

### Summary of Best Choices

- **Casual Speech**: "Do like Joe" is your best, most natural-sounding choice.
