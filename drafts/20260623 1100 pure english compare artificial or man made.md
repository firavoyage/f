# artificial or man made

### When to Use "Artificial"

According to [Dictionary.com](https://www.dictionary.com/browse/artificial), **artificial** is best used when an object is designed to imitate something real or natural. It can also describe abstract concepts like systems or human behaviors. \[[1](https://www.dictionary.com/browse/artificial)]

- **Imitation:** Artificial grass, artificial sweetners, artificial flowers.
- **Technology:** Artificial Intelligence (AI), artificial limbs.
- **Behavior:** An artificial smile (forced or insincere). \[[1](https://simple.wikipedia.org/wiki/Artificial), [2](https://www.dictionary.com/browse/artificial), [3](https://www.facebook.com/museumofscience/videos/is-artificial-intelligence-better-than-human-intelligence-the-answer-depends-on-/888550222183624/), [4](https://www.collinsdictionary.com/us/dictionary/english/artificial), [5](https://damyan.tv/blog/to-what-extent-can-people-be-ordered-to-be-enhanced-with-artificial-limbs-and-muscles/)]

### When to Use "Man-Made"

As defined by the [Cambridge Dictionary](https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/man-made), **man-made** focuses on the origin of the item—specifically that humans built or caused it. It doesn't necessarily have to mimic anything in nature. \[[1](https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/man-made), [2](https://www.collinsdictionary.com/us/dictionary/english/man-made), [3](https://dictionary.cambridge.org/us/thesaurus/articles/made-by-humans)]

- **Structures:** A man-made lake, man-made fiber (nylon), man-made landmarks.
- **Disasters:** Global warming, the Dust Bowl, pollution. \[[1](https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/man-made), [2](https://simple.wikipedia.org/wiki/Artificial), [3](https://wordpress.kpu.ca/stitched/fabric/fibers-and-fabrics/manufactured-fibers/), [4](https://brainly.in/question/5251805), [5](https://www.cliffsnotes.com/cliffs-questions/3830079)]

### Modern Usage Note

Style guides like the [Chicago Manual of Style](https://www.chicagomanualofstyle.org/qanda/data/faq/topics/Usage/faq0053.html) recommend replacing "man-made" with gender-neutral alternatives when writing professionally. Instead of man-made, you can use terms like **synthetic, manufactured, machine-made, constructed, or human-made**. \[[1](https://www.chicagomanualofstyle.org/qanda/data/faq/topics/Usage/faq0053.html)]
