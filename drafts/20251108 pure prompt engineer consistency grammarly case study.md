That's a great question! Grammarly is an AI-powered writing assistant with a broad range of features, which vary slightly between its free and paid versions.

Here is an overview of the key features, often categorized by the type of assistance they provide:

## ‚úçÔ∏è Core Writing Assistance (Available in Free & Paid)

These are the fundamental features that have made Grammarly popular:

- **Grammar Checker:** Detects and suggests corrections for grammatical errors, including verb tense, subject-verb agreement, and modifier placement.
- **Spelling Checker:** Catches misspellings and provides correct alternatives.
- **Punctuation Checker:** Suggests fixes for comma usage, apostrophes, semi-colons, and more.
- **Conciseness & Clarity:** Offers suggestions to eliminate wordiness and make your sentences clearer and easier to read.
- **Tone Detector:** Analyzes your text in real-time to identify the tone (e.g., friendly, formal, concerned) and helps you ensure it aligns with your intent.
- **Integration:** Works across multiple platforms, including browser extensions (for web writing, Google Docs, etc.), desktop apps (for Windows/Mac), and a Microsoft Office add-in.
- **Custom Dictionary:** Allows you to add specialized vocabulary, names, or jargon so Grammarly doesn't flag them as errors.
- **Dialect Options:** Lets you choose between different English dialects (e.g., American, British, Canadian, Australian English).

---

## üöÄ Advanced & AI-Powered Features (Primarily in Paid Plans)

Grammarly's paid subscriptions (often called **Grammarly Pro** or **Premium**) unlock more sophisticated tools:

- **Generative AI (GrammarlyGo):** This AI co-writer can help you:
  - **Draft** new content from a simple prompt.
  - **Rewrite** or rephrase full paragraphs or sentences for tone, clarity, or length.
  - **Generate ideas** or outlines.
  - **Compose quick email replies.**
- **Plagiarism Checker:** Scans your text against billions of web pages and academic databases to ensure your work is original and provides citation suggestions.
- **Vocabulary Enhancement:** Provides suggestions for more impactful or precise word choices to make your writing more engaging.
- **Full-Sentence Rewrites:** Offers one-click suggestions for completely restructuring complex or confusing sentences for maximum clarity.
- **Genre-Specific Suggestions:** Provides tailored feedback based on the type of writing you select (e.g., academic, business, creative).
- **Advanced Clarity & Delivery:** Suggestions to improve the flow, formality, and overall impact of your message, ensuring the right tone for your audience.
- **AI Detector:** Can analyze text to identify if it was likely generated by AI.
- **Citation Tools:** Helps generate and format citations in various styles (like APA, MLA, Chicago).

That's an important question, especially for writers working with Asian languages.

The short answer is: **Grammarly's core grammar and spelling checking features are not designed to support Chinese, Japanese, or Korean (CJK) as primary languages for correction.**

However, Grammarly has significantly expanded its features to include CJK languages in a different capacity: **Translation**.

Here is a breakdown:

### ‚ùå Core Correction (Grammar, Clarity, Tone)

- **No CJK Support:** Grammarly's main function is to provide detailed, real-time feedback on grammar, spelling, and style. Currently, this core function is limited to **English** and a few major **European languages** (Spanish, French, German, Italian, and Portuguese).
- **The Limitation:** CJK languages have fundamentally different grammatical structures and rely on characters rather than alphabetical spelling. Grammarly's algorithms are not built to parse and correct these systems.

### ‚úÖ Translation Feature (Using Generative AI)

- **Supported for Translation:** Grammarly's premium **AI translation tool** _does_ support CJK languages.
- **How it Works:** Using this feature, you can **translate** text you have written in English (or one of the other supported languages) **into** Chinese, Japanese, or Korean, and vice versa.

| Feature Type                         | Status for CJK (Chinese, Japanese, Korean)                   |
| :----------------------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Real-time Grammar/Spelling Check** | **Not Supported** (Only English and 5 European languages)    |
| **Generative AI Translation**        | **Supported** (You can translate text from/to CJK languages) |

So, while you can use Grammarly to write an email in English and instantly translate a section into Korean, you cannot use it to correct the grammar or check the style of an essay written entirely in Japanese.
