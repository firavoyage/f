### Top Recommendations:

1. **Claude (by Anthropic)**

   - **Why it's great for EPUB**: Direct upload of EPUB files (up to 100 pages for visual analysis, unlimited text extraction). It excels at deep analysis, chapter breakdowns, summaries, and creative tasks like rewriting or discussing themes. Handles formatting well without losing structure.
   - **Access**: Free tier (limited uploads); Pro ($20/month) for unlimited. Available on claude.ai or apps.
   - **Best for**: In-depth book discussions or academic analysis.
   - **How to use**: Upload your EPUB in a new chat, then ask questions like "Summarize Chapter 3" or "What themes recur in this book?"

2. **ChatGPT (by OpenAI)**

   - **Why it's great for EPUB**: Supports EPUB uploads in the Plus tier. You can analyze entire books, generate summaries, answer queries, or even translate chapters. Convert EPUB to text/DOCX first if needed (via free tools like Calibre).
   - **Access**: Free for basic text input; Plus ($20/month) for file uploads. On chatgpt.com or apps.
   - **Best for**: Quick summaries, creative writing extensions, or casual Q&A.
   - **Tip**: For free workarounds, extract text from EPUB using Calibre and paste it inâ€”ChatGPT handles large texts well.

3. **Gemini (by Google)**

   - **Why it's great for EPUB**: Upload EPUB directly for analysis, including image extraction from illustrated books. Great for multimodal tasks (e.g., discussing book covers or diagrams) and integrates with Google Drive for easy access.
   - **Access**: Free tier (limited); Advanced ($20/month) for more uploads. On gemini.google.com.
   - **Best for**: Visual books or research with integrated search.

4. **Microsoft Copilot**
   - **Why it's great for EPUB**: Supports EPUB uploads in Copilot Pro, with strong integration for Office apps (e.g., export analysis to Word). Excellent for professional summaries, data extraction, or business book reviews.
   - **Access**: Free basic version; Pro ($20/month) for uploads. On copilot.microsoft.com or Bing.
   - **Best for**: Productivity-focused users.
