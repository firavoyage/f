the history of lego

The history of LEGO <mark style="margin: 0px; text-decoration-style: solid; text-decoration-color: rgb(230, 232, 240); border-bottom: 0px none rgb(230, 232, 240);">began in <strong>1932</strong> in the carpentry workshop of <strong>Ole Kirk Kristiansen</strong> in Billund, Denmark</mark>. The company’s name is an abbreviation of the Danish words "_Leg Godt_", meaning "**Play Well**". \[[1](https://www.lego.com/en-us/aboutus/lego-group/the-lego-group-history), [2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ole%5FKirk%5FChristiansen), [3](https://www.lego.com/en-us/history/articles/lego-system-in-play)]

The Early Years (1930s–1940s)

Originally a furniture maker, Kristiansen pivoted to producing high-quality wooden toys like pull-along ducks and yo-yos during the Great Depression. His core philosophy, "**Only the best is good enough**," remains the company's motto today. \[[1](https://www.lego.com/en-us/history/articles/b-the-beginning-of-the-lego-group), [2](https://www.lego.com/en-us/aboutus/news/2026/february/90-year-history-of-the-lego-name), [3](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xWu%5FK3%5FyAcw), [4](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ole%5FKirk%5FChristiansen)]

- **1947**: LEGO became the first toy manufacturer in Denmark to purchase a **plastic injection molding machine**.
- **1949**: The company launched its first plastic bricks, then called "**Automatic Binding Bricks**," inspired by British Kiddicraft self-locking bricks. \[[1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History%5Fof%5FLego), [2](https://www.lego.com/en-us/history/articles/c-entering-the-age-of-plastics), [3](https://www.lego.com/en-us/history/articles/c-automatic-binding-bricks), [4](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ole%5FKirk%5FChristiansen)]

The Modern Brick & System (1950s–1970s) \[[1](https://www.facebook.com/EUIPO.eu/posts/on-this-day-in-1978-lego-released-their-first-minifigures-the-smiling-yellow-hum/1196273535860913/), [2](https://www.screenhub.com.au/news/features/lego-emphasises-importance-of-star-wars-in-launch-of-new-smart-play-system-2691491/), [3](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Yrr-YVxMYcY\&t=322)]

The pivotal moment for the modern LEGO brand occurred in the 1950s when the focus shifted to a "System of Play". \[[1](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qr%5FdTySMl7s\&t=581), [2](https://www.lego.com/en-us/history/articles/lego-system-in-play)]

- **1958**: The modern LEGO brick was patented with its unique **stud-and-tube design**, which provides the "clutch power" needed for stable builds.
- **1968**: The first LEGOLAND Park opened in Billund, Denmark.
- **1969**: The [LEGO DUPLO](https://www.lego.com/en-us/history/articles/e-further-development-of-the-system) system launched for younger children.
- **1978**: The iconic **LEGO minifigure** with movable arms and legs was introduced. \[[1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History%5Fof%5FLego), [2](https://www.officetimeline.com/blog/lego-history-timeline), [3](https://www.lego.com/en-us/history/articles/e-further-development-of-the-system), [4](https://www.librarypoint.org/blogs/post/history-of-lego/), [5](https://www.lego.com/en-us/history/articles/f-role-play), [6](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ole%5FKirk%5FChristiansen)]

Expansion & Recovery (1980s–Present) \[[1](https://www.legolanddiscoverycenter.com/michigan/post/lego-facts/)]

After decades of growth, LEGO faced a severe financial crisis in the early 2000s due to over-expansion and competition from video games. \[[1](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SahiRr2BkW4\&t=705), [2](https://www.officetimeline.com/blog/lego-history-timeline)]

- **1999**: The first licensed theme, [LEGO Star Wars](https://www.lego.com/en-us/aboutus/lego-group/the-lego-group-history), launched and became a major commercial success.
- **2003–2004**: Near bankruptcy, the company refocused on its core brick and streamlined production.
- **2014**: _The LEGO Movie_ premiered, turning the brand into a cultural media phenomenon.
- **Today**: LEGO is one of the world's largest toy manufacturers, still owned by the **Kirk Kristiansen family**. \[[1](https://www.britannica.com/topic/LEGO), [2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lego), [3](https://www.lego.com/en-us/aboutus/lego-group/the-lego-group-history), [4](https://www.librarypoint.org/blogs/post/history-of-lego/), [5](https://www.officetimeline.com/blog/lego-history-timeline), [6](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SahiRr2BkW4\&t=705)]
