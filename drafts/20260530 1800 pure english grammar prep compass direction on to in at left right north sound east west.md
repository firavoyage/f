Choosing the correct preposition with directions—**left, right, north, south, east, and west**—depends entirely on whether you are describing static location, relative position, or movement. \[[1](https://www.studysmarter.co.uk/explanations/english/english-grammar/prepositions-of-place/), [2](https://www.vaia.com/en-us/textbooks/math/trigonometry-8-edition/chapter-2/problem-12-draw-vectors-representing-the-following-velocitie/), [3](https://www.scribd.com/document/909393405/ARTICLES), [4](https://www.curiousjr.com/blogs/prepositions-of-movement), [5](https://www.wyzant.com/resources/answers/709602/precalculus-math-problem)]

Here is the quick-reference guide to using **on, to, in, and at** correctly with directions.

***

1\. Left and Right

| Preposition \[[1](https://forum.wordreference.com/threads/at-on-to-the-right-side-or-the-left-side.2466506/), [2](https://www.speakconfidentenglish.com/prepositions-of-place/), [3](https://speakuplondon.com/how-to-use-prepositions-at-on-and-in-simple-guide), [4](https://allen.in/english/prepositions), [5](https://www.thoughtco.com/english-prepositions-of-place-4427975)] | When to Use                                                                                         | Examples                                                                                                    |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **On the left / right**                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              | Static location; describing where something sits relative to another object.                        | \* The kitchen is **on the right**.\* He sat **on the left** side of the couch.                             |
| **To the left / right**                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              | Direction, movement, or something further out in the distance.                                      | \* Turn **to the right** at the sign.\* The mountains lie **to the left** of the city.                      |
| **At the left / right**                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              | Specific points on an object, page, or document (often used as "at the left of...").                | \* Sign your name **at the bottom right** of the page.\* The menu is located **at the left** of the screen. |
| **In the left / right**                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              | _Incorrect_ for general location, but used if something is physically inside a pocket or container. | \* The keys are **in the right** pocket of my jacket.                                                       |

2\. Compass Directions (North, South, East, West)

| Preposition \[[1](https://dictionary.cambridge.org/us/grammar/british-grammar/at-in-and-to-movement), [2](https://learnenglish.britishcouncil.org/free-resources/grammar/a1-a2/prepositions-place), [3](https://speakuplondon.com/how-to-use-prepositions-at-on-and-in-simple-guide), [4](https://forum.wordreference.com/threads/at-on-to-the-right-side-or-the-left-side.2466506/), [5](https://milausa.com/prepositions-in-on-and-at-learn-how-to-use-them/)] | When to Use                                                    | Examples                                                                                             |
| ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **In the north / south...**                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      | **Inside** a specific country, region, or city.                | \* It snows a lot **in the north** of Italy.\* We live **in the southwest** of France.               |
| **To the north / south...**                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      | **Outside** of the area; in the direction of a external place. | \* Canada is **to the north** of the United States.\* Drive **to the east** until you hit the coast. |
| **On the north / south...**                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      | Bordering a line, river, coast, or specific side of a street.  | \* The hotel is **on the north** side of the river.\* They live **on the east** coast.               |
| **At the north / south...**                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      | At a precise point, extreme tip, or absolute edge.             | \* Penguins live **at the South** Pole.\* The security guard stands **at the north** gate.           |

***

Summary Rule of Thumb

- Use **IN** for things inside a boundary.
- Use **TO** for movement or things outside a boundary.
- Use **ON** for surfaces, sides, streets, or coastlines.
- Use **AT** for exact pinpoint locations. \[[1](https://dictionary.cambridge.org/us/grammar/british-grammar/at-in-and-to-movement), [2](https://speakuplondon.com/how-to-use-prepositions-at-on-and-in-simple-guide), [3](https://forum.wordreference.com/threads/at-on-to-the-right-side-or-the-left-side.2466506/), [4](https://www.speakconfidentenglish.com/prepositions-of-place/), [5](https://learnenglish.britishcouncil.org/free-resources/grammar/a1-a2/prepositions-place)]
