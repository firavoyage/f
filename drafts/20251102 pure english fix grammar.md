> "list roles of a llm, which each focus on a separated task of a good software engineer"

### Breakdown of the Grammar Issues:

- **"list roles of a llm"**: "LLM" is an acronym and should be capitalized. Since "LLM" starts with a vowel sound ("el"), the article should be "an," not "a." It's also more natural to use "the" before "roles."

- **"a separated task"**: The adjective "separated" is not correct here. The correct word is "separate." "Separated" is the past participle of the verb "to separate" (e.g., "The pieces were separated"). "Separate" is the adjective meaning "individual and distinct."
- **"of a good software engineer"**: This is acceptable, but phrases like "performed by a..." or "of a skilled..." can sound more professional.

In short, any of the corrected versions above will be grammatically sound and clear.

3.  The subject of the verb "focuses" is the plural **"which."** Therefore, the verb must also be plural: **"focus."**

So, the core mistake is:

- **Incorrect:** "...roles, which each **focuses**..." (A plural subject with a singular verb).

**Option 1: Use the standard formal structure "each of which"**

> "List the roles of an LLM, **each of which focuses** on a separate task..."

> "List the roles of an LLM, **with each focusing** on a separate task..."
