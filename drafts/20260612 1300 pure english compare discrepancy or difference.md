discrepancy or difference

The main difference is that <mark style="margin: 0px; text-decoration-style: solid; text-decoration-color: rgb(230, 232, 240); border-bottom: 0px none rgb(230, 232, 240);">a <strong>difference</strong> is a neutral comparison between any two things, while a <strong>discrepancy</strong> is an unexpected or problematic mismatch between things that <em style="margin: 0px; text-decoration-style: solid; text-decoration-color: rgb(230, 232, 240); border-bottom: 0px none rgb(230, 232, 240);">should</em> be identical</mark>. \[[1](https://www.quora.com/Is-there-any-distinction-among-these-words-difference-discrepancy-or-dissimilarity-If-so-what-is-it), [2](https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/discrepancy)]

### When to Use "Difference"

Use this word when you are simply pointing out that two things are not the same. It does not imply that anything is wrong or broken. \[[1](https://www.quora.com/Is-there-any-distinction-among-these-words-difference-discrepancy-or-dissimilarity-If-so-what-is-it), [2](https://english.stackexchange.com/questions/627957/discrepancy-just-as-difference-or-difference-between-accounts)]

- _Example:_ There is a big **difference** between a cat and a dog.
- _Example:_ What is the **difference** between these two car models? \[[1](https://dictionary.cambridge.org/us/dictionary/english/discrepancy)]

### When to Use "Discrepancy"

Use this word when a variation points to an error, conflict, or inconsistency. It suggests that something might be wrong and requires an explanation. \[[1](https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/discrepancy), [2](https://www.collinsdictionary.com/us/dictionary/english/discrepancy), [3](https://www.vocabulary.com/dictionary/discrepancy)]

- _Example:_ The accountant found a $500 **discrepancy** between the receipt and the cash register.
- _Example:_ There were major **discrepancies** between the two witness statements. \[[1](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1s10V3Fc-d4\&t=1), [2](https://www.vocabulary.com/dictionary/discrepancy), [3](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xtQD-Bj3IBs), [4](https://www.britannica.com/dictionary/discrepancy)]
