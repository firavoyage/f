compare character and personality

**“Character”** and **“personality”** are related but not the same. Here’s a clear comparison:

---

### 1. Basic meaning

- **Personality**: The natural patterns of thinking, feeling, and behaving that make someone unique.
- **Character**: A person’s moral and ethical qualities—how they choose to act, especially when it matters.

---

### 2. Source

- **Personality**: Largely innate and shaped early by biology and temperament.
- **Character**: Developed over time through values, choices, experiences, and discipline.

---

### 3. Stability

- **Personality**: Relatively stable; it changes slowly.
- **Character**: More flexible; it can improve or deteriorate based on decisions.

---

### 4. Focus

- **Personality**: _How_ someone behaves.
- **Character**: _Why_ someone behaves that way (right vs. wrong).

---

### 5. Evaluation

- **Personality**: Descriptive, not judged as good or bad
  (e.g., introverted, outgoing, analytical).
- **Character**: Evaluative and moral
  (e.g., honest, responsible, compassionate).

---

### 6. Examples

- A person may have a **shy personality** but **strong character** (honest, reliable).
- Someone may have a **charming personality** but **weak character** (dishonest, selfish).

---

### In short

> **Personality is who you are naturally.
> Character is who you choose to be.**
