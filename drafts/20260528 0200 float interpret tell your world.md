tell your world meaning

**"Tell Your World" is a legendary VOCALOID electronic pop song composed by kz of livetune and sung by Hatsune Miku**<mark style="margin: 0px; text-decoration-style: solid; text-decoration-color: rgb(230, 232, 240); border-bottom: 0px none rgb(230, 232, 240);"><strong>, and its core meaning is about using the internet and music to share your unique, personal story with others and connect the world</strong></mark>**.** Originally written for a [Japanese Google Chrome commercial](https://vocaloid.fandom.com/wiki/Tell%5FYour%5FWorld) in 2011, the song serves as a massive celebration of internet culture, human connection, and user-generated creativity. \[[1](https://project-diva.fandom.com/wiki/Tell%5FYour%5FWorld), [2](https://vocaloid.fandom.com/wiki/Tell%5FYour%5FWorld), [3](https://otakumode.com/otapedia/vocaloid/hatsune%5Fmiku/tell%5Fyour%5Fworld)]

Key Themes & Meaning

- **The Power of Individual Creativity:** The song encourages every listener to realize that they have a unique "world" (their thoughts, art, music, or feelings) inside them that deserves to be shared.
- **The "Dot to Line" Metaphor:** The lyrics repeatedly use a geometric metaphor where a single thought or note starts as a "dot". When shared online, these dots connect to form "lines" that eventually loop into a circle, bringing different people together regardless of physical distance.
- **The Spirit of the VOCALOID Community:** Because [Hatsune Miku](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hatsune%5FMiku) is an instrument powered by thousands of different anonymous creators, the song acts as her "thank you" theme song. It celebrates how individual musicians, illustrators, and fans built an entire creative universe around her.
- **Digital Connection:** Producer kz stated he wrote the track to capture the feelings of hope, excitement, and pure happiness that come from utilizing the internet to link human hearts across the globe. \[[1](https://www.reddit.com/r/Vocaloid/comments/1a4shn/tell%5Fyour%5Fworld%5Fofficial%5Fenglish%5Fversion%5Flivetune/), [2](https://vocalife.wordpress.com/2012/04/09/tell-your-world/), [3](https://vocaloid.fandom.com/wiki/%E3%83%AF%E3%83%BC%E3%83%AB%E3%83%89%E3%82%A4%E3%82%BA%E3%83%9E%E3%82%A4%E3%83%B3%5F%28World%5Fis%5FMine%29), [4](https://otakumode.com/otapedia/vocaloid/hatsune%5Fmiku/tell%5Fyour%5Fworld), [5](https://project-diva.fandom.com/wiki/Tell%5FYour%5FWorld), [6](https://vocaloid.fandom.com/wiki/Tell%5FYour%5FWorld)]

\[Verse 1] Breaking the Mold

- **形のない気持ち忘れないように (So I don't forget these shapeless feelings)**
  - _Meaning:_ Creative inspiration starts as something vague, fragile, and emotional. The artist feels an urgent need to capture it before it fades away.
- **決まりきったレイアウトを消した (I erased the standard, pre-made layout)**
  - _Meaning:_ This is a direct nod to digital creation (like clearing a default template on a computer screen). It represents breaking away from generic rules and conformity to create something truly original.
- **ふと口ずさんだフレーズを掴まえて (I catch the phrase I accidentally hummed aloud)**
  - _Meaning:_ Capturing a sudden spark of musical inspiration or a fleeting thought before it disappears into thin air.
- **胸に秘めた言葉乗せ空に解き放つの (I load it with the words hidden in my heart, and release it into the sky)**
  - _Meaning:_ Taking those private, vulnerable thoughts, turning them into a song, and uploading them ("into the sky/cloud") for the world to hear.

\[Chorus 1] The Geometric Metaphor of Connection

- **君に伝えたいことが / 君に届けたいことが (The things I want to tell you / The things I want to reach you)**
  - _Meaning:_ The driving force behind creating art is the desperate, honest desire to communicate and be understood by someone else.
- **たくさんの点は線になって (Countless dots become lines)**
  - _Meaning:_ A single person or song is just an isolated "dot" on the internet. But as people listen and share, those individual dots connect to form "lines" of human connection.
- **遠く彼方へと響く (Echoing far into the distance)**
  - _Meaning:_ Once a creation is online, it transcends physical borders and reaches people thousands of miles away.
- **君に伝えたい言葉 / 君に届けたい音が (The words I want to tell you / The sounds I want to reach you)**
  - _Meaning:_ Reinforcing the message that both the literal lyrics (words) and the emotion of the music (sounds) carry the creator's soul.
- **いくつもの線は円になって (Those many lines become a circle)**
  - _Meaning:_ The connections grow so vast that they loop back around, creating a massive, global community where everyone is joined together.
- **全て繋げてく どこにだって, ah-ah (Connecting everything, absolutely everywhere)**
  - _Meaning:_ A celebration of the internet’s ultimate power: total, boundary-free unity through art.

\[Verse 2] The Spark of Collaboration

- **真っ白に澄んだ光は君のよう (The pure, pure white light is just like you)**
  - _Meaning:_ "You" refers to the listener, the fan, or a blank digital canvas full of infinite, uncorrupted potential.
- **かざした手の隙間を伝う声が (The voice traveling through the gaps of my held-up hand)**
  - _Meaning:_ Visualizing a singer or creator reaching out toward the screen or the stage, trying to bridge the gap between the virtual world and reality.
- **ふと動いた指先刻むリズムに (Into the rhythm tapped out by suddenly moving fingertips)**
  - _Meaning:_ The physical act of creation—typing on a keyboard, playing a piano, or drawing on a tablet when inspiration strikes.
- **ありったけの言葉乗せ空に解き放つの (I load it with every single word I have, and release it into the sky)**
  - _Meaning:_ Pouring 100% of your current self into your art and putting it out into the digital cosmos without holding back.

\[Chorus 2] Making an Impact

_(Note: This chorus is identical to the first, except for one crucial word change in line 4)_

- **遠く彼方まで穿つ (Piercing through, far into the distance)**
  - _Meaning:_ Instead of just "echoing" (響く), the song now uses "piercing/boring through" (穿つ). This signifies that the collective voice of the community is no longer just a faint sound—it is powerful enough to break through barriers and leave a permanent mark on the world.

\[Bridge] Awakening to Your Own Power

- **奏でていた変わらない日々を疑わずに (Playing out the same unchanging days without a single doubt)**
  - _Meaning:_ Living a mundane, passive, autopilot life, assuming things will always just stay the routine standard.
- **朝は誰かがくれるものだと思っていた (Thinking that the morning was just something given to me by someone else)**
  - _Meaning:_ Believing that you are passive in your own life, waiting for inspiration or permission to happen _to_ you, rather than making it happen yourself.
- **一瞬でも信じた音 景色を揺らすの (The sound I believed in, even for a moment, shakes the entire scenery)**
  - _Meaning:_ The moment you actually trust your own creative voice, it completely shatters your old, boring reality. Your perspective on life violently shifts for the better.
- **教えてよ 君だけの世界 (Show me—tell me about—your very own world)**
  - _Meaning:_ Miku (or the creator) turns to _you_, the audience. The song is no longer about _their_ creation; they are begging _you_ to share _your_ unique creativity now.

\[Final Chorus] The Ultimate Shift

_(Note: A massive grammatical shift happens here. "Kimi **ni**" (To you) changes to "Kimi **ga**" (What YOU...))_

- **君が伝えたいことは / 君が届けたいことは (The things YOU want to tell / The things YOU want to reach)**
  - _Meaning:_ The song officially passes the torch to the listener. It is no longer Miku singing _to_ you; she is singing _about_ you.
- **たくさんの点は線になって / 遠く彼方へと響く (Countless dots become lines / Echoing far into the distance)**
  - _Meaning:_ Your voice, your art, and your thoughts are now the dots connecting the world.
- **君が伝えたい言葉 / 君が届けたい音は (The words YOU want to tell / The sounds YOU want to reach)**
  - _Meaning:_ Validation that your specific thoughts and your specific creative expressions matter.
- **いくつもの線は円になって / 全て繋げてく どこにだって, ah-ah, ahhh (Those many lines become a circle / Connecting everything, absolutely everywhere)**
  - _Meaning:_ The song ends on a triumphant loop. By sharing "your world," you complete the circle of global creativity, proving that everyone on the internet has the power to connect humanity.

You said: symbolism

The official music video for "Tell Your World" (animated by Fantasista Utamaro and JNTHED) is a visual masterpiece that translates the song's digital and geometric metaphors into vibrant, pop-art animation.

Here is a breakdown of the key visual elements and their deeper symbolic meanings:

1\. The Dots (点 - Ten)

- **What they look like:** The video opens with floating, multi-colored circles and particles drifting in a void.
- **The Symbolism:** These represent **isolated individuals** or **unshared ideas**. In the vastness of the internet, a creator sitting alone in their bedroom is just a single, lonely dot. It also represents the raw data of a computer—pixels and bits of code waiting to be arranged.

2\. The Lines (線 - Sen)

- **What they look like:** As the music picks up, the dots suddenly sprout sharp, dynamic neon lines that shoot across the screen, connecting one circle to another.
- **The Symbolism:** This represents **the act of sharing and communication**. When you upload a song, a drawing, or a tweet, you are throwing a line out into the digital ether. When someone else likes, reposts, or covers it, a line is drawn between you. It symbolizes the breakdown of physical distance through technology.

3\. The Circles and Spheres (円 - En)

- **What they look like:** The lines eventually intersect so much that they form complex, spinning geometric shapes, globes, and massive interlocking circles around Hatsune Miku.
- **The Symbolism:** This represents **the global community**. The Japanese word for circle (_en_) is a homophone for the word for bond or fate (_en_ / 縁). The video visually demonstrates that millions of individual "lines" of communication naturally wrap around the globe, creating a protective, unified circle of shared human culture.

4\. The Exploding Colors and Paint Splatters

- **What they look like:** The video uses an incredibly bright, neon color palette with chaotic paint splatters and digital abstract art bursting across a sterile white background.
- **The Symbolism:** This represents **the explosion of user-generated creativity**. The "sterile white" represents the boring, "unchanging days" mentioned in the bridge. The sudden bursts of bright color symbolize how human imagination disrupts monotony and breathes vibrant life into the digital space.

5\. Hatsune Miku as the "Nexus" or Conduit

- **What she looks like:** Miku is depicted floating at the center of all these lines. Her iconic twin-tails are often shown dissolving into streams of data, light, and musical staves.
- **The Symbolism:** Miku herself is a symbol of **collective human consciousness**. Because she is a virtual singer with no set personality, she belongs to everyone. The video positions her not as an idol singing _at_ people, but as the central hub (the nexus) through which thousands of different people are channeling their own voices and connecting to one another.
