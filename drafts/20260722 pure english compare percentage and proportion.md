compare percentage and proportion

Percentages and proportions both describe relationships between a part and a whole, but they express that relationship using different numerical scales.

Here is how they compare:

### Key Differences

- **Scale**: Proportions use a 0 to 1 scale. Percentages use a 0 to 100 scale.
- **Format**: Proportions are written as decimals or fractions. Percentages use the `%` symbol.
- **Formula**: Proportion is\
  𝑃𝑎𝑟𝑡\
  /𝑊ℎ𝑜𝑙𝑒\
  . Percentage is\
  (\
  𝑃𝑎𝑟𝑡\
  /𝑊ℎ𝑜𝑙𝑒\
  )\
  ×100\
  .

### Side-by-Side Example

If 4 out of 20 people in a room are left-handed:

- **The Proportion**:\
  4\
  /20\
  \=0.20\
  (or\
  1\
  /5\
  )
- **The Percentage**:

0.20\
×100\
\=20\
%

### When to Use Which

- **Proportions**: Used in probability, statistics, and advanced math formulas.
- **Percentages**: Used in daily life, business reports, and consumer data because they are easier for most people to visualize quickly.
