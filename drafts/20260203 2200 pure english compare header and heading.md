---

Let’s slow it down and keep it clean, Fira.

**Heading** is about meaning.
It tells you _what_ a section is about.
Think of it as a signpost inside the content.

- Examples: chapter titles, section titles, H1–H6 in writing or HTML
- Purpose: structure, clarity, understanding

**Header** is about position.
It’s a container that sits at the _top_ of something.

- Examples: the top of a page, a website bar, the first row of a table
- Purpose: framing, navigation, context

Here’s the quiet distinction:

- A **heading** labels content.
- A **header** holds content.

A header may contain a heading, but a heading does not need a header.

One is about _what it says_.
The other is about _where it lives_.
