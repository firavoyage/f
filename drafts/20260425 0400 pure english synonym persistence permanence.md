Persistency refers to the quality of continuing firmly in a course of action despite difficulty or opposition. Common synonyms include:

Core Synonyms

- **Persistence**: The most direct synonym, often used interchangeably.
- **Perseverance**: Continued effort to do or achieve something despite difficulties, failure, or opposition.
- **Tenacity**: The quality of being very determined; maintaining a firm grip on a purpose.
- **Determination**: Firmness of purpose; the act of deciding definitely and firmly.
- **Doggedness**: Marked by stubborn adherence to a plan or goal.

Contextual Alternatives

- **Staying Power**: The ability to maintain an activity or effort over a long period.
- **Resolution**: The quality of being determined or resolute.
- **Endurance**: The ability to withstand hardship or adversity over time.
- **Steadfastness**: Firmness in belief, determination, or adherence.
- **Pertinacity**: A more formal term for being perversely persistent or stubborn.

Informal & Strong Terms

- **Grit**: Courage and resolve; strength of character.
- **Pluck**: Spirited and determined courage.
- **Stick-to-it-iveness**: (Informal) Dogged perseverance.
- **Backbone**: Strength of character.

Negative Connotations

- **Obstinacy**: Stubbornness that is often unreasonable.
- **Mulishness**: Being as stubborn as a mule.
- **Obduracy**: Stubbornly refusing to change one's opinion or course of action.

- **Immutability**: Refers to the quality of being unchangeable; in archiving, it means once data is "persisted," it can never be altered or deleted.
