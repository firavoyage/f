compare feel (noun) and feeling

While **"feel"** (as a noun) primarily describes external, objective qualities like texture or atmosphere, **"feeling"** refers to internal, subjective experiences such as physical sensations and emotions.

### Key Differences

| Feature           | Feel (Noun)                                        | Feeling (Noun)                                 |
| ----------------- | -------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| **Primary Focus** | External texture, quality, or atmosphere           | Internal emotion or physical sensation         |
| **Source**        | The object or environment being observed           | The person experiencing the state              |
| **Plural Form**   | **"Feels"** (Slang for strong emotional reactions) | **"Feelings"** (Standard English for emotions) |

***

### Deep Dive: "Feel" (Noun)

The noun **feel** describes the tangible, physical properties of an object or the general character of a space. It is something inherent to the item or environment itself. [link](https://www.quora.com/Whats-the-difference-betweenfeeland-feelingwhen-used-as-a-noun-Im-learning-English)

- **Physical Texture**: The tactile quality of a surface when touched.
  - _Example_: "I love the smooth **feel** of silk." [link](https://www.quora.com/Whats-the-difference-betweenfeeland-feelingwhen-used-as-a-noun-Im-learning-English)
- **Atmosphere or Vibe**: The overall mood or aesthetic of a place, design, or situation.
  - _Example_: "The restaurant has a cozy, rustic **feel**." [link](https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/feel)
- **Natural Ability**: An intuitive knack or talent for doing something.
  - _Example_: "She has a great **feel** for playing the piano." [link](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/feel)
- **Slang Usage ("The Feels")**: In casual, modern slang, the plural "feels" refers to an overwhelming wave of emotion.
  - _Example_: "That movie ending gave me all the **feels**." [link](https://www.reddit.com/r/EnglishLearning/comments/xe159s/feel%5For%5Ffeeling/)

***

### Deep Dive: "Feeling" (Noun)

The noun **feeling** describes a person's inner consciousness. It covers emotional states, bodily physical sensations, and intuitive hunches. [link](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Feeling)

- **Emotional State**: A specific, named emotion.
  - _Example_: "A **feeling** of joy washed over her." [link](https://giggleacademy.com/blog/learning/grammar/what-is-the-difference-between-feel-feeling-feeler-and-feelings)
- **Physical Sensation**: Bodily awareness that comes through the nervous system.
  - _Example_: "He lost all **feeling** in his cold toes." [link](https://giggleacademy.com/blog/learning/grammar/what-is-the-difference-between-feel-feeling-feeler-and-feelings)
- **Intuition or Belief**: A vague belief, hunch, or general impression about the future.
  - _Example_: "I have a bad **feeling** about this project." [link](https://www.dictionary.com/browse/feeling)
- **Standard Plural ("Feelings")**: The standard grammatical way to refer to a person's emotional well-being or sensitivities.
  - _Example_: "Be careful not to hurt his **feelings**." [link](https://giggleacademy.com/blog/learning/grammar/what-is-the-difference-between-feel-feeling-feeler-and-feelings)

### Scannable Comparison Guide

- **Use "feel"** if you are describing the properties of a product, a piece of clothing, or a room's design layout.
- **Use "feeling"** if you are describing your health, your mood, an intuition, or an emotional response. [link](https://giggleacademy.com/blog/learning/grammar/what-is-the-difference-between-feel-feeling-feeler-and-feelings)
