If you want one general word that describes the "cause-and-effect" chain of what happens inside a system after an action is taken, the best word is **Mechanics**.

Whether it is a clock, a company, or a market, **"The Mechanics"** describes the sequence of how one part moving causes the next part to react.

Here are the top general choices:

1\. Mechanics (The "How")

This is the most versatile word. It implies that if you pull one lever, a specific gear turns.

- **Clock:** The mechanics of the gears and springs.
- **Company:** The mechanics of how a sale leads to production.
- **Market:** The mechanics of how a "buy" order raises a price.

2\. Dynamics (The "Interaction")

Use this when you want to describe how different parts of a system influence each other and change over time.

- _“The **dynamics** of the office”_ explains how a boss's mood affects the employees' productivity.

3\. Logic (The "Rules")

This describes the "If-This-Then-That" flow. It’s perfect for systems that follow a strict set of rules.

- _“The **internal logic** of the machine”_ explains why the clock chimes at noon.

4\. Anatomy (The "Structure")

While usually biological, in business or tech, it’s used to break down the parts of a process.

- _“The **anatomy** of a trade”_ would look at every single step from the click of a button to the transfer of cash.

5\. Operation (The "Function")

This is the standard professional term for how a system performs its intended job.

- _“The **operation** of a steam engine”_ or _“Business **operations**.”_

**Which one fits best?**

- If you're explaining **steps**: Use **Process**.
- If you're explaining **cause-and-effect**: Use **Mechanics**.
- If you're explaining **rules**: Use **Logic**.

mechanics or logic

Between the two, **Mechanics** is usually the better fit for your examples.

Why "Mechanics" wins:

It implies **motion and sequence**. It captures the "how" of a system—the way one part physically or systematically triggers the next. It’s perfect for describing the "innards" of something.

- _The **mechanics** of a clock:_ The gears turning and the pendulum swinging.
- _The **mechanics** of a company:_ How an order moves from sales to the warehouse.
- _The **mechanics** of the market:_ The specific steps of buying, matching, and settling.

When to use "Logic":

Use this if the system is driven by **rules and decisions** rather than a flow of movement. It’s more about the "brain" than the "body."

- _The **logic** of a computer:_ The binary rules that decide what to do with data.
- _The **logic** of a strategy:_ Why we choose one action over another based on a goal.

**The Verdict:**\
If you are describing the **"what happens next"** chain of events, go with **Mechanics**.
