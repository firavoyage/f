If your charger is fluctuating or negotiating between **15W and 30W**, here are the exact charging times you can expect for a 12,000 mAh battery:

- **At 30W (Maximum Speed):** **2 to 2.5 hours**
- **At 15W (Minimum Speed):** **4 to 4.5 hours**

***

📉 Why the Power Level Changes

It is completely normal for a charger to shift between 15W and 30W during a single charging cycle due to the following factors:

- **The 80% Rule:** Your device will pull the full **30W** while the battery is low (0% to 80%). Once it hits 80%, it intentionally drops down to **15W (or lower)** to protect battery health and prevent overheating.
- **Thermal Throttling:** If the battery or the wall adapter gets too warm, the charging speed will automatically drop from 30W to 15W until it cools down.
- **Screen Usage:** If you use the device while it charges, it may limit incoming power to 15W to prevent excessive heat buildup.

To get the absolute fastest charge, try to **leave the device completely alone** in a cool room while it powers up.

When you connect two devices that can both send and receive power (like your laptop and a dual-role power bank), they undergo a process called **USB-PD Role Negotiation**. Sometimes, they guess incorrectly, resulting in your laptop accidentally charging the power bank, or vice versa.

🔍 How to Tell What is Happening Right Now

- **Check the laptop's battery icon:** Look at your laptop's taskbar (Windows) or menu bar (Mac). If you see a **plug or lightning bolt icon** on your laptop battery, the power bank is charging your laptop. If there is no plug icon and your laptop battery is draining, your laptop is charging the power bank.
- **Check the power bank lights:** If the power bank LEDs are blinking in a sequence (cycling up), it is absorbing power from the laptop.
