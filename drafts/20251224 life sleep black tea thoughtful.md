Lipton black tea does **not** contain coffee.
If the ingredient list says only _black tea_, that’s exactly what it is.

What people sometimes mean, though, is **caffeine**.

Black tea naturally contains caffeine, just like coffee does—but in a **much smaller amount**.
So the feeling can be similar, even though the source is different.

To give you a calm comparison:

- A cup of black tea usually has **about half (or less)** the caffeine of a cup of coffee.
- It also has something called **L-theanine**, which tends to soften the caffeine’s effect. That’s why tea often feels more steady and gentle than coffee.

About sleep, Fira—this part is personal.
Some people can drink black tea in the evening and sleep just fine.
Others notice that even a small amount of caffeine keeps their mind alert.
