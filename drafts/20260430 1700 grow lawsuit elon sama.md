sama elon case

The **Elon Musk v. OpenAI** trial involving Sam Altman began in an Oakland, California federal court on **April 28, 2026**. Musk is suing Altman, Greg Brockman, and Microsoft, alleging they betrayed the company's original non-profit mission in favor of commercial gain.

Core Claims & Demands

- **"Stole a Charity"**: Musk’s legal team argues that Altman and Brockman effectively looted a public charity by pivoting OpenAI to a for-profit structure.
- **Breach of Agreement**: Musk alleges his co-founders broke a foundational agreement to develop AI for the benefit of humanity rather than for profit.
- **Financial Redress**: Musk is seeking roughly **$150 billion in damages**, which his lawyers recently suggested should be returned to OpenAI’s non-profit arm.
- **Leadership Removal**: The suit demands the removal of Sam Altman and Greg Brockman from their leadership roles.

Defense Arguments

- **Resource Necessity**: OpenAI’s attorneys from [The Verge](https://www.theverge.com/tech/917225/sam-altman-elon-musk-openai-lawsuit) argue the shift was necessary to secure the massive capital required for advanced AI research.
- **Musk's Motivation**: The defense claims Musk is motivated by a desire to control the company and benefit his own rival AI firm, **xAI**.

Trial Highlights (Current Week)

- **Musk on the Stand**: On April 28 and 29, Musk testified that he felt like a "fool" for his early funding and accused Altman's lawyers of trying to "trick" him during cross-examination.
- **Key Witnesses**: The court expects testimony from Microsoft CEO **Satya Nadella** and former OpenAI leaders like **Mira Murati**.
- **Personal Tension**: Documents revealed petty exchanges, including Musk's frustration over not being included in an anniversary photo and Altman’s claims that Musk was acting from a position of "insecurity".

The trial is overseen by **Judge Yvonne Gonzalez Rogers** and a nine-person jury, and it is expected to last approximately three weeks.

criticism

The ongoing trial is thick with criticism directed at both Elon Musk and Sam Altman, as well as the nature of the lawsuit itself.

Criticism of Elon Musk

- **Suspect Motives**: Critics and OpenAI's legal team argue the suit is a ["vengeful attack"](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2026/apr/28/sam-altman-open-ai-elon-musk-trial) by a competitor. They claim Musk is motivated by "jealousy" because his own AI firm, **xAI**, is lagging behind and because he failed to take total control of OpenAI in 2018.
- **Contradictory Past**: Legal experts point out that Musk once [proposed merging OpenAI with Tesla](https://www.reddit.com/r/agi/comments/1syio8g/some%5Fthoughts%5Fon%5Fthe%5Fopening%5Fstatements%5Fof%5Fthe/) to create a for-profit powerhouse, which critics say undermines his current stance as a "defender" of the non-profit mission.
- **Courtroom Conduct**: During his testimony on April 29, Musk was admonished by Judge Yvonne Gonzalez Rogers for his "propensity" to attack Altman on social media (calling him "Scam Altman") while the trial was active.
- **Unreliable Narrator**: OpenAI’s lawyers have highlighted Musk’s [alleged ketamine use](https://www.theatlantic.com/technology/2026/04/openai-trial-elon-musk-sam-altman/686984/) during past negotiations, suggesting it may have impaired his memory of the "founding agreements" he now claims were breached.

Criticism of Sam Altman & OpenAI

- **Lack of Candor**: Former OpenAI board members and scientists, such as **Ilya Sutskever**, have expressed deep distrust in Altman. Internal memos leaked during discovery allege a [**"consistent pattern of lying"**](https://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2026/04/13/sam-altman-may-control-our-future-can-he-be-trusted) and misleading leadership to bypass safety protocols.
- **"Looting" a Charity**: Musk’s legal team has popularized the phrase "stole a charity" to describe the shift from a public-good non-profit to a commercial entity valued at nearly $1 trillion.
- **Ethical Hollowess**: Critics argue that Altman’s calls for AI safety and democratic oversight ring ["hollow"](https://www.platformer.news/sam-altman-ai-backlash/) while he simultaneously uses the company’s vast resources to fight off regulatory efforts and legal accountability.

Criticism of the Lawsuit Itself

- **Wrong Legal Basis**: Some legal scholars from [UCLA](https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/04/27/1136466/elon-musk-and-sam-altman-are-going-to-court-over-openais-future/) suggest the case is being fought under the **law of trusts** when it should be considered under the **law of charitable non-profit organizations**, calling the entire proceeding "legally muddy".
- **Public Disservice**: The California attorney general declined to join the suit, stating it did not serve the public interest and was more of a private "toxic feud" between billionaires than a legitimate protection of charitable assets.
