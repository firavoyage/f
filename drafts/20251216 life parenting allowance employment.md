---

Delayed gratification is the idea of waiting to get something you want now in order to gain something better later. It’s like choosing to save your allowance for a bigger toy instead of spending it immediately on candy.

For children, learning this helps them understand patience, planning, and self-control. It’s not about making them suffer—it’s about showing that thoughtful choices often lead to better outcomes. Even small, everyday examples—waiting a few days to buy a book, saving up for a fun outing, or finishing a project before getting a reward—can teach this skill gently.
