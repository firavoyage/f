### 3.1: Introduction to Chemical Bonds

Chemical bonds are the attractive and repulsive forces that hold atoms together to form molecules. These bonds arise from the interactions between the electrons of individual atoms, which can lead to the sharing or transfer of electrons between atoms. The type of chemical bond formed depends on the number of electrons shared or transferred between atoms.

### 3.2: Types of Chemical Bonds

There are three main types of chemical bonds: ionic, covalent, and metallic bonds.

#### 3.2.1: Ionic Bonds

Ionic bonds are formed when one or more electrons are transferred from one atom to another, resulting in the formation of ions with opposite charges. The electrostatic attraction between the oppositely charged ions holds them together, forming an ionic bond. Examples of ionic compounds include sodium chloride (NaCl) and calcium carbonate (CaCO3).

#### 3.2.2: Covalent Bonds

Covalent bonds are formed when two or more atoms share one or more pairs of electrons to achieve a stable electronic configuration. This sharing of electrons leads to a strong attraction between the atoms, holding them together in a molecule. Examples of covalent compounds include water (H2O) and methane (CH4).

#### 3.2.3: Metallic Bonds

Metallic bonds are formed in metals, where the electrons are delocalized and free to move throughout the metal lattice. This delocalization of electrons leads to a "sea of electrons" that holds the metal ions together, forming a metallic bond. Examples of metallic compounds include sodium (Na) and copper (Cu).

### 3.3: Molecular Structure

The molecular structure of a substance is determined by the arrangement of its atoms in space. This arrangement is influenced by the type of chemical bonds present and the electronegativity of the atoms involved.

#### 3.3.1: Molecular Shape

The shape of a molecule is determined by the arrangement of its atoms in space. There are several types of molecular shapes, including:

* **Linear**: A molecule with a linear shape has atoms arranged in a straight line.
* **Trigonal planar**: A molecule with a trigonal planar shape has atoms arranged in a plane, with bond angles of 120 degrees.
* **Tetrahedral**: A molecule with a tetrahedral shape has atoms arranged in a three-dimensional tetrahedral shape, with bond angles of 109.5 degrees.

#### 3.3.2: Isomers

Isomers are molecules that have the same molecular formula but differ in their molecular structure. Isomers can have different physical and chemical properties, making them useful in various applications.

#### 3.3.3: Molecular Polarity

Molecular polarity refers to the distribution of electrons within a molecule, which can lead to a separation of charge. This separation of charge can result in a molecule having a dipole moment, which can influence its physical and chemical properties.
