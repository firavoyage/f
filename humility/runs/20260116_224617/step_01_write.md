**Atomic Structure: Introduction to Electrons, Protons, and Neutrons**
==============================================================

### Overview

In this chapter, we will delve into the fascinating world of atomic structure, exploring the fundamental particles that make up atoms. At the heart of every atom lie three main components: electrons, protons, and neutrons. Understanding the properties and behaviors of these particles is crucial for grasping the principles of chemistry.

### Protons: The Positively Charged Nucleus

Protons are positively charged subatomic particles that reside in the nucleus, or center, of an atom. They have a mass of approximately 1 atomic mass unit (amu) and carry a charge of +1 elementary charge. The number of protons in an atom determines the element of an atom, with each element having a unique number of protons in its atomic nucleus. For example, hydrogen has one proton, helium has two protons, and so on.

### Neutrons: The Neutral Nucleus

Neutrons are subatomic particles with no electric charge, residing in the nucleus along with protons. They have a mass of approximately 1 amu, similar to protons. Neutrons play a crucial role in determining the mass of an atom, as the sum of the number of protons and neutrons in the nucleus gives the atomic mass of an element.

### Electrons: The Negatively Charged Orbitals

Electrons are negatively charged subatomic particles that orbit the nucleus of an atom. They have a mass of approximately 1/1836 that of a proton and carry a charge of -1 elementary charge. Electrons occupy specific energy levels or orbitals around the nucleus, with each energy level having a specific capacity for electrons. The arrangement of electrons in an atom is known as the electron configuration.

### Key Concepts

* **Atomic Number (Z)**: The number of protons in an atom's nucleus, which determines the element of an atom.
* **Mass Number (A)**: The sum of the number of protons and neutrons in an atom's nucleus, which determines the atomic mass of an element.
* **Electron Configuration**: The arrangement of electrons in an atom's energy levels or orbitals.
